Polytheism, in contrast, is the belief in multiple gods or deities, each with distinct roles, powers, and domains—such as gods of the sky, war, fertility, or the sea.  Examples include ancient Greek, Roman, and Egyptian religions, as well as Hinduism, which features a vast pantheon of deities.  Polytheistic systems often include rich mythologies and allow for diverse forms of worship, with believers potentially focusing on one or more gods based on personal, cultural, or situational needs.
