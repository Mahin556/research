# The Battle of Tabuk — The Last Campaign of the Prophet ﷺ — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: Background — The Roman Threat

### The New Reality After Mecca's Conquest
- After the conquest of Mecca, **all of Arabia** recognized Muhammad ﷺ as Allah's Messenger
- People entered Islam in **massive numbers** from across the Arabian Peninsula
- The Islamic Empire was **spreading rapidly** across all of Arabia
- Inside Arabia — no significant threat remained

### The External Threat — Rome Is Watching
- Outside Arabia, the world's greatest superpower — the **Roman (Byzantine) Empire** — was watching the rapid unification of Arabia with deep concern
- Romans knew that **united Arabs** could pose a serious geopolitical challenge
- Rumors spread across the entire Arab world:
  - Some said Rome was mobilizing **40,000 soldiers**
  - Others said **100,000 soldiers**
  - The threat felt imminent — Romans could attack **at any time**

### The Prophet's ﷺ Unprecedented Decision
- In the **9th year after Hijra**, the Prophet ﷺ made a declaration unlike any before
- Previously, he **never revealed** the target of any military campaign before departure
- This time, he **publicly announced**:
  > *"We are going to fight the Roman Empire — the greatest superpower in the world. Every Muslim MUST participate. Anyone who stays behind without a valid excuse will be declared a Munafiq (hypocrite)."*

### Why This Was Different
- This was the **most transparent and difficult** campaign ever announced
- The enemy was the **world's greatest superpower** — fresh from defeating Persia
- The timing was the **hottest season** of the Arabian year
- A plague had recently struck parts of Arabia
- Everyone knew exactly how dangerous this would be — no room for illusions

---

## 💰 Part 2: The Great Fundraising — Who Gave What

### The Background
- The Prophet ﷺ had always distributed war spoils **generously** among the Muslims after every battle
- Now it was the Muslims' turn to fund this massive campaign themselves
- He asked every Muslim to contribute according to their ability

### The Famous Competition Between Umar ؓ and Abu Bakr ؓ

**Umar's ؓ Contribution:**
- Hazrat Umar ؓ always tried to **outdo Abu Bakr ؓ** in good deeds — and almost never succeeded
- This time he thought: *"This is my chance"*
- He went and donated **half of his entire wealth** — everything he owned

**Abu Bakr's ؓ Contribution:**
- Shortly after, Abu Bakr ؓ arrived
- He brought **everything** — his entire wealth AND every single item in his house
- The Prophet ﷺ asked: *"O Abu Bakr — what have you left for your family?"*
- Abu Bakr ؓ replied: *"I have left them Allah and His Messenger"*
- Once again — **Abu Bakr ؓ surpassed Umar ؓ**
- Umar ؓ later said: *"I realized that day I could never outdo Abu Bakr in anything"*

**Uthman's ؓ Extraordinary Donation:**
- Hazrat Uthman ؓ was the **wealthiest businessman in Medina's market**
- He donated:
  - **900 camels**
  - **100 horses**
  - **1,000 gold dinars**
- The Prophet ﷺ said about him: *"Nothing Uthman does after today can harm him"*

**Abdur Rahman bin Awf ؓ:**
- Also donated large numbers of camels, horses, and goods

**The Poorest Companion's Donation:**
- Not just the wealthy gave — even the **poorest companions** contributed
- One companion worked **all night** doing manual labor
- He earned only **a handful of dates**
- He brought those few dates — everything he had earned — and donated them to the campaign
- The hypocrites mocked this small donation
- Allah revealed a Quranic verse defending him:
  > *"Those who mock the believers who give charity voluntarily — Allah will throw their mockery back at them"*

---

## 🪖 Part 3: The Army — 30,000 Strong

### The Size
- Slowly, from across Arabia, volunteers joined
- The army eventually reached **30,000 soldiers**
- This was the **largest Muslim army ever assembled** in the Prophet's ﷺ lifetime
- It was also the **most logistically challenging** — managing 30,000 people in peak summer heat

### The Logistical Reality
- The destination was **Tabuk** — **700 kilometers** from Medina
- The journey would take at least **15 days** each way
- Resources were desperately short:
  - **Every 18 companions shared ONE camel** — taking turns riding
  - Food and water supplies were already strained at departure

### Running Out of Everything
- Within a few days on the road, the situation became critical:
  - Food supplies **ran out completely**
  - Water became scarce in the desert heat
  - The companions were forced to **slaughter the camels** they were sharing for food
  - This made the journey even harder — now they had to walk

### Abu Dhar al-Ghifari ؓ — The Man Who Walked Alone
- One companion, **Abu Dhar al-Ghifari ؓ**, was left behind when his camel collapsed and could not move
- Rather than stay behind (which would mean being declared a Munafiq), he did something remarkable:
  - He **abandoned his camel** and started **walking alone** in the desert heat
  - 700 km to Tabuk — on foot — in Arabia's hottest season
- When the Prophet ﷺ was informed that a man was walking alone far behind the army, he said:
  > *"Let him be — if there is any good in him, Allah will bring him to you"*
- Abu Dhar ؓ eventually **caught up with the army** — having walked through the desert alone
- The Prophet ﷺ said warmly: *"May Allah have mercy on Abu Dhar — he walks alone, dies alone, and will be raised alone"*

### Passing Through the Land of Thamud
- On the way, the army passed through the **ruins of the people of Thamud** — an ancient civilization mentioned in the Quran
- These were people who had carved **magnificent buildings into mountains** — and were destroyed by Allah for their disobedience
- Seeing these ruins **strengthened the faith** of the companions
- However, the Prophet ﷺ gave a specific order:
  > *"Do not use the water from this area"*
- He wanted the companions to feel **awe and lesson** from the place — not settle in it casually

## ⚔️ Part 4: Arriving at Tabuk — The Anticlimax That Became a Victory

### The Position
- After 15 days of brutal travel, the 30,000-strong Muslim army arrived at **Tabuk**
- Tabuk was located **inside the Ghassanid Kingdom** — a vassal state of Rome — right in the center of Roman-allied territory
- In other words: Muslims had **left their own territory** and marched deep into enemy lands
- They lined up in battle formation — **completely ready to fight**

### Nothing Happened
- They waited
- And waited
- **No Roman army appeared**

### The Historical Debate — Why Didn't Rome Come?
Two main opinions among scholars:

**Opinion 1 — Rome Was Afraid:**
- The Romans heard about the **30,000-strong Muslim army** marching toward them
- They knew Muslims had **never lost a battle** despite always being outnumbered — at Badr (1:3), Uhud (1:4), Khandaq (1:3), Mu'tah (1:67)
- A 30,000-strong Muslim army was something no one had faced before
- The Roman and Ghassanid forces simply **dispersed and fled** before any engagement

**Opinion 2 — The Threat Was Exaggerated:**
- Some scholars believe no actual Roman army was ever mobilized
- The original reports of 40,000-100,000 Romans were **just rumors**
- There was never a real invasion planned

**Either way — the outcome was the same**

## 🌍 Part 5: The Strategic Consequences — Massive Gains Without a Fight

### The Small Kingdoms Surrender
- All the **small kingdoms near Tabuk** that had previously been under Roman protection saw what happened
- They realized:
  - Rome — the world's greatest superpower — had not come to face the Muslims
  - The Muslims had marched **700 km into enemy territory** without hesitation
- One by one, these small kingdoms came to the Prophet ﷺ and:
  - **Surrendered voluntarily**
  - Agreed to pay **Jizyah** (a protection tax)
  - Became **vassal states** of the Islamic Empire instead of Rome

### The Historic Border Shift
- For the **first time ever**, the borders of the Islamic state now **directly touched** both the Roman AND Persian Empires
- Previously there were buffer states between them — now gone
- The entire world began viewing Muslims as a **global superpower**

### What the Muslims Gained Without Fighting
- Large numbers of **horses, camels, and gold** from the surrendering kingdoms
- **Geopolitical recognition** as a world-level power
- The **removal of Roman influence** from Arabia's borders
- The **exposure of the hypocrites** — those who refused to come on the campaign were now publicly known

---

## 😤 Part 6: The Hypocrites Are Exposed

### The Dual Purpose of Announcing the Target
- By announcing the target openly, the Prophet ﷺ created a **test for everyone**
- Those with genuine faith came — regardless of hardship
- Those who were hypocrites **made excuses and stayed behind**
- When the army returned, it was **clear to all of Medina** exactly who had come and who had not
- The hypocrites could no longer hide — they were publicly identified

### The Three Who Stayed Behind — A Different Story
- Three companions stayed behind — **not out of hypocrisy** but out of **laziness and procrastination**
- **Ka'b bin Malik ؓ** was one of them — he later gave the most honest account of what happened
- He kept putting off his preparation thinking *"I'll get ready tomorrow"* until the army had left
- When the Prophet ﷺ returned, these three **honestly admitted** they had no excuse
- The Prophet ﷺ ordered all Muslims to **boycott them completely** — no talking, no greeting, nothing
- For **50 days** — no one spoke to them
- Even their wives were separated from them
- After 50 days of this agonizing isolation, Allah revealed a verse **forgiving them**
- Their honest admission and suffering had purified them

---

## 🕌 Part 7: The Return to Medina

### The Welcome
- Despite the campaign being physically brutal with no actual battle, the return was celebrated
- Muslims had achieved **all strategic objectives without a single casualty**:
  - Demonstrated military reach into Roman territory
  - Gained vassal states and resources
  - Established direct borders with both superpowers
  - Exposed the hypocrites

### The Final Consequence — All of Arabia Accepts Islam
- After Tabuk, the **remaining Arab tribes** who hadn't yet accepted Islam came forward
- Seeing the complete unification of Arabia and Muslim dominance, they accepted Islam **voluntarily**
- All of Arabia abandoned idol worship and came to **Tawheed (monotheism)**
- The Islamic Empire now covered the **entire Arabian Peninsula**

---

## 💔 Part 8: The Significance — The Last Campaign

### The Mission Is Complete
- The Battle of Tabuk was the **last military campaign** of the Prophet ﷺ
- Shortly after his return, the Prophet ﷺ performed his **Last Hajj**
- During that Hajj, Allah revealed:
  > *"Today I have completed your religion for you, completed My favor upon you, and approved Islam as your religion"* — Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:3)
- The Prophet ﷺ knew his mission on Earth was complete
- **A few months later** — the Prophet ﷺ passed away

## 🗝️ Key Lessons from the Battle of Tabuk

| Lesson | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **True faith costs something** | 30,000 people walked 700 km in peak summer — sharing one camel per 18 people |
| **Giving changes lives** | Abu Bakr ؓ gave everything and left his family with "Allah and His Messenger" |
| **Small donations matter** | The man who donated a handful of dates was defended by Allah in the Quran |
| **Honest failure is better than false loyalty** | Ka'b bin Malik's honest admission ultimately led to forgiveness — hypocrisy leads to ruin |
| **Abu Dhar's lesson** | One man walked 700 km alone in desert heat rather than be called a hypocrite |
| **Strategic presence is victory** | Simply showing up with 30,000 men achieved more than any battle could have |
| **Missions complete when Allah wills** | The last campaign marked the completion of the Prophet's ﷺ earthly mission |
