# The Day of Ta'if & the Road to Hijra — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: The Two Pillars of Protection

### Early Islam's Most Difficult Period
- In the early years of Islam in Mecca, Muslims faced **severe persecution**
- Yet despite all the torture and oppression, the Prophet ﷺ kept giving his companions a remarkable promise:
  > *"Very soon, all of Arabia will be under Muslim rule — and Muslims will govern the Roman and Persian Empires"*
- The Meccans heard this and **mocked him even more intensely** and increased their persecution

### Why the Prophet ﷺ Was Safe — Two Shields
Despite all the hatred, two people provided the Prophet ﷺ with critical protection:

| Protector | Relationship | Role |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| **Abu Talib** | Prophet's uncle | Most powerful tribal chief — nobody dared touch the Prophet ﷺ while he lived |
| **Khadijah ؓ** | Prophet's wife | Emotional, financial, and spiritual support — the first Muslim |

---

## 🚫 Part 2: The Great Boycott — Three Years of Starvation

### Mecca's Extreme Measure
- When persecution failed to stop Islam's spread, the Meccans imposed a **total boycott** on the entire **Banu Hashim** clan
- The terms of the boycott:
  - **Nobody** would buy from or sell to Banu Hashim
  - **Nobody** would marry into or accept marriage from them
  - **No social contact** of any kind

### Important Detail — Who Was Boycotted
- The boycott was **not just against Muslims** — it was against the **entire Banu Hashim clan**
- This included **Muslims AND non-Muslims** from that family
- The only exception: **Abu Lahab** — the Prophet's ﷺ own uncle who joined the Meccans against his own family

### The Suffering
- Banu Hashim was forced to live in a valley called **Shi'b Abi Talib** on the outskirts of Mecca
- Mecca is **pure desert** — all food was imported from outside
- The Meccans would **buy up all food supplies** the moment any caravan arrived — leaving nothing for Banu Hashim
- For **3 years**, the Prophet ﷺ and his entire clan survived on:
  - **Leaves from trees**
  - **Leather and animal hides** — boiled and eaten
- Children cried from hunger — their cries could be heard outside the valley

### End of the Boycott
- Eventually, even some non-Muslim Meccans could not bear to watch this cruelty
- The boycott was **lifted** after 3 years
- But the damage was done — the starvation had taken its toll

---

## 💔 Part 3: The Year of Grief — Two Deaths That Changed Everything

### Abu Talib Dies
- Shortly after the boycott ended, **Abu Talib** — the Prophet's greatest protector — passed away
- Without Abu Talib's tribal protection, the Prophet ﷺ became **completely vulnerable** in Mecca
- Anyone could now harm him without fear of tribal retaliation

### Khadijah ؓ Dies
- In the **same year**, the Prophet's ﷺ beloved wife **Khadijah ؓ** also passed away
- She had been his emotional anchor, his first believer, his financial supporter
- The Prophet ﷺ called this year **"Aam ul-Huzn" — The Year of Grief**

### The Final Blow — Citizenship Revoked
- After Abu Talib's death, the new head of Banu Hashim was **Abu Lahab**
- Abu Lahab was the Prophet's ﷺ uncle — yet his **fiercest enemy**
- One of his first acts as clan chief: he **revoked the Prophet's ﷺ citizenship** of Mecca
- The Prophet ﷺ was now:
  - Without his two greatest supporters
  - Without tribal protection
  - Without even the right to live in Mecca
  - Completely alone and vulnerable

---

## 🚶 Part 4: The Journey to Ta'if — Walking into the Worst Day

### Why Ta'if?
- Allah's command was clear — Islam must be spread everywhere
- With Mecca hostile and his protection gone, the Prophet ﷺ looked outward
- **Ta'if** was the second most important city in Arabia — wealthy, powerful, with its own influential tribal leadership
- If Ta'if accepted Islam, it would be a game-changer

### The 100 km Walk
- The Prophet ﷺ and **Zaid bin Haritha ؓ** walked approximately **100 kilometers on foot** to Ta'if
- No horse, no camel for much of the journey — just walking through the Arabian desert

### The Rejection
- The Prophet ﷺ met with **all the senior leaders** of Ta'if one by one
- He presented Islam to each of them
- Every single one **refused** — not politely, but with contempt
- Their final message:
  > *"Never come back to Ta'if again"*
- After **10 days** in Ta'if with zero progress, the Prophet ﷺ began the long walk home

---

## 🩸 Part 5: The Most Painful Day — Stones of Ta'if

### The Attack
- As the Prophet ﷺ and Zaid ؓ began leaving Ta'if, the people of the city began **mocking and jeering**
- Then the children of Ta'if were **incited to throw stones** at him
- What followed was one of the most heartbreaking scenes in Islamic history:
  - **Children lined both sides of the road**
  - They threw stones relentlessly as the Prophet ﷺ walked
  - Zaid ؓ threw himself in front of the Prophet ﷺ trying to shield him — and was severely injured
  - The stones hit the Prophet's ﷺ blessed feet so many times that his **sandals filled with blood**
  - He was bleeding so heavily that his feet stuck to the ground with dried blood

### Taking Shelter in a Garden
- Eventually, the crowd stopped following when the Prophet ﷺ entered a **garden/orchard**
- The garden belonged to **Utbah and Shaybah** — two of the Prophet's ﷺ biggest enemies in Mecca
- Even they were **moved to pity** seeing his blood-soaked condition
- They sent their **Christian slave Addas** with a bunch of grapes

---

## 🙏 Part 6: The Du'a Under the Tree — The Most Moving Prayer

### The Prophet ﷺ Sits Alone
- The Prophet ﷺ sat under a tree — exhausted, bleeding, rejected, and alone
- He raised his hands and made a prayer that Muslims have treasured for 1,400 years:

> *"O Allah — to You alone I complain of my weakness, my lack of means, and my being insignificant to people.*
> *O Most Merciful of the merciful — You are the Lord of the oppressed, and You are my Lord.*
> *To whom do You entrust me? To a distant stranger who will mistreat me? Or to an enemy whom You have given power over me?*
> *If You are not angry with me, I do not care about any of this.*
> *But Your pardon is more comfortable for me.*
> *I seek refuge in the light of Your face — by which all darkness is illuminated — from Your anger descending upon me, or Your displeasure coming upon me.*
> *To You belongs the right to reproach until You are pleased. There is no power and no might except through You."*

### Why Allah Did NOT Stop Him from Going to Ta'if
- Allah knows past, present, and future — He could have stopped the Prophet ﷺ before the journey
- But Allah **allowed this to happen** deliberately:
  > *"So that for thousands of years to come, all of humanity could witness the Prophet's ﷺ patience, dedication, and unshakeable trust in Allah"*
- The Day of Ta'if stands as the **single most powerful testament** to the Prophet's ﷺ perseverance

---

## 🌟 Part 7: Light in the Darkest Moment

### The Slave Who Became Muslim
- When Addas the slave came with grapes, the Prophet ﷺ — despite everything he had just endured — **set aside his own pain**
- He engaged Addas in conversation and **invited him to Islam**
- He asked: *"Where are you from?"*
- Addas replied: *"I am from Nineveh"*
- The Prophet ﷺ said:
  > *"Nineveh — the city of my brother Prophet Yunus ؑ (Jonah). He was a Prophet, and I am a Prophet"*
- This connection moved Addas deeply
- He **kissed the Prophet's ﷺ hands and feet and accepted Islam** right there
- From across the garden, Utbah and Shaybah watched and said:
  > *"There goes our slave too..."*

### The Angel of Mountains
- As the Prophet ﷺ departed, **Jibreel ؑ (Gabriel)** appeared — accompanied by the **Angel of the Mountains**
- The Angel of Mountains said:
  > *"O Prophet of Allah — give me the command, and I will crush these people between the two mountains of Ta'if"*
- The Prophet ﷺ refused:
  > *"No. Perhaps from their descendants, someone will be a Muslim"*
- He **forgave the people of Ta'if completely** — the same people who had just filled his sandals with blood

### The Jinn Accept Islam
- On the way back from Ta'if, the Prophet ﷺ stopped to pray at night
- A **group of Jinn** (unseen beings) passed by, heard the Quran being recited, and stopped
- They listened — and **accepted Islam**
- The Quran records this in **Surah Al-Ahqaf (Chapter 46)** and **Surah Al-Jinn (Chapter 72)**
- Even when humans rejected him, the Jinn were accepting him

---

## 🏠 Part 8: Returning to Mecca — Without Citizenship

### The Need for Protection
- As the Prophet ﷺ approached Mecca, he faced a stark reality:
- He was **no longer a citizen of Mecca** — Abu Lahab had revoked that
- Without tribal protection, entering Mecca meant he could be killed
- In that era, entering a city required either citizenship or **someone's formal protection (aman)**
- He asked several people for protection — **two refused**
- Finally, **Mut'im bin Adi** — a respected non-Muslim Meccan — granted him protection
- The Prophet ﷺ entered Mecca under Mut'im's guarantee of safety

---

## 🌱 Part 9: The Seeds of Hijra — Medina Begins to Open

### Continuing Despite Everything
- Even after Ta'if, the Prophet ﷺ **never stopped his mission**
- Every year during Hajj season, thousands came to Mecca from across Arabia
- The Prophet ﷺ would go **secretly at night** to the pilgrims' camps and invite people to Islam
- Abu Lahab would literally **follow him from tent to tent** telling people: *"Don't listen to him!"*

### The Six Men from Medina — The Turning Point
- One year after Ta'if, the Prophet ﷺ met **six men from Medina** at a pilgrims' camp
- He invited them to Islam
- They looked at each other and said:
  > *"Could this be the Prophet that the Jews have been telling us about for years?"*

### A 1,000-Year Divine Setup
- This is extraordinary: For **hundreds of years**, the Jewish tribes of Medina had been telling the people of Medina:
  > *"A Prophet is coming soon — when he comes, we will follow him and destroy you with his help"*
- Allah had been **preparing the hearts and minds of Medina's people** for over a thousand years through Jewish prophecy
- So when these six men heard the Prophet ﷺ — they immediately recognized the description they had heard all their lives
- **All six accepted Islam on the spot**

### The Snowball Begins
- These six returned to Medina and began spreading Islam
- The **next year, 12 people** came from Medina and accepted Islam
- Year after year, the numbers grew
- Slowly, Islam reached **every household in Medina**

---

## ✨ Part 10: Allah's Gift After the Darkest Night — Isra wal Miraj

### The Divine Comfort
- After all these trials — boycott, loss of loved ones, Ta'if, loss of citizenship — Allah gave the Prophet ﷺ the greatest gift:
- **The Night Journey (Isra) and Ascension through the Seven Heavens (Miraj)**
- Allah took him on a journey through the heavens — the greatest honor ever given to any human being
- This was Allah's way of saying:
  > *"The world may have rejected you — but We honor you above all of creation"*

## 🗝️ Key Lessons from the Day of Ta'if

| Lesson | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Patience has no limit** | After boycott, loss, homelessness, and bleeding feet — he still forgave |
| **Mercy over revenge** | When offered the chance to destroy Ta'if — he refused for the sake of future generations |
| **Purpose survives pain** | Even with blood-filled sandals, he invited Addas to Islam |
| **Allah's timing is perfect** | 1,000 years of Jewish prophecy prepared Medina's hearts — all for this moment |
| **Darkness before dawn** | Ta'if was followed by Miraj — the highest honor in human history |
| **Character is tested in suffering** | The Prophet ﷺ was never more majestic than when he was most broken |

> 📖 *The Prophet ﷺ himself said that the Day of Ta'if was the hardest day of his life — harder even than the Battle of Uhud. Yet from that day of total rejection came the opening of Medina — the city that would become the capital of Islam and change the world forever.*
