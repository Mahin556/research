# The Signs of Qiyamah — The Hadith of Jibreel & the Bedouin Prophecy — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: The Famous Visitor — Jibreel ؑ in Human Form

### The Setting
- This event happened **shortly before the Prophet's ﷺ death** — when almost all of the Quran had already been revealed
- The Prophet ﷺ was sitting in **Masjid an-Nabawi** with Hazrat Umar ؓ and other companions
- Suddenly — a **completely unknown man** walked into the mosque

### The Description of This Stranger
Everything about him was unusual:

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Clothes** | Extremely white — brighter than normal |
| **Hair** | Jet black — no signs of aging |
| **Appearance** | Extraordinarily handsome and dignified |
| **Travel signs** | None — no dust, no tiredness, no signs of a long journey |
| **Familiarity** | Nobody in Medina had ever seen him before |

- In that era, if a stranger arrived in Medina — people would know. Medina was not that large.
- This man showed **no signs of travel** yet was completely unknown
- The companions stared at him curiously

### What He Did
- He walked straight to the Prophet ﷺ
- Sat down **right in front of him** — knees touching knees
- Placed his hands on the Prophet's ﷺ thighs
- And said directly:
  > *"O Muhammad — tell me about Islam"*

---

## 📖 Part 2: The Three Questions — The Foundation of Islamic Knowledge

### Question 1 — What Is Islam?

The Prophet ﷺ answered — **the Five Pillars**:

| Pillar | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Shahada** | Testifying there is no god except Allah and Muhammad ﷺ is His Messenger |
| **Salah** | Performing the five daily prayers |
| **Zakat** | Paying the obligatory charity |
| **Sawm** | Fasting in the month of Ramadan |
| **Hajj** | Performing pilgrimage to Mecca (for those who are able) |

The stranger said: *"You have spoken the truth"*
The companions were amazed — he was asking questions **and then confirming the answers** as correct. Who does that?

---

### Question 2 — What Is Iman (Faith)?

The Prophet ﷺ answered — **the Six Articles of Faith**:

| Article | What It Means |
|---------|--------------|
| **Allah** | Belief in Allah — one God, no partners |
| **Angels** | Belief in the existence of angels |
| **Revealed Books** | Belief in the scriptures — Torah, Psalms, Gospel, Quran |
| **Prophets** | Belief in all the prophets sent by Allah |
| **Day of Judgment** | Belief in the afterlife and accountability |
| **Qadar** | Belief in divine decree — both good and bad come from Allah's knowledge |

The stranger again said: *"You have spoken the truth"*

---

### Question 3 — What Is Ihsan (Excellence/Perfection)?

The Prophet ﷺ answered:
> *"Ihsan is to worship Allah as if you can see Him — and even if you cannot see Him, know that He sees you"*

The stranger confirmed this too.

---

### Question 4 — When Is the Day of Judgment?

The stranger asked: *"When will Qiyamah (the Day of Judgment) come?"*

The Prophet ﷺ gave a remarkable answer:
> *"The one being asked does not know any more than the one asking — meaning neither you nor I know. Only Allah knows."*

- This was a **profound teaching** — even the greatest Prophet and the greatest Angel do not know when Qiyamah will come
- Only **Allah alone** knows

### Question 5 — Tell Me Its Signs

Since the exact time was unknown, the stranger asked:
> *"Then tell me about its signs — how will we know it is approaching?"*

The Prophet ﷺ gave **two major signs** — and these are the focus of this entire lesson.

---

### The Stranger Leaves
- After receiving the answers, the stranger got up and left
- The Prophet ﷺ sent companions to find him — **nobody could locate him**
- Then the Prophet ﷺ revealed:
  > *"Do you know who that was? That was Jibreel ؑ (Gabriel). He came to teach you your religion."*

### Why This Hadith Is Called "Hadith Jibreel"
- This is one of the **most famous hadith** in all of Islam
- It contains the **complete framework of Islamic belief and practice** in one conversation
- Jibreel ؑ came in human form specifically so this teaching would be **witnessed, heard, and preserved** by many companions at once

## 🔮 Part 3: The Two Signs of Qiyamah

### Sign 1 — Children Ruling Over Parents

> *"When a slave woman gives birth to her master"*

**Scholarly Interpretation:**
- Most scholars understand this as:
  > *"When children treat their parents like servants — ordering them around, disrespecting them, making them feel like slaves"*
- Parents once commanded respect and obedience
- A sign of approaching Qiyamah: this relationship **reverses**
- Children become the authority; parents become subservient

---

### Sign 2 — The Barefoot Desert Shepherds Competing in Tall Buildings

> *"When you see the barefoot, naked, poor shepherds competing with each other in building tall structures"*

This is the **central prophecy** of this entire video — and it is happening right now.

---

## 🏜️ Part 4: Who Were the Bedouins?

### The Context — Why This Prophecy Was Shocking

**Who were the Bedouins (Badu/Bedouin)?**
- Nomadic Arab tribes who **wandered the desert** with their animals
- They never settled in one place
- They were the **poorest class** in all of Arabia — not just in the Prophet's ﷺ time but for **1,000 years after** his death
- They were sometimes called "Badu" — a somewhat dismissive term used by settled Arabs
- They had **no cities, no buildings, no wealth**
- Just tents, camels, and desert

**Why the companions were confused:**
- The Prophet ﷺ described these people — the absolute bottom of Arabian society — competing in building **tall structures**
- To the companions, this was **inconceivable**
- For the next **1,000+ years** after this hadith, the Bedouins remained nomadic and poor — the prophecy showed no signs of coming true

---

## 🌍 Part 5: 1,400 Years of History — How the Prophecy Set Up

### The Islamic Caliphate Spreads
- After the Prophet ﷺ, the Islamic Empire expanded rapidly
- It became the **largest empire in human history** at its peak — larger than Alexander's empire and Rome
- The Bedouins spread with it across the world
- But their **culture did not change** — they remained nomadic for centuries

### The Ottoman Era — The First Attempt at Change
- In the final years of the **Ottoman (Uthmaniya) Caliphate**, the world was transitioning from traditional methods to the **Industrial Age**
- Sultan Abdul Hamid II tried to **modernize the empire** — including building a famous **railway from Istanbul to Medina**
- But the Bedouins were a constant obstacle:
  - In **1757**, Bedouins had **massacred 200 Hajj pilgrims**
  - They attacked and disrupted construction and trade repeatedly
  - The Ottomans tried to **settle them permanently** in fixed locations
  - The Bedouins **refused** — they valued their nomadic freedom

### The First Saudi Kingdom
- The first Saudi king, **Ibn Saud**, united the Bedouin tribes
- With **British assistance**, he conquered the Arabian Peninsula
- This became modern **Saudi Arabia**
- The Bedouins who had been the most powerless people in Arabia were now the **rulers of Arabia**

---

## 🛢️ Part 6: Oil — The Game Changer

### The Discovery That Changed Everything
- A few years after the Saudi kingdom was established — **oil was discovered** beneath the Arabian desert
- The same barefoot shepherds who had **nothing** suddenly had access to **unimaginable wealth**
- Bedouin tribal families went from poverty to becoming **millionaires and billionaires** within a generation

### The Warning They Received
- But the new Arab rulers were warned:
  > *"This oil will not last forever — it will run out. When it does, all your luxury will disappear with it."*
- This created urgency: **How do we build a future after oil?**

---

## 🏗️ Part 7: The Competition Begins — Fulfilling the 1,400-Year-Old Prophecy

### Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid — Dubai's Transformation
- The ruler of Dubai, **Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum**, comes from a **Bedouin tribal background**
- He has publicly stated in interviews that he is **proud of his Bedouin heritage**
- He recognized that Dubai's oil would run out — and made a **visionary decision**:
  - Transform Dubai from an oil economy to a **tourism and business hub**
  - Build world-class infrastructure

### The Burj Khalifa
- The result: **Burj Khalifa** — the **world's tallest building** — was built in Dubai
- Before Burj Khalifa, the tallest structures in history were:

| Building | Location | Held Record For |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Great Pyramid of Giza** | Egypt | **3,800 years** |
| Various European cathedrals | Europe | Several centuries |
| Eiffel Tower | Paris | Decades |
| World Trade Center | New York | Until 9/11 (destroyed) |
| **Burj Khalifa** | Dubai | Current record holder |

> *The Pyramid of Giza was the world's tallest structure for **3,800 years** — no one could surpass it. Then in the modern era, the record changed rapidly.*

### Saudi Arabia Responds
- Seeing Dubai's success, **Saudi Arabia** began copying the model
- They built the **Abraj Al-Bait Clock Tower** (Mecca Royal Hotel) directly across from the Kaaba — currently the **fourth tallest building in the world**
- Then Saudi announced: **Kingdom Tower (Jeddah Tower)** — which will surpass Burj Khalifa

### The Race Nobody Will Disclose
- A fierce **secret competition** has now broken out among Arab nations
- Countries deliberately **refuse to reveal** the planned height of their next building projects
- Why? Because if they announce it, another Arab country will immediately announce an **even taller one**
- Every few months, a new mega-tower project is announced somewhere in the Arab world

### The Common Thread
- Behind **virtually all of these projects** — Dubai, Saudi, Qatar, UAE, Kuwait — are people whose families trace back to **Bedouin tribal origins**
- The same people the Prophet ﷺ described as *"barefoot, naked, poor shepherds"*

## 🌟 Part 8: The Prophecy Fulfilled Before Our Eyes

### Connecting It All

The Prophet ﷺ said to Jibreel ؑ, in front of the companions, **1,400 years ago**:

> *"A sign of Qiyamah is when you see the barefoot, naked, poor shepherds competing with each other in building tall structures"*

**What we see today:**
- Descendants of nomadic Bedouin shepherds
- Who were the **poorest people in Arabia for over 1,000 years**
- Now have the **world's tallest buildings**
- And are in an **open competition** to keep building taller and taller
- **Not hiding it** — openly racing each other

### What the Companions Thought
- The companions **could not imagine** this happening
- For them, these were the poorest people in their society
- For **1,000 years** after the Prophet ﷺ, nothing happened to suggest this prophecy would come true
- Then — in the span of **one century** — it all unfolded exactly as described

### Jibreel's ؑ Purpose
The narrator makes a beautiful point:
> *"Jibreel ؑ didn't just come to teach the companions. He came to teach US — the Muslims of today. Because the signs he asked about were not for the companions to see in their lifetime. They were for us to recognize in ours."*
