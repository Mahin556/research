title: Why do we recite loudly in Fajr, Maghrib, and Isha (first two rak'as), but silently in Dhuhr and Asr?
category: Islam

---

**The Answer — Islam is built on Submission (Islam literally means submission):**

At the end of Surah Al-Baqarah: When Allah said He would hold people accountable even for thoughts in their minds, the companions were distressed — they said they could handle jihad, fasting, prayer, and zakat, but being judged for their thoughts felt impossible. The Prophet ﷺ reminded them not to say "we hear and we disobey" like those before them, but rather "we hear and we obey." Allah then revealed mercy — He does not burden a soul beyond its capacity.

**The core lesson:** The Prophet ﷺ said **"Pray as you have seen me pray."** He recited loudly in Fajr, and in the first two rak'as of Maghrib and Isha, and silently in Dhuhr, Asr, and the remaining rak'as. He never explained *why* — and crucially, **none of the companions ever asked why either.** They simply followed.

The sheikh's conclusion is powerful: religion is a **package deal**. We don't get to question why Fajr is 2 rak'as, Maghrib is 3, or why there is one ruku and two sajdas in each rak'a. This is how it was revealed and transmitted to us, and our role is to comply — because that is the very meaning of Islam.
