# The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah & Dawah to the World — Detailed Notes

## Part 1: The Dream — Journey Toward Mecca

### The Prophet's ﷺ Vision
- After the victory at Khandaq, the Prophet ﷺ had a **dream one night**
- He saw himself performing **Tawaf (circumambulation) of the Kaaba** with his companions
- To fulfill this dream, he set out toward Mecca with **1,000 companions**
- Their intention was purely **Umrah (minor pilgrimage)** — absolutely no fighting

### Mecca Blocks the Way
- The Meccans heard about the approaching Muslims and **blocked their path**
- They refused to allow entry into Mecca under any circumstances

---

## Part 2: Negotiations Begin

### The Meccan Ambassador's Remarkable Report
- The Meccans sent an ambassador to assess the situation
- When he returned, he gave the **Quraysh leaders a stunning report**:

> *"I have seen the Emperor of Rome (Caesar). I have seen the Emperor of Iran (Kisra). I have seen the King of Yemen. But I have **never in my life seen a king like Muhammad ﷺ**. When he performs Wudu (ablution), his companions do not let even a single drop of water touch the ground. Such a man with such followers — **fighting them is impossible**. I am telling you — accept their terms."*

- Of course, the arrogant Meccan leaders **ignored this advice**

### Why Hazrat Umar ؓ Couldn't Be Sent as Negotiator
- The Prophet ﷺ first considered sending **Hazrat Umar ؓ** as a negotiator to Mecca
- But Hazrat Umar ؓ himself said:
  > *"I don't have any powerful relative in Mecca who can protect me"*
- Hazrat Umar ؓ belonged to **Banu Adi** — a clan that was not particularly powerful among the Quraysh

### Why Hazrat Uthman ؓ Was the Perfect Choice
- Hazrat Umar ؓ suggested sending **Hazrat Uthman ؓ** instead
- Hazrat Uthman ؓ belonged to **Banu Umayya** — the **richest and most powerful family** in all of Quraysh
- This was the same family that would later:
  - Rule over Muslims for almost **100 years**
  - Establish the **Umayyad Empire** — the largest centralized Muslim empire in history
  - Produce the most powerful Muslim ruler who governed the **largest landmass** in Islamic history

---

## Part 3: The Crisis — Rumor of Uthman's ؓ Death

### The Dangerous Delay
- Hazrat Uthman ؓ went to Mecca to negotiate but was **detained for many days**
- His prolonged absence caused a **devastating rumor to spread** among the Muslims:
  > *"Hazrat Uthman ؓ has been martyred!"*

### Why This Was Extremely Serious
- In the customs of that time, **killing an ambassador was equivalent to a declaration of war**
- This rumor meant Mecca had declared all-out war

### Bayat-e-Rizwan — The Oath Under the Tree
- All companions gathered urgently
- Under a tree called **Rizwan**, they placed their hands in the Prophet's ﷺ hand one by one
- They took a **solemn oath**:
  > *"We will all die — but we will avenge the blood of Uthman ؓ"*
- This was one of the most **emotionally charged moments** in early Islamic history
- The Quran later praised the companions who took this oath

### The Relief
- It turned out to be a **complete misunderstanding**
- Hazrat Uthman ؓ returned **completely safe and unharmed**
- But this event sent a **powerful signal to the Meccans**:
  > *"These people are NOT going to back down — ever"*

---

## Part 4: The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah — Terms That Seemed Unfair

### The Agreement Was Finalized
Despite enormous tension and confusion, a peace treaty was signed — known as the **Treaty of Hudaybiyyah**

### The Three Main Terms:

| Term | What It Said |
|------|-------------|
| **Term 1** | Muslims will NOT perform Umrah this year — they can come **next year** instead |
| **Term 2** | **No war** between Mecca and Medina for **10 years** |
| **Term 3** | If any man leaves **Mecca for Medina** (accepting Islam), he will be **returned to Mecca**. But if anyone leaves Medina for Mecca (leaving Islam), he will NOT be returned |

### The Companions' Outrage
- **Not a single companion was happy** with this treaty
- It seemed completely one-sided and humiliating:
  - They couldn't even perform Umrah this year
  - They had to return new Muslims who came to them
  - But apostates who left Islam were not returned
- Even **Hazrat Umar ؓ** — one of the bravest companions — openly expressed his discomfort
- But the Prophet ﷺ **overruled everyone's opinions** and signed the treaty

### The Moment That Tested the Agreement
- As the treaty was being signed, the Meccan representative's **own son** arrived — having fled Mecca and accepted Islam
- The son **begged with tears**: *"Please don't send me back to those oppressors!"*
- The Prophet ﷺ was **visibly saddened** but **sent him back** — honoring the treaty
- Many more people came afterward — all were **returned to Mecca** per the agreement

---

## Part 5: The Loophole — Abu Basir and the Unintended Consequence

### The Clever Legal Gap
- The Meccans had written the treaty carelessly
- The exact wording said: **"If any MAN goes from Mecca to Medina..."**
- The word used specifically meant **man (male)** — women were not mentioned
- When a **woman** later fled Mecca and came to Medina, she was **not returned**
- Because the treaty technically only covered men!
- This became the **standard going forward**:
  - Man comes from Mecca to Medina → returned
  - Woman comes from Mecca to Medina → stays

### Abu Basir — The Man Who Changed Everything
- A man named **Abu Basir** came from Mecca to Medina accepting Islam
- Per the treaty, the Prophet ﷺ **sent him back to Mecca**
- But Abu Basir managed to **escape from his Meccan handlers**
- He didn't go to Medina (which would violate the treaty) and didn't stay in Mecca
- He settled in **the desert between Mecca and Medina** — in a legal gray zone

### An Entire Town Is Born
- More people kept coming from Mecca — being sent back by the Prophet ﷺ — but then **escaping to join Abu Basir**
- Slowly, **an entire settlement grew** in the desert
- Their location was **perfectly strategic** — right next to the main **trade route between Mecca and Syria (Sham)**
- This group began **raiding Meccan trade caravans** — causing **billions in losses** to Mecca's trade

### Mecca Begs to Cancel Their Own Term
- The Meccans had originally **insisted on Term 3 with great pride and confidence**
- Now, because of Abu Basir's group, this very term was **destroying them economically**
- In an extraordinary reversal, Mecca **came to the Prophet ﷺ and begged**:
  > *"Please cancel Term 3 — from now on, if anyone comes from Mecca to Medina accepting Islam, please keep them in Medina and treat them well!"*
- The term that Mecca had considered their **greatest victory** had become a **noose around their own neck**

## Part 6: Dawah to the World's Superpowers

### Using the 10 Years of Peace Wisely
- With **no war with Mecca for 10 years**, the Prophet ﷺ could now focus outward
- He began sending **letters of invitation to Islam** to the rulers of the world's greatest empires
- This was an act of extraordinary **boldness and vision**

### The Three Famous Letters

To understand how audacious this was:
> *It was like a small village in rural Pakistan today writing letters to both Donald Trump and Xi Jinping — simultaneously*

**Letter 1 → King of Abyssinia (Negus/Najashi)**
- He was already **Christian**
- He knew from Christian scripture that a **Prophet was coming soon**
- He **accepted Islam** upon receiving the letter

**Letter 2 → Roman Emperor Heraclius (Herqal)**
- He was also **Christian**
- He summoned some Arabs who were in the Roman Empire at the time
- **Abu Sufyan** happened to be in Roman territory — he was brought before the Emperor
- Abu Sufyan was asked about the Prophet's ﷺ character and had to answer honestly
- The Emperor was **deeply impressed** by what he heard
- He said:
  > *"I knew a Prophet was coming soon — but that he would come from among you Arabs? That I never imagined."*
- After the meeting, **Abu Sufyan told his companions**:
  > *"Now even the Roman Emperor is afraid of Muhammad — things are becoming very serious"*
- However, despite his personal belief, Heraclius **did not officially accept Islam** — fearing the political consequences

**Letter 3 → Persian Emperor Khusrow (Kisra)**
- His reaction was the complete opposite — **arrogant and dismissive**
- He said:
  > *"You people from that desert are going to teach ME how to live?"*
- He **tore the letter apart and threw it away**
- He sent **two men to Medina** with orders to bring the Prophet ﷺ to him (or kill him)

### The Prophecy About Khusrow
- When the two Persian messengers arrived in Medina, the Prophet ﷺ called them and said calmly:
  > *"The king who gave you this order — his own son has killed him"*
- They were **completely stunned** — how could he possibly know this?
- They returned to Persia and confirmed — **Khusrow had indeed been killed by his own son** while they were traveling
- This was seen as yet another **prophetic miracle**
- The Prophet ﷺ also said about Khusrow tearing the letter:
  > *"Just as he tore my letter — his kingdom will be torn apart"*
- Within decades, the **entire Persian Empire was conquered** by the Muslims

## Summary — What Hudaybiyyah Really Achieved

### Why the Prophet ﷺ Was Right to Sign It

| Appeared to be | Actually was |
|----------------|-------------|
| Humiliating for Muslims | A massive strategic victory |
| Unfair terms | Terms that backfired on Mecca |
| Blocking Umrah | Buying 10 years of peace to spread Islam worldwide |
| Forcing return of Muslims | Led to Abu Basir's group destroying Meccan trade |
| Muslim weakness | Recognized by Roman Emperor as Muslim strength |

The Quran described this treaty as:
> **"A clear victory" (Fatah-e-Mubeen)** — Surah Al-Fath (Chapter 48)

The companions couldn't understand it at the time — but history proved the Prophet ﷺ was absolutely right.

## Part 7: What Comes Next — The Conquest of Khaybar

- With Mecca at peace for 10 years and dawah spreading worldwide
- The Prophet ﷺ now turned the Muslim army's full attention toward **Khaybar**
- Khaybar was a powerful Jewish fortress settlement north of Medina
- The next great chapter was about to begin...

## Key Lessons from Hudaybiyyah

| Lesson | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Long-term vision beats short-term emotion** | Every companion was upset — but the Prophet ﷺ saw what they couldn't see |
| **Patience is strategic** | 10 years of peace = time to spread Islam across the entire known world |
| **Treaties can be turned** | What Mecca thought was their advantage destroyed their economy |
| **Character is the greatest weapon** | Even enemies like Heraclius were won over by hearing about the Prophet's ﷺ character |
| **Prophecy and reality** | The Prophet's ﷺ knowledge of Khusrow's death stunned the world |
