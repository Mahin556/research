## Shirk meanings
  * Treating anyone or anything like Allah
  * Worshipping someone else besides Allah.
  * Believing someone shares Allah’s power, knowledge, or authority.
  * Praying to idols or dead people.
  * Believing someone controls your fate besides Allah
  * Saying someone is all-powerful like Allah
  * Believing someone unseen (dead person, saint, angel) can solve your problems independently → this is shirk

---

* Shirk is considered the biggest sin in Islam

---

## Type of shirks

### Major Shirk (Shirk al-Akbar)

This takes a person **outside of Islam**.

#### Shirk in Worship (Ibadah)

* Worshipping someone other than Allah
* Examples:
  * Asking dead people for help
  * Praying (salah) to someone besides Allah --> saints or idols
  * Making dua to saints, graves, or dead people
  * Bowing (sajdah) to idols, people, or objects
  * Sacrificing animals in the name of someone other than Allah
  * Vowing (mannat) for someone other than Allah

#### Shirk in Lordship (Rububiyyah)

* Believing someone shares Allah’s power
* Examples:
  * Believing someone controls life, death, or رزق (rizq) besides Allah
  * Thinking someone can independently change destiny
  * Believing a person has power equal to Allah

#### Shirk in Names & Attributes (Asma wa Sifat)

* Giving Allah’s unique qualities to others
* Examples:
  * Saying someone is all-knowing (omniscient) like Allah
  * Giving divine qualities to humans or objects
  * Believing someone hears everything everywhere like Allah


---

### Minor Shirk (Shirk al-Asghar)

Does **not remove from Islam**, but still a serious sin.

#### Showing off (Riya)
* Doing good deeds to impress people instead of Allah
* Example:
  * Praying just to be seen
  * Giving charity to look generous

#### Swearing by other than Allah
* Saying things like:
  * “I swear by my life”
  * “I swear by my parents”

#### Depending on means more than Allah
* Trusting something while forgetting Allah is the real source

---

### Hidden Shirk (Shirk al-Khafi)
Very subtle and inside the heart.

#### Intentions not purely for Allah
* Mixing sincerity with showing off

#### Loving something equal to Allah
* Loving someone/something **more than or equal to Allah**

* Fear or reliance on someone like you should only have for Allah

## All Shirks

* **Superstitions & Objects**
  * Wearing amulets/taweez thinking they protect on their own
  * Believing in lucky charms (rings, threads, stones)
  * Hanging things to ward off evil independently

* **Astrology & Fortune**
  * Believing stars or horoscopes control your life
  * Going to fortune tellers and believing them

* **Words people casually say**
  * “This happened because of luck only”
  * “My boss controls my رزق completely”
  * “This person saved me completely” (forgetting Allah)

* **Visiting shrines and graves for help**
  * Going to graves/shrines with the intention of seeking relief from problems
  * Calling upon dead saints or righteous people for help
  * Believing they can hear, respond, or solve your problems
  * Performing acts of worship (dua, سجدة, offerings) at graves

* **Wearing or using amulets (taweez, charms, threads)**
  * Wearing necklaces, rings, black threads, or lockets for protection
  * Hanging charms in cars, homes, or on children
  * Believing these objects protect from harm, evil eye, or misfortune independently

* **Making dua (supplication) to other than Allah**
  * Calling upon prophets, saints, or dead people for help
  * Saying phrases like “help me” to unseen beings
  * Asking for رزق (rizq), healing, success, or protection from anyone besides Allah

* **Believing in astrology, horoscopes, and fortune tellers**
  * Thinking stars or zodiac signs control personality or future
  * Visiting astrologers, palm readers, or soothsayers
  * Believing predictions about unseen matters or future events

* **Trusting saints or others for provision and outcomes**
  * Believing someone other than Allah provides wealth, children, or success
  * Thinking saints control life events or outcomes
  * Depending on them instead of relying on Allah

* **Making vows (nazar) to other than Allah**
  * Promising offerings, food, or actions in the name of saints
  * Saying “If I get this, I will give this at the shrine”
  * Sacrificing animals or giving charity for someone other than Allah

* **Using someone as a middleman in dua**
  * Saying “O Allah, accept my dua through this saint or prophet”
  * Believing Allah accepts prayers only because of someone’s status
  * Thinking a dead person has influence over Allah

* **Exaggerating praise of Prophet Muhammad**
  * Attributing divine qualities to him
  * Believing he knows everything (unseen knowledge)
  * Thinking he controls the universe or hears all calls everywhere

* **Swearing by other than Allah**
  * Saying “I swear by my mother/father/life”
  * Taking oaths in the name of people or things instead of Allah

* **Believing someone knows the unseen (ghayb)**
  * Thinking prophets, saints, or astrologers know the future
  * Believing someone knows what is hidden in hearts
  * Accepting claims of unseen knowledge besides Allah

* **Showing off in worship (Riya)**
  * Praying, fasting, or giving charity to impress people
  * Wanting praise or recognition for religious acts
  * Performing عبادت (worship) for status instead of sincerity

* **Using false protection methods (superstitions)**
  * Hanging evil eye charms (like blue eye), shoes, or objects
  * Burning chilies or using cultural rituals for protection
  * Believing these methods protect instead of relying on Allah

---

## Important Note
* **Major shirk** → unforgivable if a person dies without repentance
* **Minor & hidden shirk** → dangerous but forgivable with repentance

---

* Asking help from people in normal situations is NOT shirk
  * Example: “Please give me water”
  * Even the Prophet Prophet Muhammad preferred self-reliance, but asking normal help is allowed
