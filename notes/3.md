# The Final Days of the Prophet ﷺ & the First Khalifa — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: The Mission Complete — The Last Hajj

### The State of Islam After 23 Years
- After **23 years** of prophethood, the transformation was extraordinary:
  - The Prophet ﷺ was now the **undisputed leader of all Arabia**
  - All of Arabia had abandoned idol worship and embraced **Tawheed (monotheism)**
  - The Islamic Empire had become a **global power**
  - News had spread worldwide: *"A Prophet in Arabia has united all Arabs under one God"*
  - People were traveling from distant lands just to **see and meet him**

### The First and Last Hajj
- The Prophet ﷺ performed Hajj **only once** in his entire life — and it was also his last
- This Hajj is known as **Hajjat ul-Wida** — the Farewell Pilgrimage
- At the plains of **Arafat**, more than **100,000 people** stood before him
- They came from every race:
  - White, Brown, Black
  - Arab and non-Arab
  - Every nationality present in the Islamic world

### The Farewell Sermon — Words That Shook the World
The Prophet ﷺ delivered one of the most important speeches in human history:

**On Racism:**
> *"No white person has superiority over a black person, and no Arab has superiority over a non-Arab — except through piety (Taqwa)"*
- This was the **first formal declaration against racism** in human history
- Some of these principles are **part of the UN Charter today**

**On Women's Rights:**
- He addressed the rights of women explicitly — a revolutionary concept in 7th century Arabia

**On Life and Property:**
> *"Your blood, your wealth, and your honor are sacred — as sacred as this day, this month, and this city"*

**The Final Question:**
- He asked the 100,000+ people:
  > *"Have I conveyed the message?"*
- They all replied: *"Yes — you have"*
- He raised his finger to the sky and said:
  > *"O Allah — bear witness"*
- The entire crowd confirmed that the Prophet ﷺ had **fulfilled his duty as the final Messenger**

### The Divine Confirmation
- During this Hajj, Allah revealed what Muslims believe is one of the last verses of the Quran — **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:3)**:
  > *"Today I have perfected your religion for you, completed My favor upon you, and approved Islam as your religion"*
- When Abu Bakr ؓ heard this verse, he **wept** — he understood its implication
- Others asked why he was crying at such a joyful announcement
- He replied: *"When something is completed — it means it is about to end"*

## 🛤️ Part 2: The Sermon of Ghadir Khumm

### On the Way Back from Hajj
- As the Prophet ﷺ and the massive crowd returned from Mecca toward Medina, he stopped at a place called **Ghadir Khumm**
- He delivered another major sermon to thousands of Muslims gathered there

### The Famous Statement About Ali ؓ
- The Prophet ﷺ declared in front of thousands:
  > *"Whoever I am his Maula (master/leader/friend) — Ali is also his Maula"*
- He also said at Tabuk when leaving Ali ؓ behind as Medina's leader:
  > *"Ali's relation to me is like Harun's ؑ relation to Musa ؑ — except there is no Prophet after me"*

### The Historical Controversy
- This statement became the **foundation of the greatest divide in Islamic history**
- **Sunni Muslims (87% of all Muslims)** interpret "Maula" as "friend/supporter" — meaning the Prophet ﷺ was expressing his love for Ali ؓ and asking people to love him too
- **Shia Muslims (13% of all Muslims)** interpret "Maula" as "leader/successor" — meaning the Prophet ﷺ was formally appointing Ali ؓ as his successor
- This difference in interpretation has divided Muslims for **1,400 years**

---

## ⚔️ Part 3: The Final Army — Usama's Expedition

### Honoring the Martyrs of Mu'tah
- Back in Medina, the Prophet ﷺ prepared **one final military expedition** against the Roman Empire
- Purpose: To avenge the Muslims martyred at the **Battle of Mu'tah**
- The commander he appointed: **Usama bin Zaid ؓ** — the son of Zaid bin Haritha ؓ who had been the first commander at Mu'tah and was martyred there
- This was a deeply meaningful gesture — the son avenging his father's martyrdom

### The Army Stalls
- The army had assembled and was about to depart
- Then — **the Prophet ﷺ suddenly fell ill**
- The army **stopped and waited** — nobody wanted to leave while the Prophet ﷺ was sick

---

## 💔 Part 4: The Final Illness

### The Progression
- The illness came **suddenly** and grew progressively worse
- Despite extreme weakness, the Prophet ﷺ continued leading prayers at **Masjid an-Nabawi** for as long as he could
- This showed his dedication — leading his community in prayer even from his sickbed

### The Final Days — Unable to Lead Prayer
- In his **last days**, the Prophet ﷺ could no longer stand for prayer
- He gave a specific instruction:
  > *"Tell Abu Bakr to lead the prayers in my place"*
- Hazrat Aisha ؓ suggested others — knowing her father's tender heart
- The Prophet ﷺ insisted: **Abu Bakr ؓ must lead**
- This was seen later as a **significant indication** of who should succeed him

### The Final Location
- The Prophet ﷺ spent his **last days** in the room of **Hazrat Aisha ؓ**
- This room was directly attached to **Masjid an-Nabawi**
- Today, this is where his **blessed tomb** stands — visited by millions every year

## 😢 Part 5: The Death — The Most Devastating News in History

### The Announcement
- One day, the voice came from Hazrat Aisha's ؓ room:
  > *"The Messenger of Allah ﷺ has passed away"*
- The people of Medina were **completely stunned**
- His age at death:
  - By today's calendar: **61 years**
  - By the Islamic lunar calendar: **63 years**
- All of Medina began **weeping loudly**

### Umar's ؓ Reaction — Denial and Shock

**Hazrat Umar ؓ drew his sword** and stood in the mosque declaring:
> *"Anyone who says the Prophet ﷺ has died — I will cut off his head! He has gone to meet Allah like Musa ؑ went to Mount Tur for 40 days — and he will return!"*

**Why did Umar ؓ say this?**
- He was reasoning from the story of **Musa ؑ** — who left his people for 40 days and they thought he had died, but he returned
- Umar ؓ was also thinking of all the Prophet's ﷺ unfulfilled prophecies:
  > *"One day Muslims will conquer both Rome and Persia"*
  > *"One day Muslims will be the world's greatest power"*
- He thought: *"How can he leave us before all of this happens?"*
- Umar ؓ did not know that **Allah would fulfill these promises through him personally**

---

## 🕊️ Part 6: Abu Bakr's ؓ Greatest Moment — The Speech That Saved Islam

### Abu Bakr ؓ Goes to the Prophet ﷺ
- While Umar ؓ was standing with his sword, Abu Bakr ؓ quietly went to Aisha's ؓ room
- He uncovered the blessed face of the Prophet ﷺ
- He **kissed his forehead** and said:
  > *"You are beautiful in life — and beautiful in death. May my father and mother be sacrificed for you"*
- Then he recited:
  > *"You will surely die, and they will surely die"* — (Quran 39:30)

### The Moment Abu Bakr ؓ Stepped Up
- The man who always **cried at the Prophet's ﷺ words** more than anyone
- On the day when all of Medina was crying — **Abu Bakr ؓ controlled his grief**
- He knew: *"This is not just a day for crying. This is the day to carry the mission forward."*
- He stepped onto the **Prophet's ﷺ minbar (pulpit)** and addressed everyone

### The Most Important Speech in Islamic History

**First to Umar ؓ:**
> *"Umar — sit down"*
- Umar ؓ refused — still standing with his sword
- Abu Bakr ؓ said: *"Leave Umar"* and addressed everyone directly

**To All Muslims:**
> *"O people — whoever worshipped Muhammad, let him know that Muhammad has passed away.*
> *And whoever worshipped Allah — let him know that Allah is Ever-Living and will never die"*

**Then he recited the Quranic verse from Surah Al-Imran (3:144) — revealed after the Battle of Uhud:**
> *"Muhammad is no more than a Messenger. Messengers have passed away before him. If he dies or is killed — will you turn back on your heels? And whoever turns back will not harm Allah at all. And Allah will reward the grateful."*

### The Effect — Umar ؓ Collapses
- The moment Abu Bakr ؓ recited this verse, **Hazrat Umar ؓ** — one of the physically strongest and bravest men in Arabia — **fell to the ground**
- His legs gave way beneath him
- The companions said later:
  > *"It was as if we were hearing that verse for the very first time"*
- Gradually, all of Medina came to terms with the reality

## 👑 Part 7: The Crisis of Succession — Who Leads Next?

### The Emergency Meeting
- While the Prophet's ﷺ body was still awaiting burial, news came that the **Ansar of Medina** were gathering to choose a new leader
- They were about to give their pledge (**Bayah**) to **Sa'd bin Ubada ؓ** — the chief of the Khazraj tribe (the largest tribe in Medina)
- Their reasoning: *"The capital is Medina — so the leader should be from Medina"*

### Abu Bakr ؓ and Umar ؓ Rush to the Meeting
- Abu Bakr ؓ, Umar ؓ, and Abu Ubayda ؓ immediately left and went to the gathering
- Abu Bakr ؓ addressed the crowd:

> *"This is no longer just the small state of Medina. This is now the empire of ALL of Arabia. The Arabs will only accept a leader from the Quraysh — because:*
> *1. Quraysh are from Mecca — the most sacred city in Arabia*
> *2. Quraysh defended the Kaaba against Abraha's 60,000-man army through a miracle — earning eternal respect*
> *3. The Prophet ﷺ himself was from the Quraysh"*

- The crowd **agreed with this reasoning**
- They abandoned the idea of choosing Sa'd bin Ubada ؓ

### The Back-and-Forth — Nobody Can Agree
- Various names were suggested — no consensus
- Finally, Abu Bakr ؓ suggested:
  > *"Choose between Umar and Abu Ubayda — either of them is worthy"*

### Umar's ؓ Decision — The Unanimous Choice
- Umar ؓ immediately responded:
  > *"No — you are better than both of us. You were the Prophet's ﷺ companion in the cave. You led the prayers in his place. There is no one better for this position than you."*
- Umar ؓ placed his hand in Abu Bakr's ؓ hand and gave his **pledge of allegiance (Bayah)**
- The moment Umar ؓ did this — **every person in the gathering immediately followed**
- All names that had been debated moments before were **instantly forgotten**
- **Abu Bakr ؓ became the first Khalifa of Islam**

### The Historic Significance
- In virtually **every other empire** in history, a change of leadership meant:
  - Wars
  - Assassination
  - Family coups
  - Years of bloodshed
- Here — the **world's largest empire** chose its new leader through **consultation (Shura)** within hours of the Prophet's ﷺ death
- **Completely peacefully**

## 💔 Part 8: Ali's ؓ Absence — The Beginning of the Great Divide

### Where Was Ali ؓ?
- Hazrat Ali ؓ was **not present at the succession meeting**
- He was occupied with the **Prophet's ﷺ funeral preparation and burial**
- This was among the most important things he could be doing — but it meant he missed the meeting

### Ali's ؓ Initial Reaction
- When Ali ؓ learned about the meeting and the decision, he **initially did not give his pledge**
- His objection: *"Such an important decision was made without the presence of the Prophet's ﷺ family"*
- This was not a rebellion — it was a principled objection

### Abu Sufyan's ؓ Attempt to Cause Fitna
- Abu Sufyan ؓ — who had only recently accepted Islam — came to Ali ؓ and said:
  > *"Abu Bakr is from a weak clan of Quraysh. If you say the word, I will raise an army against him for you"*
- Ali ؓ **firmly and completely refused**:
  > *"No — absolutely not"*
- Ali ؓ would not allow a civil war to tear apart what the Prophet ﷺ had built

### The Final Resolution
- After some time, **Ali ؓ gave his pledge to Abu Bakr ؓ**
- He then played a **major role** in supporting and advancing the Khilafat
- The Islamic state remained united

## 📖 Part 9: The Sunni-Shia Divide — Explained Fairly

### The Core Disagreement

| | **Sunni View (87% of Muslims)** | **Shia View (13% of Muslims)** |
|--|--------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Did the Prophet ﷺ appoint a successor?** | No — he left it to Muslims to decide by consultation | Yes — he appointed Ali ؓ at Ghadir Khumm |
| **What does "Maula" mean at Ghadir?** | Friend/supporter/beloved | Leader/successor/ruler |
| **Was Abu Bakr's ؓ election correct?** | Yes — it followed Islamic principles of Shura | No — it bypassed the Prophet's ﷺ designated successor |
| **Who should have been first Khalifa?** | Abu Bakr ؓ | Ali ؓ |

Both sides **deeply love the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr ؓ, and Ali ؓ** — the disagreement is purely about succession and interpretation.

## 👔 Part 10: Abu Bakr ؓ as Khalifa — Leadership with Humility

### The Morning After
- The very **next day** after being chosen as Khalifa — the most powerful ruler in Arabia — Abu Bakr ؓ **went to open his cloth shop in the market**
- Companions asked: *"What are you doing here?"*
- He said: *"If I don't do business — how will I feed my family?"*

### Setting the Salary
- Muslims said: *"We should set you a salary"*
- Abu Bakr ؓ agreed — on one condition:
  > *"Set my salary equal to an ordinary laborer's wage"*
- Companions said: *"A laborer's wage is very little"*
- Abu Bakr ؓ replied:
  > *"Then increase the laborer's wage — and give me the same"*
- **The leader of the world's greatest empire** refused to take more than a common worker

### Immediate Actions as Khalifa

**Action 1 — Dispatched Usama's ؓ Army**
- The first thing Abu Bakr ؓ did was **send Usama's army** that had been waiting
- Some companions objected: *"The Prophet ﷺ just died — this is not the time for a military campaign"*
- Abu Bakr ؓ replied:
  > *"I will not untie a string that the Prophet ﷺ tied"*
- Whatever the Prophet ﷺ had set in motion — continued

**Action 2 — The Riddah Wars (Wars Against Apostasy)**
- With the Prophet ﷺ gone, the **hypocrites came out openly**
- They had been silent before because the Prophet ﷺ received divine revelation — he knew things before they happened
- Now they declared:
  > *"We accepted Muhammad — not Abu Bakr. We will not pay Zakat anymore"*
- Some **false prophets** also emerged — most notably **Musaylama al-Kadhdhab** (Musaylama the Liar)
- He had actually been making his false claims even during the Prophet's ﷺ lifetime
- Abu Bakr ؓ made a firm declaration:
  > *"By Allah — I will fight anyone who separates prayer from Zakat. If someone used to give even a rope as Zakat to the Prophet ﷺ and now refuses — I will fight them for it"*
