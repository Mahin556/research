# How Your Salary Is Structured — From Offer Letter to Bank Account

---

## 1. CTC — Cost to Company

The **total amount a company spends on you** per year. This is the big number on your offer letter — but it is NOT what lands in your bank account.

```
CTC = In-hand salary + PF + Gratuity + Health insurance + Training costs + All allowances
```

> ⚠️ The most common mistake: assuming CTC ÷ 12 = monthly take-home. It never does.

---

## 2. Gross Salary

Your salary **before any deductions** (tax, PF, etc.) are applied.

```
Gross Salary = Basic + HRA + All Allowances
```

---

## 3. Basic Salary

The **core component** of your pay. No exemptions apply here — it is **100% taxable**.

- Usually 40–50% of CTC
- PF and Gratuity are both calculated on Basic
- A lower Basic means more room for tax-saving allowances

> 💡 Lower Basic → more allowances → less tax overall.

---

## 4. HRA — House Rent Allowance

Given to cover rent expenses. If you **actually pay rent**, a portion is tax-exempt.

| City Type | Tax-free HRA limit |
|---|---|
| Non-metro cities | Up to 40% of Basic |
| Metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, etc.) | Up to 50% of Basic |
| Own house | Entire HRA is taxable |

> 💡 Keep your rent receipts — you'll need them during investment declaration.

---

## 5. Medical Allowance

Previously ₹15,000/year was tax-free. It is now merged into the **Standard Deduction**, but some companies still show it separately on the salary slip.

---

## 6. Special Allowances

Companies split part of your salary into specific allowance buckets. When you submit actual bills for these, **that amount is not taxed**.

| Allowance | Example |
|---|---|
| 🍽️ Food / Meal | Restaurant bills, Swiggy receipts |
| 👶 Children's School Fee | School fee receipts |
| 🌐 Internet | Broadband monthly bill |
| 📱 Phone | Mobile postpaid / recharge bill |
| 📚 Books & Magazines | Amazon / bookstore invoice |
| ⛽ Petrol / Conveyance | Petrol pump receipts |

> ⚠️ If you declared ₹40,000 but only submitted ₹30,000 in bills, the remaining ₹10,000 becomes taxable income.

---

## 7. Provident Fund (PF)

A **government-mandated retirement savings** scheme shared between you and your employer.

```
Your contribution       = 12% of Basic
Employer's contribution = 12% of Basic (matched)
  └─ 8.33% → EPS (pension)
  └─ 3.67% → Your PF account
```

- Returns are excellent (~8%+ per year, government-backed)
- Can be withdrawn 3 months after leaving a company
- Deducted before salary hits your bank — automatic saving

> 💡 Think of PF as forced, high-return savings you can't accidentally spend.

---

## 8. Gratuity

A **loyalty bonus** from the company — but only paid out after **completing 5 years**.

```
Gratuity = (Basic × 15 × Years of service) ÷ 26
```

- Leave before 5 years → you forfeit it entirely
- It appears in your CTC every month but you never see it until you qualify

---

## 9. Corporate Health Insurance

Health coverage provided by your company — **always use it**. It is often far better than what you can buy individually.

- Can cover you, spouse, children, and sometimes parents
- Included in your CTC calculation
- Some companies also provide term life insurance

---

## The Big Picture — Where Your Money Goes

```
CTC
 └─ − Gratuity provision
     └─ Gross Salary
         └─ − PF (your 12%)
             └─ − Income Tax (TDS)
                 └─ ✅ In-hand / Net Salary
```

---

## How to Increase Your In-Hand Salary

- ✅ Submit actual bills for every allowance you have (food, petrol, phone, internet)
- ✅ Claim HRA if you pay rent — keep rent receipts and landlord's PAN
- ✅ Fill your Investment Declaration at the start of the year, not in March
- ✅ Use your corporate health insurance — don't pay out of pocket unnecessarily
- ❌ Never submit fake bills — it is illegal and can end your career

> 📌 If you skip bills all year and realise in March, a large lump-sum tax gets deducted from your last few salary credits — plan ahead.

**No, you cannot claim HRA tax exemption if you live in your own house.**

Here's how it works:

**If you own your house and live in it:**
- HRA will still appear on your salary slip (it's part of your salary structure)
- But the entire HRA amount becomes **fully taxable**
- You cannot claim any exemption on it

**If you own a house but live in a rented place** (e.g., you own a house in your hometown but rent in the city where you work):
- You **can** still claim HRA exemption for the rent you pay
- You need to submit rent receipts and your landlord's PAN (if rent exceeds ₹1 lakh/year)

**If you pay rent to your parents** (and you don't own the house):
- This is legally allowed
- You can claim HRA, and your parents declare it as rental income on their side
- Proper rent agreement is recommended

---

**Bottom line:** HRA exemption is only for people who are actually paying rent. Living in your own home means no exemption — but the HRA component still gets added to your taxable income, so you end up paying more tax on it.

# How to Claim All Allowances — Complete Process

> Do all of this and you recover roughly **₹8,000/month** in extra take-home on a ₹40L CTC — even with an own house.

---

## The 4 Core Habits

| When | What to do |
|---|---|
| **April** | Fill Investment Declaration on your company's HR portal |
| **Every month** | Drop bills (food, petrol, internet, phone) into a folder |
| **February** | Upload all bills to the HR portal before deadline |
| **July** | File ITR on incometax.gov.in using your Form 16 |

---

## Step 1 — Know What Allowances You Have

First, check your **offer letter or salary slip** — it lists every allowance. Common ones on a ₹40L CTC:

| Allowance | Monthly Amount | Annual Tax-free |
|---|---|---|
| 🍽️ Food / Meal | ₹2,200/month | ₹26,400/yr |
| 🌐 Internet | ₹1,500/month | ₹18,000/yr |
| 📱 Phone | ₹1,000/month | ₹12,000/yr |
| ⛽ Petrol / Conveyance | ₹3,200/month | ₹38,400/yr |
| 📚 Books & Magazines | varies | Tax-free with bills |
| 👶 Children School Fee | varies | ₹100/child/month |

> 💡 If you can't find the list, ask your HR or check your company's payroll portal — it is always listed there.

---

## Step 2 — Do Investment Declaration at Year Start (April)

Every April, your company's HR/payroll portal opens an **Investment Declaration form**. This is where you *promise* how much you will spend/invest — your TDS is then calculated based on this.

1. Login to your company's **HRMS / payroll portal** (e.g. Darwinbox, Keka, Greytip, Workday)
2. Find the section called **"Investment Declaration"** or **"IT Declaration"**
3. Enter amounts for each allowance you plan to use
4. Enter 80C investments (PF, PPF, ELSS, LIC etc.)
5. Submit before the deadline (usually **April–May**)

> ⚠️ If you skip this step, your company assumes zero deductions and deducts **maximum TDS** every month from day one.

---

## Step 3 — Collect Bills Every Month (Don't Skip This)

After declaring, you must actually **collect proof** that you spent the money.

| Allowance | What bill/proof to keep | Format |
|---|---|---|
| 🍽️ Food / Meal | Restaurant bills, Swiggy/Zomato receipts, canteen receipts | PDF / printed |
| 🌐 Internet | Monthly broadband bill (Airtel, Jio, BSNL etc.) | PDF email bill |
| 📱 Phone | Mobile recharge receipt / postpaid bill | PDF / SMS |
| ⛽ Petrol | Petrol pump receipts / FastTag statement | Printed / PDF |
| 📚 Books | Amazon / bookstore invoice | PDF email |
| 👶 School Fee | School fee receipt with child's name | Printed receipt |

> 💡 Make a folder on your phone/Google Drive — drop every bill in it the same day you get it. Takes 10 seconds and saves thousands.

---

## Step 4 — Submit Bills to HR Portal (Jan–Feb)

Around **January–February**, your company opens bill submission. You upload the collected bills to prove you actually spent the declared amount.

1. Login to HRMS portal → find **"Proof of Investment"** or **"Bill Submission"**
2. Upload scanned/PDF copies of each bill
3. Enter the amount spent per category
4. Submit before the deadline (usually **February 15**)

> ⚠️ If bills submitted < amount declared → the difference becomes taxable.
> **Example:** Declared ₹3,200 petrol but submitted only ₹2,000 bills → ₹1,200 extra becomes taxable income.

---

## Step 5 — Submit 80C & Other Investment Proofs

Along with bills, also submit proof of **tax-saving investments**:

| Section | Proof Required | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **80C** | PF statement (auto), ELSS statement, PPF passbook, LIC receipt | ₹1,50,000 |
| **80D** | Health insurance premium receipt | ₹25,000 (self) / ₹50,000 (parents 60+) |
| **80CCD(1B)** | NPS contribution statement | ₹50,000 extra over 80C |
| **Section 24b** | Bank interest certificate (home loan) | ₹2,00,000 on interest |

> 💡 Your PF (₹1,92,000/yr) already exceeds the ₹1,50,000 limit of 80C — so this is **automatically taken care of**. You don't need to invest more for 80C.

---

## Step 6 — File ITR in July (Recover Any Extra Tax Paid)

Even after all this, if extra TDS was deducted, you can get a **refund** by filing your Income Tax Return (ITR).

1. Go to **incometax.gov.in**
2. File **ITR-1** (for salaried employees)
3. Deadline: **July 31** every year
4. Your **Form 16** (given by employer in June) has all the details pre-filled
5. Any excess TDS is refunded directly to your bank account

> 💡 Form 16 = your company's summary of all TDS deducted. You just verify and submit — it takes 15 minutes.

---

## Full Year Calendar — What to Do When

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| **April** | Fill IT declaration on HRMS portal. Enter all allowances + 80C plans. Low TDS starts from this month. |
| **Apr → Jan** | Drop every receipt into a folder. Food, petrol, internet, phone — every single one. |
| **Jan–Feb** | Upload all bills and 80C/80D proofs to HRMS portal before the Feb 15 deadline. |
| **March** | Company reconciles everything. If tax short → extra deducted. If over-deducted → refunded in salary. |
| **Jun–Jul** | Get Form 16 from employer. File ITR-1 on incometax.gov.in by July 31. Refund hits bank in 2–4 weeks. |
