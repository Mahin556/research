---
title: 10 things you should NEVER share at your workplace
category: Corporate
tags: [Corporate]
---

---

**1. Your Salary**
Sharing it invites jealousy, comparison, and resentment. If asked, just say: *"I'm happy with my package and focused on growth."*

**2. How Close You Live to the Office**
If people know you're nearby, you'll become the default person for last-minute emergencies and extra work. Just say: *"I have a manageable commute."*

**3. Your Future Job Plans**
If your boss finds out you want to switch, you'll be passed over for promotions and key projects. Discuss career goals only with trusted mentors.

**4. Complaints About Your Boss**
Office walls have ears. A casual rant can reach your manager fast and damage your performance reviews. Vent to friends outside work, or write it in a journal.

**5. Your Side Hustle**
Your employer may see it as a distraction, and jealous colleagues might report you. Share it only if it doesn't violate company policy.

**6. Your Dating Life**
Personal relationship drama spreads fast in offices and affects how people judge your professionalism. Keep your private life private.

**7. Time-Wasting Hobbies**
Saying you binge-watch Netflix for hours daily makes people question your work ethic. Share only hobbies that signal growth, like reading or learning new skills.

**8. Your Secret Shortcuts**
If you've found a smarter way to work, keep it to yourself. Colleagues may steal credit, and your boss may just pile on more work.

**9. Personal Struggles**
The office is not therapy. Today's sympathy can become tomorrow's gossip and affect how people perceive your ability to handle pressure.

**10. Your True Opinions About Colleagues**
You never know who's friends with whom. Stay neutral, keep your poker face on, and vent only to trusted friends outside work.

---

The core lesson: **maintain clear boundaries between your personal and professional life.** Oversharing might feel natural, but in a corporate environment it can seriously derail your career.
