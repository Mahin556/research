## ✅ Farz (Obligatory) of Nikah — **3 things**

These are the essential pillars without which the Nikah is **invalid**:

1. **Eejab (Proposal)** — One party must clearly state the offer of marriage in past tense (e.g., "I have given my daughter in Nikah to you").
2. **Qubool (Acceptance)** — The other party must clearly accept it in the same sitting, also in past tense (e.g., "I have accepted").
3. **Presence of Witnesses** — At least **2 adult Muslim male witnesses** (or 1 male + 2 female witnesses), who must hear every word of the Eejab and Qubool. The witnesses must be free, sane, and of legal age.

> Without any one of these three, the Nikah does not take place at all.

---

## ⚠️ Wajib (Necessary) of Nikah — **1 main thing**

1. **Mahr (Dowry/Mehr)** — It is wajib upon the husband to give Mahr to the wife. Not stating it does not invalidate the Nikah, but it remains obligatory and must be paid.

---

## 🌙 Sunnat (Recommended) of Nikah — **several things**

These are the Sunnah practices that should be followed:

1. **Reciting the Khutba-e-Nikah** (preferably the one mentioned in Hadith)
2. **Performing the Nikah in a Masjid**
3. **Performing it on a Friday**
4. **Announcing it publicly** (so it is known to people)
5. **Conducting it in the presence of trustworthy and righteous witnesses**
6. **Walima** (feast after marriage) — this is also Sunnah Muakkadah (emphasized Sunnah)

---

### 📌 A note on Nikah itself:
The ruling of Nikah as a whole depends on the person's situation:
- **Sunnat Muakkadah** — for a person with normal desires who can fulfill the rights of marriage
- **Wajib** — if there is a strong fear of falling into sin (zina) without it
- **Farz** — if one is certain they will commit sin without marriage and can fulfill obligations
- **Makrooh** — if one fears they cannot fulfill the rights of the spouse
