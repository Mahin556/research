# The Hijra — The Most Important Migration in History — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: Why Hijra Is the Starting Point of the Islamic Calendar

### Umar's ؓ Decision
- After Muslims became a superpower spanning **three continents**, Hazrat Umar ؓ — the most powerful ruler in the world at that time — needed to create an **Islamic calendar**
- The question was: *What should be Day 1 of this calendar?*
- He chose **the day of Hijra** — not the Prophet's ﷺ birth, not the first revelation
- Why? Because **Hijra changed not just Islamic history — but all of world history forever**

### The Scale of What Hijra Led To
- Before Hijra: Only a **handful of people** had accepted Islam after 12 years of effort
- After Hijra: Within just a **few years**, Muslims ruled **three continents** and became the world's greatest superpower

---

## 📜 Part 2: The Situation Before Hijra — 12 Years of Failure?

### The Reality
- The Prophet ﷺ had been giving Dawah (invitation to Islam) in Mecca for **12 full years**
- Despite all his efforts, only a **small number** had accepted Islam
- Even this small number was **unbearable** to the Meccans

### The Opportunity Opens — Medina
- About **400 km north** of Mecca, in the city of Medina, two major tribes — **Aws and Khazraj** — had many members who had already accepted Islam
- They wanted to bring the Prophet ﷺ to Medina as their **leader and ruler**
- **72 people from Medina** came to Mecca and formally made this offer to the Prophet ﷺ
- He accepted

### The Secret Migration Begins
- Muslims began **secretly and gradually** moving from Mecca to Medina
- Approximately **70-80 Muslims** made the Hijra
- Despite being in the **greatest danger** himself, the Prophet ﷺ was among the **last to leave** — ensuring everyone else was safe first

---

## 🗡️ Part 3: The Assassination Plot — Abu Jahl's Master Plan

### The Problem the Meccans Faced
- Mecca was a **deeply tribal society** — every clan protected its own members, right or wrong
- If **any single tribe** killed the Prophet ﷺ, his clan **Banu Hashim** — one of the strongest clans in Mecca — would take revenge on that tribe alone
- This meant no single tribe was willing to take the risk alone

### Abu Jahl's Brilliant but Evil Solution
> *"If one clan can't kill him alone — let ALL clans do it together. Select one young man from EVERY clan. They will all strike simultaneously. Then Banu Hashim cannot fight ALL of us — they would have to accept blood money instead of revenge."*

- Every clan selected **one young warrior**
- **11 men in total** gathered secretly
- They surrounded the Prophet's ﷺ house at **midnight**
- Their plan: Strike together at dawn when he emerged

### The House Was Empty
- When dawn came and they entered — they found **Hazrat Ali ؓ sleeping in the Prophet's ﷺ bed**
- The Prophet ﷺ was **already gone**

---

## 🌙 Part 4: The Night of Departure — Every Detail Perfectly Planned

### How the Prophet ﷺ Escaped
- The Prophet ﷺ and **Abu Bakr ؓ** had planned everything carefully in advance
- That night, the Prophet ﷺ walked **right past the 11 armed men** waiting outside
- He recited verses of the Quran as he passed — Allah caused them not to see him

### The Direction — A Genius Deception
- Medina is to the **NORTH** of Mecca — the obvious direction to run
- Instead, the Prophet ﷺ headed **SOUTH** — toward Yemen — the exact opposite direction
- He hid in **Cave of Thawr (Ghar-e-Thawr)** — just **5 km south** of Mecca
- His reasoning: *"They will search the Medina road first. By going south, we buy time."*

---

## 💰 Part 5: The Manhunt — 100 Camels Reward

### The Announcement
- The very next day, Abu Sufyan and Abu Jahl made a public announcement across Mecca:
  > *"Whoever brings Muhammad and Abu Bakr — dead or alive — will receive **100 camels** as reward"*
- In today's value: approximately **1-2 crore rupees (₹10-20 million)**
- The entire Arab world began searching

### The Search Party Reaches the Cave
- Searchers fanned out in all directions
- One group **actually reached Cave of Thawr** — directly at the entrance
- They were **inches away** from discovering the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr ؓ inside
- Yet they turned back without looking inside

### What Happened at the Cave
- When Abu Bakr ؓ saw the searchers at the cave entrance, he was terrified — not for himself but for the Prophet ﷺ
- He whispered: *"They are right there — if one of them just looks down, they'll see us"*
- The Prophet ﷺ calmly replied:
  > *"O Abu Bakr — what do you think about two people, the third of whom is Allah?"*
- Allah caused the searchers to turn away without looking

### How Hazrat Ali ؓ Was Treated
- Back in Mecca, Abu Jahl and others **beat Hazrat Ali ؓ severely** trying to get information about the Prophet's ﷺ location
- Hazrat Ali ؓ said **absolutely nothing**

### Hazrat Asma ؓ — Slapped but Unbroken
- Abu Jahl went to Abu Bakr's ؓ house and questioned his daughter **Hazrat Asma ؓ**
- She refused to give any information
- Abu Jahl **slapped her hard across the face**
- Despite this, for **three days**, Hazrat Asma ؓ secretly **carried food to the cave** — hiding from the Meccan searchers every time

---

## 🐪 Part 6: The Guide — Merit Over Religion

### The Problem of Finding a Guide
- After three days, the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr ؓ needed to leave the cave
- They needed a **guide** who knew secret desert routes that ordinary Arabs never used
- The challenge: This person would know the Prophet's ﷺ location — if tempted by the 100-camel reward, he could betray them

### An Extraordinary Decision
- Abu Bakr ؓ had already arranged a man named **Abdullah bin Urayqit**
- He had given him **two of his best camels** with instructions to meet them at the cave after three days
- The remarkable detail: **Abdullah bin Urayqit was NOT a Muslim at the time**
- Yet he was chosen for this most critical mission in Islamic history

### Why a Non-Muslim Was Chosen
The Prophet ﷺ chose him based entirely on **merit**:
- He had **unparalleled knowledge** of Arabia's secret, little-known desert routes
- These were paths that **no other Arab traveled** — making discovery impossible
- His **skill and trustworthiness** mattered more than his religion for this task

> *"The Prophet ﷺ chose the right person for the right job — based on ability, not just on faith. This itself is one of the greatest lessons in Islamic leadership."*

### The Prophet's ﷺ Insistence on Paying
- When the camels arrived, the Prophet ﷺ told Abu Bakr ؓ:
  > *"I will only ride this camel on one condition — that I pay you its full price"*
- Even in these **most dangerous and desperate circumstances** — being hunted by the entire Arab world — the Prophet ﷺ insisted on **paying for what he used**
- He would not take even a camel without proper payment

### Before Leaving — Returning Trusts
- Before departing Mecca, the Prophet ﷺ had **left Hazrat Ali ؓ behind** for an important reason
- Despite every tribe in Mecca wanting to kill the Prophet ﷺ, people from across Mecca had **trusted him with their valuables** — because of his title **"Al-Amin" (The Trustworthy)**
- Even his enemies trusted him with their goods
- The Prophet ﷺ left Ali ؓ to **return every single trust** to its rightful owner before leaving
- He would not leave Mecca owing anyone anything

---

## 🛤️ Part 7: The Secret Route — By the Sea

### The Journey
- Abdullah bin Urayqit led them on a route **along the Red Sea coast** — completely unknown to others
- The route was so secret that despite the entire Arab world searching, **nobody found them**
- They traveled safely toward Medina

### Stop at Quba
- Before entering Medina, the Prophet ﷺ stopped at **Quba** — a settlement just outside Medina
- He stayed there for **a few days**
- He built the **first mosque in Islamic history** — **Masjid Quba** — while waiting
- This mosque is mentioned in the Quran itself

---

## 🎉 Part 8: The Welcome in Medina — The Most Memorable Reception in History

### The City Erupts in Joy
- The Prophet ﷺ had left Mecca in complete secrecy — hunted, with a price on his head
- But as he approached Medina, the **entire city came out to welcome him**
- Men, women, children — all lined the roads
- Young girls of Medina sang famous welcoming verses:
  > *"The full moon has risen upon us — from the valley of Wada'*
  > *Gratitude is due from us — as long as a caller calls to Allah"*
- This reception was fit for a **king and leader** — because that is exactly what he was to them

### Everyone Wanted Him to Stay With Them
- Every family in Medina wanted the **honor of hosting the Prophet ﷺ**
- People lined the route holding the reins of his camel, begging him to stay with them
- How could he choose one family without hurting everyone else?

### The Camel Decides
- The Prophet ﷺ made a **beautiful and fair decision**:
  > *"I will stay wherever my camel sits down — the decision is not mine"*
- The camel wandered through the streets of Medina
- It eventually **sat down** at a spot belonging to two orphan boys
- The Prophet ﷺ chose that spot
- **Masjid an-Nabawi (The Prophet's ﷺ Mosque)** was built on that very land
- He bought the land from the orphans at a fair price and paid for it

### Why This Decision Was Genius
- **Nobody's feelings were hurt** — the camel decided, not the Prophet ﷺ
- The **two orphan boys** — the most vulnerable members of society — received honor
- The mosque was built in a **central location** naturally chosen

---

## 🌍 Part 9: What Medina Became — The Transformation

### From Ordinary City to World Capital
- Before Hijra: Medina was just an ordinary Arab city
- After Hijra: Within a few years, **decisions affecting three continents** were being made from Medina

### The Ansar's Sacrifice — Never Forgotten
- The **Ansar (Helpers of Medina)** gave up their homes, their wealth, and shared everything with the arriving Muslim migrants
- They asked for nothing in return
- The Prophet ﷺ said about them:
  > *"If all people went one way and the Ansar went another — I would go with the Ansar"*
- This sacrifice of the Ansar is something **Muslims will never forget** as long as Islam exists
