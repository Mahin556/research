title: 21 tips for succeeding at your job,
category: Corporate

---

1. **Observe everything carefully** — Notice how things work, how people talk and behave. This helps you understand the company culture. You can't fight culture; you can only adapt to it.

2. **Don't wait for feedback — ask for it** — The longer you wait, the less you improve. Ask for feedback even if it feels uncomfortable.

3. **Listen more than you speak** — When you listen, you understand others' perspectives. Speaking only broadcasts your own.

4. **Understand company culture** — Fitting into the culture is as important as the quality of your work.

5. **Stay out of office politics** — If you get involved, you'll eventually become a victim of it. Avoid workplaces where politics is constant.

6. **Talk to people in other departments** — Ask what they know that you don't. Most inter-department conflicts happen because people never try to understand each other's perspective.

7. **Know your colleagues beyond work** — Build genuine human connections. In remote work this is harder, but still necessary.

8. **Learn first, then create impact** — Especially for young professionals in their 20s: don't try to make an impact before you've learned. Without learning, your work won't be trusted.

9. **Carry a notebook to meetings, not a laptop** — Laptops are distracting. Note everything down, then send a summary email to everyone afterward.

10. **Don't evaluate jobs only by salary** — Ask how much your *skills and exposure* will grow, not just how much money you'll make.

11. **Own your work** — Whatever you submit has your name on it. Take full responsibility.

12. **Honor your commitments** — If you commit to something, deliver it on time.

13. **Build genuine relationships at work** — Not just for networking, but because colleagues who become friends will genuinely help you grow.

14. **Never complain** — Complaining gives away control of your life to others. It signals you have no power over your own situation.

15. **Always be on time** — Be ready before things are supposed to begin, not just at the start time.

16. **Start with trust** — When meeting someone new, assume good intent. Assume they want the company and you to succeed, and help them accordingly.

17. **When in doubt, just ask** — Asking is one of the most powerful things you can do.

18. **Never disrespect anyone** — Regardless of their seniority or your mood, treat everyone with dignity.

19. **Let go of credit** — If you don't get credit for something, let it go. Work done purely for credit is work done to impress — and that mindset makes you anxious and ineffective.

20. **Acknowledge mistakes and don't repeat them** — Saying sorry matters only when the other person sees you've understood the mistake. Repeating it wastes even the lesson from the first time.

21. **Work for your own growth, not for approval** — Don't work so people will praise you or love you. Work because it's the right thing for your own development. The praise will follow naturally.
