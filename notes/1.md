Here’s a **simple, clear comparison of all forms of “be”**:

---

### 🔹 **Am / Is / Are (present)**

* Used for: *present time*

* **Am** → with I

  * I **am** happy

* **Is** → with he/she/it

  * She **is** a teacher

* **Are** → with you/we/they

  * They **are** ready

---

### 🔹 **Was / Were (past)**

* Used for: *past time*

* **Was** → I/he/she/it

  * I **was** tired

* **Were** → you/we/they

  * They **were** late

---

### 🔹 **Been (past participle)**

* Used with: *has/have/had*

* Example:

  * I have **been** busy
  * She has **been** to Jaipur

---

### 🔹 **Being (present participle)**

* Used in: *continuous / passive*

* Example:

  * He is **being** rude
  * The work is **being** done

---

### 🔹 Easy Summary

* **Be** → base form
* **Am / Is / Are** → present
* **Was / Were** → past
* **Been** → with have/has/had
* **Being** → continuous (-ing)

---

### 🔹 One Line Trick

* **Present → am/is/are**
* **Past → was/were**
* **Perfect → been**
* **Continuous → being**
* **Base → be**
