# Why Were Jews in Medina? — The Dead Sea Scrolls & The Prophecy — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: The Central Question

### A Historical Mystery
- During the **entire time the Prophet ﷺ was in Mecca** — not once in any incident were Jews mentioned
- But the **moment he arrived in Medina** — Jews were already living there in large numbers
- The question is: **Why were so many Jews living in Medina and surrounding areas?**
- The answer takes us back **over 1,000 years** before the Prophet ﷺ was born

---

## 📜 Part 2: The Dead Sea Scrolls — One of History's Greatest Archaeological Discoveries

### How They Were Found — The Accidental Discovery
- About **70 years ago** (around 1947), **three ordinary Arab shepherd boys** were wandering in the mountains of **Palestine**
- They noticed a **cave in a distant rocky hillside** they had never seen before
- Curious, they went inside
- Inside the cave they found **only two jars**
- They opened the jars and found **old papers and scrolls** inside
- Their reaction: *"We don't know what these are — probably nothing important"*
- They took the scrolls to a **market and sold them for a small amount of money**

### How They Reached the World of Science
- The scrolls passed through several hands and **eventually reached an American scientist**
- The scientist immediately recognized something extraordinary — these appeared to be a **heavenly book of the Jews (Torah)**
- But it was written in a **completely different and unusual style** from all modern versions of the Torah
- The scientist was **John C. Trever**

### The Scientific Verification
Trever compared these scrolls with the **oldest known Torah** of that time — which was already 1,800 years old:
- The writing style and language of both were **completely identical**
- He then arranged for **Carbon Dating** of the paper itself

**Carbon Dating Result:**
> The paper was **at least 2,200 years old**

### What This Means:
- This Torah — found by accident in a Palestinian cave by three shepherd boys — is one of the **oldest Torahs in the entire world**
- It predates **even Prophet Jesus ﷺ (Hazrat Isa ﷺ)**
- It is known today as the **Dead Sea Scrolls** or **Cave Bible**
- It is not the complete Torah but contains **many significant portions** of it

## 🔮 Part 3: The Prophecies Inside — Prophet Isaiah's Predictions

### Who Was Isaiah (Ayya)?
- The scrolls begin with references to a Jewish Prophet named **Isaiah (Ayya)**
- Both **Christians and Muslims** recognize him as a true prophet
- Inside these ancient scrolls, Isaiah had written **prophecies about future events**

## 📖 Part 4: The First Prophecy — About Cyrus the Great

### The Historical Context
- A powerful Babylonian king named **Bakht Nasr (Nebuchadnezzar)** had committed what historians compare to **Hitler-level atrocities** against the Jews — thousands of years before this Torah was even written
- Isaiah's **First Prophecy** stated:

> *"Bakht Nasr is oppressing us severely right now — but very soon, a powerful person will come who will destroy his kingdom and bring the Jews back to their homeland"*

### The Fulfillment — 300 Years BEFORE This Torah Was Even Written
- This prophecy was fulfilled by **Cyrus the Great (Cyrus II)** — the enormously powerful King of Persia
- Cyrus conquered Babylon, freed the Jews, and allowed them to return to their lands
- Interestingly, many modern Islamic scholars believe **Cyrus the Great** may actually be the same figure mentioned in the Quran as **Dhul-Qarnayn**
- The prophecy had been fulfilled **300 years before** this 2,200-year-old Torah was even copied

## 🌟 Part 5: The Second Prophecy — The One That Brought Jews to Medina

### The Exact Text of the Prophecy
The ancient Torah contains this remarkable passage:

> *"Behold My servant whom I uphold — I will put My spirit in him and he will establish justice in the world. He will not step back until justice is established on earth. No one will be able to crush him. And I will not give My praise to idols. Let the people of **Kedar** rejoice. Let the people of **Sela** climb the mountain and shout cries of joy. Let them give glory to the Lord."*

### Breaking Down the Key Words

**Word 1: KEDAR (قیدار)**
- Who is Kedar?
- Kedar was the **second son of Prophet Ismail ﷺ** — son of Prophet Ibrahim ﷺ
- The descendants of Kedar established a **great kingdom in Arabia**
- **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ himself** was from the lineage of Prophet Ismail ﷺ — specifically from the line of **Kedar**
- The prophecy mentions Kedar's people rejoicing → **clearly pointing to Arabia**

**Word 2: IDOLS**
- The prophecy mentions *"I will not give my praise to idols"*
- In that era, **idol worship was most prevalent in Arabia** — the Kaaba had 360 idols
- This further confirms the prophet will come to **an idol-worshipping land — Arabia**

**Word 3: SELA (סֶלַע)**
- The prophecy says: *"Let the people of Sela climb the mountain and shout cries of joy"*
- Spelled **S-E-L-A**
- This exact word is written the **same way in the Torah today** as it was 2,200 years ago

### Where is Sela?

> *"Don't take my word for it — open Google Maps, type 'Sela Arab' and you will be taken directly to the location"*

- **Sela is a mountain** — and it exists today
- It is located in **Medina**
- Not just anywhere in Medina — it is the **closest mountain to Masjid-e-Nabawi** (the Prophet's ﷺ Mosque)
- Distance from Masjid-e-Nabawi to Mount Sela: **only 500 meters** — not even half a kilometer

### The Prophecy's Meaning Becomes Clear
The ancient Torah was telling the Jews:

> *"The people living near Mount Sela — in Medina — should climb that mountain and shout with joy — because a man is coming to you who will establish justice across the entire world"*

## 🚶 Part 6: The Migration of Jews to Medina

### Acting on the Prophecy
- The Jews had **known about this prophecy for over 1,000 years**
- They were absolutely convinced: **a prophet was coming, and he would come near Medina**
- Gradually, **large numbers of Jews began migrating from Palestine to Medina** and its surroundings
- Their hope: *"Perhaps we will be alive when he comes — and we can meet him"*
- Additionally, after **wars between Jews and the Roman Empire**, even more Jews relocated to the Medina region
- They made **Khaybar** — the closest city to Medina — their headquarters

### The Jews' Impact on Medina
- The Arabs of Medina were **deeply impressed** by the Jews:
  - Their **knowledge and scholarship**
  - Their **business acumen and intelligence**

> *"Just as the entire world today is impressed by Jewish intelligence and business skills — the Arabs of Medina felt the same way 1,400 years ago"*

- Slowly, **Jewish population grew** in Medina
- Eventually, **conflicts began** between Arab tribes and Jewish tribes

### The Jews' Own Warning to Arabs
- Whenever disputes arose, the Jews would tell the Arab tribes:
  > *"Do whatever you want today — but when OUR prophet comes, we will not spare you"*
- The Arabs of Medina were **genuinely frightened** by this repeated warning
- Generation after generation, Medina's Arabs were told: *"A prophet is coming soon"*
- Eventually they became **completely convinced** — a prophet was indeed coming

## 📣 Part 7: The First Dawah to Medina — And Its Remarkable Reception

### Mus'ab bin Umair ؓ Arrives
- One day, a man arrived in Medina from Mecca — his name was **Mus'ab bin Umair ؓ**
- He came to give the people of Medina the invitation to Islam
- He told them: *"In Mecca, an Arab prophet has appeared — but the people of Mecca are not accepting him"*

### Why Medina Accepted So Quickly
- The people of Medina heard this and were **immediately struck**:
  > *"An Arab-speaking prophet? This must be the same prophet the Jews have been warning us about for generations!"*
- The people of Medina became **the first community in the world to accept Islam en masse**
- Their centuries of conditioning — hearing about the coming prophet — made them **mentally prepared** to accept

## 😮 Part 8: The Jewish Reaction — Denial Despite Knowing the Truth

### The Contradiction
- When Jews heard that *their* prophet had arrived among the Arabs — they **flatly denied it**:
  > *"This is NOT that prophet"*
- This was despite the fact that **they themselves had been predicting his arrival for 1,000 years**
- And despite the fact that the **signs matched** — an Arab prophet, from Kedar's lineage, near Mount Sela in Medina

### Some Jews Did Accept
- Not all Jews denied — **some Jews accepted Islam** and even gave their lives for it
- Interestingly, it was these **Jewish converts to Islam** who helped discover and verify the prophecies about Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the Torah
- **Hazrat Salman al-Farsi ؓ** — the Persian companion who suggested the trench at Khandaq — had also been sent to Medina by a **Christian priest** who told him: *"A prophet is coming there"*
- Multiple religious traditions — Jewish, Christian, and others — had knowledge of the coming prophet

### The Path of Denial Led to Destruction
- The majority of Jews not only **denied the Prophet ﷺ** but also **plotted against him** constantly
- They were behind the **Battle of Khandaq**
- They made alliances with the Meccans
- Their power was **permanently broken** at the **Battle of Khaybar**
- All the wealth they had accumulated over **hundreds of years** passed to the Muslims
