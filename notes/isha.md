## What is Isha?
- **Isha** is the **fifth and final daily prayer** in Islam. 
- It is prayed at **night**, after twilight disappears.
- It completes the daily cycle of prayers.
---
## Time of Isha
- **Starts:** When the red twilight (after sunset glow) disappears completely.
- **Ends:** At **midnight** (preferred time).
- Can be prayed until **before Fajr**, but delaying too much without reason is discouraged.
---
## Rak‘ahs in Isha

| Type                      | Rak‘ahs | Status                           |
| ------------------------- | ------- | -------------------------------- |
| Sunnah (Ghair Mu’akkadah) | 4       | Optional                         |
| Fard                      | 4       | Obligatory                       |
| Sunnah (Mu’akkadah)       | 2       | Strong Sunnah                    |
| Nafl                      | 2       | Optional                         |
| Witr                      | 3       | Wajib (Hanafi) / Sunnah (others) |
| Nafl (optional)           | 2       | Optional                         |

### Total = 17 Rak‘ahs (Minimum required = 4 Fard + 3 Witr)
---
## Importance of Isha
- Very rewarding prayer. 
- Prophet ﷺ encouraged praying Isha in congregation.
- Sleeping after Isha is recommended (unless beneficial reason).
---
## Recitation Rule
- Fard: **Aloud**
- Witr: **Aloud**
- Sunnah/Nafl: **Silent**
---
## Special Note: Tahajjud
- Tahajjud is prayed after Isha and before Fajr.
- It is optional but highly rewarding.
