### Metadata
- **Surah Name:** An-Naas (الناس)
- **Surah Number:** 114
- **Meaning of Name:** Mankind.
- **Classification:** Makki (Revealed in Mecca)
- **Number of Verses (Ayaat):** 6
- **Number of Sections (Rukoos):** 1
- **Juz (Para):** 30
- **Title/Theme:** Seeking refuge in Allah from the evil whisperings of Shaytaan (Satan).

### English Translation
**In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.**
1.  Say, "I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind,
2.  The Sovereign (King) of mankind,
3.  The God of mankind,
4.  From the evil of the retreating whisperer —
5.  Who whispers [evil] into the breasts of mankind —
6.  From among the jinn and mankind."

### Tafseer (Commentary / Haashiyah)

**Background of Revelation (Shaan-e-Nuzul):**
Surah An-Naas was revealed in conjunction with Surah Al-Falaq, often referred to as "Al-Mu'awwidhatayn" (The Two Surahs of Refuge). As detailed in the commentary of Surah Al-Falaq, these two Surahs were revealed to provide the Prophet Muhammad (صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ وَسَلَّمَ) with a divine means of protection against the harmful effects of black magic performed by Labeed bin A'sam. While Surah Al-Falaq focuses on seeking refuge from *external* physical harms (magic, darkness, envy), Surah An-Naas focuses on seeking refuge from an *internal* spiritual harm: the whisperings of Shaytaan that can corrupt a person's faith and heart.

**Explanation of the Verses:**

1.  **Verse 1-3: "Rabbin-Naas", "Malikin-Naas", "Ilaahin-Naas"**
    - These three verses establish a powerful foundation by describing Allah's relationship with humanity in three distinct roles, building a compelling case for why one should seek His refuge:
        - **"Rabb" (Lord/Sustainer):** He is the Creator, Provider, and Nurturer. He takes care of all our needs. If the One who sustains us is our protector, who can harm us?
        - **"Malik" (Sovereign/King):** He is the absolute Owner and Ruler of all mankind. A subject seeks refuge in their king from any enemy. We are Allah's subjects, and we seek His sovereign protection.
        - **"Ilaah" (God):** He alone is worthy of worship. Our entire devotion is for Him. We seek refuge in the One we worship from anything that would corrupt that worship.

2.  **Verse 4: "Min sharril-waswaasil-khan-naas"**
    - **"Al-Waswaas"** : The one who whispers constantly. This refers to Shaytaan (Satan), whose primary weapon against humanity is not physical force, but subtle suggestions and doubts cast into the mind.
    - **"Al-Khan-naas"** : The one who retreats or slinks away. The "Haashiyah" (footnote) provides a beautiful explanation of this: It is Shaytaan's habit that when a person is heedless (ghaafil) of Allah, he whispers evil suggestions into his heart. But the moment a person remembers Allah (does Dhikr), Shaytaan withdraws, retreats, and hides. He is a persistent attacker who flees at the mention of his opponent.

3.  **Verse 5: "Allazi yuwaswisu fee sudoorin-naas"**
    - This describes the target of the attack: **"Sudoor"** (chests/breasts/hearts). The whisperings are not external noises but internal doubts, evil suggestions, and temptations that arise within a person's heart and mind. These whispers can lead to hypocrisy, disbelief, sinful desires, and innovations in religion.

4.  **Verse 6: "Minal-jinnati wan-naas"**
    - This verse reveals the source of these whisperings. The "waswaas" (whisperer) is not only from the Jinn (Shayateen) but can also be from **among mankind itself**.
    - Human beings can also whisper evil into the hearts of others. A friend can whisper corrupt ideas, a propagandist can spread doubts about faith, and a tyrant can instill fear. The Surah teaches us that the spiritual disease of "waswasah" can come from both visible (insaan) and invisible (jinn) sources, and we must seek Allah's refuge from all of them.

**Summary (Unwaan):**
The titles given in the text accurately summarize the Surah's core message:
- **"Waswasah daalnay waalon kay shar say Panaah-e-Elahi talab karnay ki ta'leem"** : Teaching us to seek Divine refuge from the evil of those who whisper (evil suggestions).
- **"Jin aur insaan donon hi waswasay daaltay hayn"** : Both Jinn and mankind are sources of these whisperings.
