## What is Zohar?

- **Zohar (Dhuhr / ظهر)** is the **second prayer** of the five daily prayers.
- It is offered **after midday**, when the sun passes its highest point (Zenith).
- It replaces Jumu’ah prayer on Fridays for women and those not attending mosque.

---
## Time of Zohar
- **Starts:** Just after the sun passes its highest point (Zawal).
- **Ends:** When the shadow of an object becomes equal to its length  
    (Hanafi: shadow becomes twice its length).

---
## Rak‘ahs in Zohar

| Type                | Rak‘ahs | Status        |
| ------------------- | ------- | ------------- |
| Sunnah (Mu’akkadah) | 4       | Strong Sunnah |
| Fard                | 4       | Obligatory    |
| Sunnah (Mu’akkadah) | 2       | Strong Sunnah |
| Nafl (Optional)     | 2       | Optional      |

### Total = **12 Rak‘ahs** (6 Sunnah + 4 Fard + 2 Nafl)

Minimum required: **4 Fard**

---
## Importance of Zohar
- Midday prayer – reminds Muslims of Allah during busy hours.
- Very rewarding.
- The Prophet ﷺ was regular in praying 4 Sunnah before Zohar.

---
## Recitation
- Zohar is prayed **silently** (not aloud).
- Imam also recites silently in congregation.
