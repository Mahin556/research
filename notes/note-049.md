## Ibrahim's Father and Idolatry

Hazrat Ibrahim was born in Babylon. His father used to carve and sell idols, and he himself worshipped them. Ibrahim (AS) asked his father and his people, "What do you worship?" They replied, "Idols." He asked, "When you call upon them, do they hear you? Can they benefit or harm you?" The people said, "We don't know, but we found our forefathers doing the same."

Allah had guided Ibrahim (AS) from a young age, so he never worshipped idols even for a moment. He tried hard to advise his father, saying, "Father, I have received knowledge that you have not. Follow me, and I will guide you to the straight path. Do not worship Satan, for he is disobedient to Allah. I fear that Allah's punishment may seize you."

His father replied harshly, "O Ibrahim, have you turned away from my gods? If you do not stop, I will stone you. Leave me and never come near me again." Ibrahim (AS) then said, "Peace be upon you. I will pray to my Lord for your forgiveness." After his father's harsh response, Ibrahim distanced himself from him.

Ibrahim (AS) hated idol worship. On the day of their festival, his people cooked food, placed it before their idols, and then went to an open ground outside the city to play and celebrate. In the evening, they would return and eat the food they had left before the idols, considering it blessed.

This time, when the festival arrived, everyone prepared to go. Ibrahim (AS) did not. Before leaving, they asked him, "Won't you come with us?" He looked at the sky and said, "I am sick." They left without him.

Then Ibrahim (AS) went to the idols. He saw food placed before them, but the idols had not even touched it. He said, "Why don't you eat? What is wrong with you that you do not speak?" Then, with full force, he took an axe and broke all the small idols, leaving the largest one intact. He placed the axe on the shoulder of the big idol.

When the people returned, they were shocked. They gathered everyone and called Ibrahim (AS). They asked, "O Ibrahim, did you do this to our gods?" He replied, "Ask the big idol. If it can speak, it will tell you who broke them." They said, "You know they cannot speak." Then Ibrahim (AS) said, "Why do you worship besides Allah those who can neither benefit nor harm you? Shame on you and on what you worship instead of Allah. Do you have no sense?"
You workship  those you made yourself.
Despite understanding, they remained stubborn in their disbelief and decided to punish Ibrahim (AS).

## The Miracle of the Fire

The people gathered a huge amount of wood over many days and set it on fire. When the fire was blazing fiercely, they tied Ibrahim (AS) with ropes and threw him into the fire. At that moment, he prayed, *"Hasbun Allah wa ni'mal wakeel"* (Allah is sufficient for me, and He is the best Disposer of affairs).
![alt text](image.png)
Allah commanded the fire, "O fire, be cool and safe for Ibrahim." When Ibrahim (AS) was thrown in, the fire became cool. The fire did not burn his clothes or any part of his body; it only burned the ropes that had tied him. The people stood around expecting to see him burn, but instead, they saw him sitting gracefully in the middle of the fire. They were astonished. After witnessing this great miracle, only **Lut (AS)** believed. The rest of the people of Babylon remained in disbelief.
After this incedent idrahim was disappointed with them.

## King Nimrod

At that time, Babylon was ruled by a king named Nimrod. When the news of this miracle reached him, he summoned Ibrahim (AS) to his court. He asked Ibrahim about his Lord. Ibrahim (AS) replied, "My Lord is the One who gives life and causes death." Nimrod said, "I also give life and cause death." He ordered two prisoners brought from jail, killed one, and spared the other, saying, "See, by my command people die and live."

Then Ibrahim (AS) said, "Indeed, Allah brings the sun from the east. You bring it from the west." Nimrod had no answer. He then ordered Ibrahim (AS) to leave the city. Ibrahim left Iraq and traveled towards Syria (Shaam).

---

## Worshipers of Stars

In that city, the people did not worship idols but worshipped stars, performing their rituals at night. One night, Ibrahim (AS) went out with them. Seeing a bright star, he jokingly said, "This is my Lord." When it set, he said, "This cannot be my Lord, for it sets." The next night, seeing the moon, he said, "This is my Lord." When the moon set, he said, "If my Lord does not guide me, I will be lost." Then, seeing the sun rise, he said, "This is my Lord; this is the greatest." But when the sun also set, he declared, "These are all creations. Their Creator is Someone else. I worship the One who created the heavens and the earth." Still, none of them believed. So Ibrahim (AS) decided to migrate again and went to Egypt.

---

## The Pharaoh of Egypt and Sara (AS)

The king of Egypt was informed that a man had migrated to his land with a very beautiful woman. The king sent his men to bring her and to find out who the man was. If he was her husband, they were to kill him.

Allah sent revelation to Ibrahim (AS), telling him what to do. Ibrahim told his wife, Sara (AS), "If they ask about me, say that I am your brother." He explained, "On this earth, there is no believer besides you and me. So, you are my sister in faith."

The king summoned Sara (AS) to his palace. When he tried to harm her, she prayed to Allah, and the king's hand became frozen. He screamed in pain. Sara got scared, thinking that if he died, people would accuse her of killing him. She prayed again, and his hand was healed. The king tried again, and the same thing happened three times. Finally, he promised to release her. After she prayed again, he was healed. The king then freed Sara with honor and gave her his daughter, Hajra (Hagar), as a servant.

When Sara returned, Ibrahim (AS) said, "We cannot stay here." They went back to Palestine.

---

## Marriage with Hajra (AS)

Ibrahim (AS) was now 86 years old but had no children. Sara, knowing she was barren and that Ibrahim longed for offspring, suggested he marry Hajra (AS). So Ibrahim married Hajra. Allah accepted Ibrahim's prayer for children and blessed him with a son, Ismail (AS).

---

## Leaving Wife and Son Alone in Makkah

Allah commanded Ibrahim (AS) to take his wife Hajra and son Ismail to Makkah. At that time, Makkah was a completely barren, uninhabited land with no water, food, or people. As Ibrahim prepared to leave them there, Hajra asked in confusion, "O Ibrahim, where are you leaving us? There is nothing here." Ibrahim did not answer and kept walking. She followed him, repeatedly asking, "To whom are you entrusting us?" Finally, she asked, "Did Allah command you to do this?" Ibrahim said, "Yes." Then Hajra, full of faith, said, "If it is so, then Allah will not let us be lost(zaya)."

---

## Hajra Runs Between Safa and Marwa

Soon their food and water ran out. Even Hajra's milk dried up, and baby Ismail began crying from hunger. Unable to bear seeing her child suffer, she left him on the ground and ran to the nearby hill of Safa, hoping to see water or a person. Seeing nothing, she then ran to the hill of Marwah. She repeated this seven times between Safa and Marwah.

Allah loved this act of Hajra and made it a part of Hajj. That is why Muslims perform Sa'ee between Safa and Marwah during Hajj and Umrah.

---

## The Story of Zamzam

On her seventh time at Marwah, she heard a sound near Ismail. She rushed to her child and saw water gushing from beneath his feet. Allah had sent Jibreel (AS) to help her. He struck the ground near Ismail's feet with wings, and the blessed spring of Zamzam burst forth. This was the beginning of Zamzam water.

Hajra, amazed and overjoyed, began digging around the water to prevent it from spreading. She placed stones around it and said, "Zamzam" (stop, stop). The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said, "May Allah have mercy on Ismail's mother. If she had not acted hastily, Zamzam would have been a flowing spring."

This water became their source of food and drink. Soon, a migrating tribe searching for water arrived. They asked Hajra's permission to camp there. She agreed on the condition that they pay rent. They readily agreed, and so settlement began in Makkah. This was the result of Ibrahim's prayer when he had left his family there: "O our Lord, I have settled some of my offspring in a barren valley near Your Sacred House so that they may establish prayer. Make the hearts of people incline toward them and provide them with fruits."

Ibrahim would visit his family once or twice a year. When Ismail grew up, another trial came.

---

## The Sacrifice of Ismail – Qurbani

Ibrahim (AS) saw a dream in which he was sacrificing his son Ismail. He knew that the dreams of prophets are revelation from Allah. He told Ismail, "My dear son, I have seen in a dream that I am slaughtering you. What do you think?" Ismail replied, "O my father, do as you are commanded. You will find me, if Allah wills, among the patient."

Both father and son submitted to Allah's command. Ismail said, "When you lay me down, place my forehead on the ground so you don't see my face, and tie your hands so your heart does not soften. Blindfold yourself." Ibrahim did so. He took the knife and drew it across Ismail's neck, but the knife did not cut. He tried again and heard the sound of a sheep.

He removed his blindfold and saw that a ram was sacrificed, and Ismail was standing nearby, smiling. Allah had tested Ibrahim, and he had passed. Ibrahim was ready to sacrifice his only son, given to him at 86 after a lifetime of childlessness. Allah did not take his son but sent Jibreel with a beautiful ram from Paradise to be sacrificed instead. Since then, Allah has kept this tradition alive, and Muslims around the world sacrifice animals during Hajj to honor Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice.

---

## Birth of Ishaq (AS) in Old Age

As a reward for Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son, and for Sara's virtue, Allah gave them the good news of another son: Ishaq (Isaac). Ibrahim was then 120 years old, and Sara was 90. Both were advanced in age. Angels came to give the glad tidings. Sara was astonished, saying, "Shall I bear a child while I am barren and my husband is old?" The angels replied, "Do not be surprised at Allah's decree. This is Allah's mercy upon you."

---

## Construction of the Kaaba by Ibrahim and Ismail

Ibrahim (AS) would visit Ismail in Makkah. On one occasion, he said, "O my son, Allah has commanded me to build His House at this place." Ismail replied, "I am ready." Together, they raised the foundations of the Kaaba. While building, they prayed, "Our Lord, accept from us. You are the All-Hearing, All-Knowing. Make us both submissive to You, and from our descendants raise a community that submits to You. Show us our rites of worship and accept our repentance. Our Lord, send among them a Messenger from themselves who will recite Your verses to them, teach them the Book and wisdom, and purify them."

![alt text](image-2.png)

During construction, Allah sent a stone from Paradise called the **Hajr-e-Aswad** (Black Stone). It was originally pure white but has turned black due to people's sins. Ibrahim (AS) stood on a stone while building; he was barefoot, and the stone melted under his feet, leaving his footprints. That stone is known as **Maqam-e-Ibrahim** and can still be seen today.

---

## Hajj

After the Kaaba was built, Allah commanded Ibrahim (AS) to proclaim the Hajj to the people. Ibrahim asked, "O Allah, my voice will not reach far." Allah replied, "It is your duty to call; it is Ours to convey." Ibrahim climbed **Mount Arafat** and called out, "O people, perform Hajj to the Sacred House." Allah placed love for the Kaaba in people's hearts, and millions have come every year since to perform Hajj and Umrah.

Ibrahim (AS) passed away at the age of 175. Many trials came upon him, but in every matter, he remained faithful to Allah. For this, Allah honored him with the title **Khalil-ur-Rahman** – the Friend of Allah.
