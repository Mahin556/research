# Isra wal Miraj — The Night Journey & Ascension to the Heavens

---

## 📍 Part 1: The Context — Why This Journey Happened When It Did

### The Darkest Period of the Prophet's ﷺ Life
This miraculous journey happened at the **absolute lowest point** of the Prophet's ﷺ life:

| Trial | What Happened |
|-------|--------------|
| **3-year boycott** | Entire family ate leaves and leather for 3 years |
| **Abu Talib dies** | Lost his tribal protector — now vulnerable to anyone |
| **Khadijah ؓ dies** | Lost his emotional anchor and first believer |
| **Ta'if** | Stoned until sandals filled with blood |
| **Citizenship revoked** | Abu Lahab stripped him of Meccan citizenship |
| **Living under protection** | Had to beg a non-Muslim for shelter in his own birthplace |

- This journey happened in the **11th year of Prophethood** — exactly the midpoint of his prophetic mission
- It was the **turning point** of his entire life:
  - Everything **before** it = suffering, rejection, persecution
  - Everything **after** it = Hijra to Medina, a new state, eventual dominance over all of Arabia

---

## 🌙 Part 2: The Night Begins — Isra (The Night Journey)

### From Mecca to Jerusalem
- One night in Mecca, at **midnight**, Jibreel ؑ (Gabriel) came to the Prophet ﷺ
- He brought the **Buraq** — a miraculous creature of transport

### What Was the Buraq?
- The word **Buraq** comes from **"Barq"** — meaning **lightning**
- It was likely named for its **lightning-like speed**
- The Prophet ﷺ rode the Buraq from **Mecca to Jerusalem (Bayt al-Maqdis)** in an instant
- A journey that would normally take **months** — done in moments

### Leading All the Prophets in Prayer
- Upon arriving at Bayt al-Maqdis (the Al-Aqsa Mosque area), something extraordinary happened
- The Prophet ﷺ found **all the Prophets assembled** there
- He **led them all in prayer** as their Imam
- This symbolized that he was the **final and seal of all the prophets** — their leader

---

## 🌟 Part 3: The Miraj — Ascending Through the Seven Heavens

### The Ascent Begins
- From Jerusalem, Jibreel ؑ accompanied the Prophet ﷺ upward
- They reached the **door of the first heaven**
- The door opened for them — described as unlike any door we know

---

### 🌠 First Heaven — Meeting Adam ؑ

**Who is Adam ؑ?**
- The **first human being** — father of all humanity
- Every single person alive today is his descendant
- Mentioned **25 times** in the Quran

**What the Prophet ﷺ Saw:**
- Adam ؑ standing with:
  - The people of **Paradise on his right side**
  - The people of **Hell on his left side**
- When he looked right — he smiled
- When he looked left — he wept

The Prophet ﷺ greeted him → Adam ؑ replied and welcomed him as *"the righteous son and righteous Prophet"*

---

### 🌠 Second Heaven — Meeting Isa ؑ and Yahya ؑ

**Who are they?**
- **Isa ؑ** = Jesus Christ in Christianity
- **Yahya ؑ** = John the Baptist in Christianity
- They were **cousins** — born about 2,000 years ago in Roman-controlled Palestine
- Both lived extremely difficult lives:
  - Yahya ؑ was **martyred by a tyrant ruler** — his martyrdom is often compared to the martyrdom of Imam Husain at Karbala
  - Isa ؑ was about to be killed too — but Allah raised him up before that could happen
- Yahya ؑ mentioned **5 times** in the Quran; Isa ؑ mentioned **25 times**

The Prophet ﷺ greeted them → They replied and welcomed him as *"the righteous brother and righteous Prophet"*

---

### 🌠 Third Heaven — Meeting Yusuf ؑ

**Who is Yusuf ؑ?**
- Born approximately **3,600 years ago** in Palestine
- Thrown into a well by his own brothers, sold into slavery
- Rose from slave to become **Vizier of Egypt** — the second most powerful man in the world's superpower
- An equivalent today: Imagine someone going from homeless refugee to **Prime Minister of the USA**
- Mentioned **27 times** in the Quran — the **only Prophet whose complete detailed story** is told in the Quran
- His story has **more detail in the Quran than in the Bible**
- His story has inspired billions — the narrator notes that **Superman, Harry Potter, and many Hollywood blockbusters** follow story structures inspired by Yusuf ؑ

The Prophet ﷺ greeted him → Yusuf ؑ replied and welcomed him

---

### 🌠 Fourth Heaven — Meeting Idris ؑ

**Who is Idris ؑ?**
- One of the earliest human beings — came after Adam ؑ and Shees ؑ (Seth)
- Considered the **third Prophet** of humanity
- Remarkable achievements:
  - First person to **fight against oppression** (first jihad against injustice)
  - Considered the **world's first scientist**
  - First person to **use a pen** for writing
  - First to **observe stars and planets** and use astronomy to benefit humanity
- Mentioned **twice** in the Quran

The Prophet ﷺ greeted him → Idris ؑ replied and welcomed him

---

### 🌠 Fifth Heaven — Meeting Harun ؑ

**Who is Harun ؑ?**
- Brother of Musa ؑ (Moses) — Aaron in the Bible
- When Musa ؑ became a Prophet, he made a special prayer:
  > *"O Allah — include my brother Harun in my mission"*
- Allah answered this prayer and made Harun ؑ a Prophet too
- Why did Musa ؑ specifically choose Harun ؑ?
  - Musa ؑ had grown up in **Pharaoh's palace** — so he didn't know the scriptures of Banu Israel deeply
  - Harun ؑ was the **greatest scholar** of Banu Israel's scriptures and traditions
  - Musa ؑ needed someone with deep knowledge — Harun ؑ was that person
- Legacy: **Even today, the majority of Jewish scholars** are from the lineage of Harun ؑ — giving his descendants a special honored status in Judaism (similar to how Sayyids are honored in Islam)
- Mentioned **20 times** in the Quran

The Prophet ﷺ greeted him → Harun ؑ replied and welcomed him

---

### 🌠 Sixth Heaven — Meeting Musa ؑ

**Who is Musa ؑ?**
- **Most mentioned Prophet** in the entire Quran — **136 times**
- His story is considered **the most interesting and extraordinary** in all of human history
- Even today — books, comics, and Hollywood's biggest films are inspired by his story:
  - Superman (baby sent away to be raised elsewhere, returns as savior)
  - Harry Potter (chosen one, hidden from enemies)
  - The Prince of Egypt, The Ten Commandments
  - These stories have collectively earned **billions of dollars**

**The Background of His Story:**
- When Yusuf ؑ became Prime Minister of Egypt, he brought his 11 brothers to Egypt
- Over generations, Banu Israel (Children of Israel / Jews) became the **strongest and wealthiest community in Egypt**
- Egypt grew **afraid** of them and enslaved them
- **400 years of slavery** under Pharaoh (Fir'awn)
- Finally, Allah sent **Musa ؑ** to free them from Pharaoh's tyranny

**At the Sixth Heaven:**
- The Prophet ﷺ greeted Musa ؑ — he replied and welcomed him
- Musa ؑ **wept** when the Prophet ﷺ passed him on the way back
- Someone asked why he was crying — he said:
  > *"A young man was sent after me, and more of his followers will enter Paradise than mine"*

---

### 🌠 Seventh Heaven — Meeting Ibrahim ؑ

**Who is Ibrahim ؑ?**
- The Prophet's ﷺ **great-great-great...grandfather** — direct ancestor
- The **father of all three Abrahamic religions** — Islam, Christianity, Judaism
- Today, **60% of the entire world's population** follows one of the Abrahamic faiths
- In every single prayer (Salah), Muslims recite a **special blessing on Ibrahim ؑ** — the Durud Ibrahim
- The single most influential human being in the history of religion

The Prophet ﷺ greeted him → Ibrahim ؑ replied warmly and welcomed him

**What the Prophet ﷺ saw:**
- Ibrahim ؑ was seated with his **back against Bayt al-Ma'mur** — the heavenly Kaaba directly above the earthly Kaaba
- Bayt al-Ma'mur: **70,000 new angels perform Tawaf around it every single day** — and the same angels never get to return again (so vast is the number of angels)

---

## 🌌 Part 4: Sidrat al-Muntaha — The Final Boundary

### The Last Tree
- Beyond the seventh heaven is **Sidrat al-Muntaha** — the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary
- This is the **absolute final limit of all creation** — nothing created can pass beyond it
- Even Jibreel ؑ — the greatest of all angels — **cannot go beyond this point**

### The Prophet ﷺ Alone
- Jibreel ؑ stopped here
- The Prophet ﷺ **continued alone** — beyond where any angel had ever gone
- He entered the **direct presence of Allah**

### Did He See Allah?
- After returning, companions asked: *"Did you see Allah?"*
- The Prophet ﷺ replied:
  > *"How could I see Him? There was a veil of pure light (Noor) between us"*

---

## 🕌 Part 5: The Greatest Gift — The Five Daily Prayers

### The True Purpose of Miraj
- This entire extraordinary journey had one central, practical purpose
- At this highest point, in the direct presence of Allah — **Salah (prayer) was made obligatory** on Muslims

### Why Salah Wasn't Made Obligatory Earlier
- In Mecca, building mosques was **impossible** — the Muslims were persecuted
- Salah was given at Miraj because **immediately after** came the Hijra to Medina
- In Medina, the **first mosque in Islam** was built
- Salah then became the **foundation of Muslim community** in Medina

### The Negotiation — From 50 to 5

This is one of the most beloved accounts in all of Islamic tradition:

| Round | Prayers Required | Who Advised to Go Back | Result |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|--------|
| Start | **50 prayers daily** | Musa ؑ: *"Your people can't do 50"* | Goes back to Allah |
| Round 2 | Reduced to **40** | Musa ؑ: *"Still impossible"* | Goes back |
| Round 3 | Reduced to **30** | Musa ؑ: *"Still too many"* | Goes back |
| Round 4 | Reduced to **20** | Musa ؑ: *"Still too many"* | Goes back |
| Round 5 | Reduced to **10** | Musa ؑ: *"Still too many"* | Goes back |
| Final | **5 prayers** | Musa ؑ: *"Even 5 is too many for your people"* | Prophet ﷺ stops |

The Prophet ﷺ said:
> *"After this, I feel too embarrassed to go back and ask for further reduction. I accept 5 prayers."*

Allah then declared:
> *"These are 5 prayers — but they carry the reward of 50. My word does not change."*

**Why is Musa's ؑ role here so remarkable?**
- Musa ؑ had experienced leading a **difficult community** for 40 years in the desert
- He knew from personal experience how hard it was to get people to pray
- His advice came from **genuine concern for the Muslim community**

## 🌅 Part 6: The Return — Testing Everything

### What the Prophet ﷺ Saw on the Way Back
- Paradise and Hell — shown to him
- The punishments of various sinners — witnessed
- On the way back to Jerusalem, he passed a **caravan** — one of whose camels had escaped

### Back in Mecca — The Morning After
- The entire journey — Mecca to Jerusalem to seven heavens and back — happened in **one single night**
- When the Prophet ﷺ returned, **everyone in Mecca was still asleep**

### Einstein's Theory of Relativity — A Modern Parallel
- The narrator connects this to **Einstein's Theory of Relativity**:
  > *"Time is not absolute. If someone is moving very fast and another is standing still — they experience time completely differently. If someone travels near the speed of light, time passes extremely slowly for them."*
- Buraq's very **name comes from lightning**
- For the Prophet ﷺ traveling on Buraq — **that night was very long**
- For everyone in Mecca — **it was just a normal night**

---

## 😮 Part 7: The Reaction in Mecca — Belief and Disbelief

### The Prophet ﷺ Tells Everyone
- Despite knowing the Meccans would mock him, the Prophet ﷺ **told the complete story publicly**
- The Meccans immediately began mocking and questioning:
  > *"You claim to have gone to Jerusalem — a journey of months — in one night? We know you've never been there before. Describe it!"*

### Allah's Support — Perfect Memory
- At that moment, **Allah placed Bayt al-Maqdis visually before the Prophet ﷺ**
- It was as if he was looking at a **live image** of Jerusalem right then
- He described every detail — the Meccans had no answer
- He also described the **lost camel in the caravan** — and that caravan arrived shortly after with the same details he had mentioned

### The Consequences

**Those who disbelieved:**
- Some **weak-faithed Muslims** left Islam over this — they could not accept something so beyond ordinary comprehension
- Even **Mut'im bin Adi** — the man who had sheltered the Prophet ﷺ — refused to believe

**The Defining Moment of Abu Bakr ؓ**
- When Abu Bakr ؓ was told about the night journey, he said:
  > *"If the Prophet ﷺ said it — then it is absolutely, certainly true."*
- He didn't ask for proof. He didn't ask for explanation.
- **Pure, unquestioning faith.**
- From that day, his title became **"Abu Bakr as-Siddiq"** — Siddiq meaning **"The Truthful Confirmer / The One Who Believes Completely"**
- This is the title by which he is known for all of history

## 🌅 Part 8: The Dawn After the Darkness

### What Changed After Miraj
- The **hardest period of Islam was now ending**
- The very next year after Miraj:
  - **72 people from Medina** traveled to Mecca
  - They pledged allegiance to the Prophet ﷺ
  - They invited him to **come to Medina as their leader and king**
  - This became the **Second Pledge of Aqaba** — the formal invitation that led to the Hijra
- The era of persecution was over
- The era of the **Islamic State** was about to begin
