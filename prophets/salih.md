# The Story of Prophet Salih ؑ and the People of Thamud — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: Who Were the Thamud?

### Their Origins
- The Thamud came **after the destruction of Aad**
- They lived in the region of **Al-Hijr** — in the northwestern Arabian Peninsula (modern-day Saudi Arabia, near Madain Salih)
- Like Aad before them, they were an extraordinarily powerful and skilled civilization

### Their Achievements
- They were **exceptional craftsmen and builders**
- Like their predecessors (Aad), they **carved buildings and palaces directly into mountains**
- They lived in **beautiful homes** hewn from rock
- Their land was **extremely fertile** — producing abundant crops and harvests
- They were **hardworking and prosperous**

### Their Downfall Began With Prosperity
- As soon as their material conditions improved — **Shaytan's influence took over**
- They became **ungrateful and arrogant**
- Turned away from Allah's worship
- The **wealthy became oppressors**
- They abandoned worship of Allah and turned to **idol worship**
- A large stone/rock on a mountain became their central object of worship

---

## 🕌 Part 2: Prophet Salih ؑ — Allah's Messenger to Thamud

### Who Was Salih ؑ?
- Salih ؑ was a **native of Thamud** — one of their own people
- He was known as **a wise, good, and intelligent man**
- People respected him and held him in high regard
- Because of his exceptional qualities, people had expected him to one day become their community leader

### His Message
When Salih ؑ began his prophethood, he called his people:
> *"Worship Allah alone — there is no master except Him"*

### Their Reaction
The community was shocked and upset:
> *"We honored you Salih — we thought you would become our leader one day. How can you tell us to abandon our gods? How can we forget the deities our ancestors worshipped? We do not believe in Allah. Leave!"*

And so the struggle between good and evil began.

---

## 🗿 Part 3: The Mountain Worship — What Salih ؑ Witnessed

### The Scene at the Rock
- One day, the community gathered near a **large rock on a mountain**
- They worshipped the stone for a long time — bowing, praying, offering sacrifices
- When their children saw them, **the children also started doing the same**
- They offered a **sheep as sacrifice** to the stone
- They prayed to it

### Salih's ؑ Response
Salih ؑ was deeply saddened:
> *"Offer your sacrifice to Allah — He alone is your Master"*

One of them challenged:
> *"Why do you only tell us to worship Allah?"*

Salih ؑ replied:
> *"Because He created you. He gave you everything — the sun, the moon, the mountains, the oceans — everything you see around you"*

---

## 🐪 Part 4: The Miracle — The She-Camel from the Rock

### Their Demand for a Miracle
- A man stepped forward and said:
  > *"Show us a miracle — then we will believe you"*
- Another pointed at the large rock they worshipped:
  > *"Tell your Allah to bring a she-camel out from this rock"*
- A third added:
  > *"And she must be pregnant"*
- They laughed mockingly

### Salih's ؑ Condition
- Salih ؑ thought carefully and replied:
  > *"If Allah performs this miracle — do you promise you will believe in Allah and acknowledge He is the true Master? Will you accept that I am His Prophet?"*
- They replied: **"Yes"**
- Salih ؑ prayed to Allah

### The Miracle Happens
- First — the people heard the **sound of the rock cracking and splitting**
- Suddenly — the rock broke apart
- And there stood **a beautiful she-camel** — large, magnificent, and **pregnant**

### The Divided Reaction
- Those who witnessed it were **completely stunned** — they couldn't believe their eyes
- **Some accepted** — they recognized this as truth and believed
- **Others — especially the wealthy and powerful** — called it magic and trickery
- Salih ؑ prostrated before the she-camel in reverence of Allah's power
- Others who had witnessed the miracle also prostrated with him — their faith confirmed

---

## 🌿 Part 5: Life With the She-Camel

### The Miracle Continues
- **Three days after the miracle** — the she-camel gave birth to a beautiful calf
- The calf always walked closely behind its mother
- The mother looked at her calf with tenderness and love
- Together they became **symbols of mercy and gentleness**

### The Community's Fascination
- When people saw them, they would say:
  > *"Look — there goes the Prophet Salih's camel"*
- Salih ؑ told the people:
  > *"Worship Allah alone — He has no partner. This is a sign from Allah — let her graze freely on Allah's Earth. Do not harm her. If you do — Allah will punish you severely"*

### The Water Sharing Arrangement
- The she-camel was enormous and drank enormous quantities of water
- When she came to the well to drink — **no animal or person could approach until she was done**
- The people came to Salih ؑ:
  > *"The camel's milk could be a blessing — her calf cannot drink it all. Allow us to use some of it"*
- Salih ؑ agreed with conditions:
  - **One day**: People could use her milk while she drank from the well
  - **Next day**: Leave the milk for her calf
- They agreed and the arrangement worked
- The she-camel provided **enormous quantities of milk** — enough for the entire community's needs
- At first people were pleased

---

## 😈 Part 6: The Plot to Kill the She-Camel

### Growing Hostility
- The disbelievers began opposing Salih ؑ more aggressively
- They hated that his message was drawing people away from idol worship
- They extended their hostility to the she-camel — seeing her as the symbol of Salih's ؑ prophethood
- The community **split into two groups**:
  1. **Muslims** — those who believed in Allah and His Prophet
  2. **Disbelievers** — those who rejected the truth

### The Mocking of Believers
Disbelievers taunted the believers:
> *"Do you really think Salih is Allah's Prophet?"*
> *"Do you really believe Allah is the true Master?"*

The believers replied firmly:
> *"Yes — we believe in Allah and in His Prophet"*

### The Conspiracy
- One night, the wealthy and powerful disbelievers **gathered secretly**
- They planned the murder of the she-camel
- They needed **nine strong men** for the task
- Their wives helped persuade these men — women convinced nine men to participate in the crime

### The Brutal Murder
- The next day, the nine men tracked the she-camel's movements
- They studied her daily routine
- When she came to the well to drink — one man shot her leg with an arrow
- She screamed in pain and tried to run — but the arrow in her leg prevented escape
- Another cruel man chased her and struck her other leg with a sword
- All nine men repeatedly struck her with their swords
- The innocent she-camel was murdered

### Their Reaction — Celebrating Evil
- The murderers were welcomed as **heroes**
- Songs were sung in their honor
- The disbelievers rejoiced
- They had destroyed Allah's sign — and celebrated it

---

## 😢 Part 7: Salih's ؑ Warning and Final Appeal

### Salih's ؑ Grief
- When Salih ؑ saw the dead body of the she-camel, his heart was deeply pained
- Yet he remained composed
- He told them:
  > *"Enjoy your lives for three more days. Then Allah will punish you"*

### Their Arrogant Response
> *"Three days? Why three days? Bring the punishment as soon as possible!"*
- They danced and mocked
- They had no understanding of what was coming

### One Last Attempt
Salih ؑ tried once more:
> *"Why are you walking on Shaytan's path? Ask Allah for forgiveness — perhaps He will have mercy on you"*

They refused to listen. They continued dancing.

### The Plot to Kill Salih ؑ
- Not satisfied with killing the she-camel, the evil men now planned to **kill Salih ؑ himself**
- But Allah protected Salih ؑ and his believers from this conspiracy
- With a grieving heart, Salih ؑ left the land of the evildoers
- His believers left with him — departing before the punishment arrived

---

## ⚡ Part 8: The Punishment — Three Days of Warning, Then Destruction

### The Signs Before Punishment
- The Quran and Islamic narrations mention a sequence:
  - **First day**: Their faces turned red
  - **Second day**: Their faces turned yellow
  - **Third day**: Their faces turned black
- These were signs — yet they refused to repent

### The Final Punishment
- On the appointed day, the disbelievers were sitting **confidently in their stone houses** — feeling completely secure
- Suddenly:
  - **Lightning began striking** from the sky
  - A **terrifying earthquake** shook the earth
  - The ground trembled violently

### The Result
- Their animals died
- The people died
- **Neither their massive buildings saved them**
- **Nor their stone houses protected them**
- Everything was destroyed in an instant — before they could even understand what was happening
- Only those who had believed in Allah — and had already left — were saved

---

## 🌍 Part 9: The Archaeological Evidence — Madain Salih

### What Remains Today
- The ruins of the Thamud civilization still exist today in **Madain Salih** (also called Al-Hijr) in northwestern Saudi Arabia
- This site is now a **UNESCO World Heritage Site**
- Visitors can still see:
  - Massive rock-carved structures
  - Tombs and buildings carved directly into sandstone mountains
  - Remnants of what was once a mighty civilization
- The site stands as a **permanent physical witness** to the story told in the Quran

### The Quran References to Thamud
The Quran mentions Thamud in multiple chapters:
- **Surah Al-A'raf (7:73-79)**
- **Surah Hud (11:61-68)**
- **Surah Al-Hijr (15:80-84)**
- **Surah Al-Qamar (54:23-31)**
- **Surah Al-Haqqah (69:4-5)**
- **Surah Al-Fajr (89:9)**
- **Surah Al-Shams (91:11-15)**
