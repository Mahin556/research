# The Life of Prophet Yaqub (Jacob) (AS) — Detailed Notes

---

## Part 1: Introduction

### The System of Divine Guidance
- Allah Almighty sent approximately 124,000 Prophets (AS) for the guidance of humanity
- Among these noble figures is Prophet Yaqub (Jacob) (AS)
- His name is Yaqub, and his title (laqab) is Israel (Isra'il)

### His Blessed Lineage
- Prophet Yaqub (AS) holds a unique distinction in the line of Prophethood:
  - He is the son of Prophet Ishaq (Isaac) (AS)
  - He is the grandson of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) (AS) — the Friend of Allah (Khalilullah)
  - He is the father of Prophet Yusuf (Joseph) (AS)
- This means:
  - His grandfather was a Prophet
  - His father was a Prophet
  - He himself was a Prophet
  - His son was a Prophet
- This is a four-generation chain of Prophethood — an honor rarely granted in human history

### The Glad Tidings Given to Ibrahim (AS)
- Allah Almighty gave glad tidings of Yaqub's (AS) birth to Ibrahim (AS) even before Ishaq's (AS) birth
- The Quran states in Surah Hud (11:71):
  - "Then We gave her (Sarah) glad tidings of Ishaq, and after Ishaq, of Yaqub."
- In Surah Al-An'am (6:84), Allah says:
  - "And We granted Ibrahim (AS) a son like Ishaq and a grandson like Yaqub, and We gave guidance to all of them."

---

## Part 2: The Meaning of Israel (Isra'il)

### The Two Names
- Prophet Yaqub (AS) had two names:
  1. **Yaqub** — his given name from birth
  2. **Israel (Isra'il)** — his divinely granted title

### The Etymology of Israel
- "Isra'il" is a combination of two words:
  - **"Isra"** — means "servant" (Abd) or "one who strives"
  - **"Il"** — means "Allah" or "God"
- Thus, "Israel" means:
  - "The Servant of Allah"
  - "Abdullah" (in Arabic)
  - "One who strives with/for Allah"

### The Significance
- The title "Israel" was given to Yaqub (AS) by Allah Himself
- This name became the identifier for all his descendants
- His progeny are called "Bani Israel" (The Children of Israel)
- The Quran frequently addresses them with this name

---

## Part 3: The Birth and Early Life of Yaqub (AS)

### The Twin Sons of Ishaq (AS)
- Prophet Ishaq (AS) had twin sons:
  - **Ees (Esau)** — the elder, also called "Eesau" or "Mahis"
  - **Yaqub (Jacob)** — the younger

### Parental Affection
- Ishaq (AS) loved his elder son Ees very much — with special affection
- His wife, Rebekah (Rifqa) (AS), loved the younger son Yaqub (AS) more
- This difference in parental affection would lead to significant events

---

## Part 4: The Blessing Incident

### Ishaq's (AS) Craving
- One day, Prophet Ishaq (AS) expressed a strong desire to eat game meat to his elder son Ees
- Ees, ever the dutiful and enthusiastic hunter, immediately set out to hunt game for his father

### The Mother's Intervention
- While Ees was away hunting, Rebekah (AS) — the mother — saw an opportunity
- She approached Yaqub (AS) and said:
  - "Slaughter a goat and present the prepared food to your father."
  - "Receive his prayers and blessings before your brother returns."

### Yaqub (AS) Follows His Mother's Advice
- Yaqub (AS) followed his mother's counsel
- He slaughtered a goat, prepared the meat exactly as his father liked it, and brought it to Ishaq (AS)
- Ishaq (AS), being elderly and having weakened eyesight, believed it was Ees who had brought the food
- He ate the food and gave Yaqub (AS) his special, heartfelt prayers and blessings

### The Elder Brother's Reaction
- Ees returned with the hunted game meat, roasted and prepared
- When he presented it to his father, Ishaq (AS) was surprised and said:
  - "I have already eaten and have given my blessings."
- Ees realized what had happened
- He became furious and deeply resentful toward his younger brother Yaqub (AS)
- The jealousy and anger were so intense that Ees became Yaqub's (AS) enemy
- He harbored intentions of harming or even killing Yaqub (AS)

---

## Part 5: The Flight to Harran

### The Mother's Concern
- When Rebekah (AS) saw Ees's intense anger and hatred, she feared for Yaqub's (AS) life
- She immediately told Yaqub (AS):
  - "You must leave at once."
  - "Go to your maternal uncle Laban (Laban) in Harran (a city in Iraq)."
  - "Stay there until your brother's anger subsides."
  - "I fear that Ees may kill you in his rage."

### Ishaq's (AS) Agreement
- Ishaq (AS) was also aware of Ees's extreme displeasure
- He agreed with the advice that Yaqub (AS) should seek refuge with his uncle
- He gave his blessing for the journey

### The Departure
- Yaqub (AS) set out on the very first part of that night
- He left his home, his parents, and everything familiar
- He was a young man, embarking on a long and uncertain journey alone
- He walked through the night and continued through the next day

---

## Part 6: The Dream at Bethel — The Ladder to Heaven

### The Night in the Wilderness
- After traveling for a full day and into the night, Yaqub (AS) was exhausted
- He stopped near a village, in the open countryside
- He found a stone and placed it under his head as a pillow
- He lay down on the bare ground and fell into a deep sleep

### The Magnificent Vision
- In his dream, Yaqub (AS) witnessed an extraordinary sight:
  - A ladder (or staircase) stretching from the earth all the way up to the heavens (the sky/the divine realm)
  - Angels were ascending and descending upon this ladder continuously
  - It was a connection between the earth and the heavens

### The Divine Address
- During this vision, Allah Almighty spoke directly to Yaqub (AS)
- Allah said:
  - "I will soon bless you abundantly."
  - "I will make your descendants numerous and widespread."
  - "I will give this land to you."
  - "And after you, I will give it to your descendants as well."

### The Joy Upon Awakening
- When Yaqub (AS) woke from his sleep, he was overwhelmed with immense joy
- He had received a direct promise from the Lord of the Worlds
- A lonely fugitive had been given a divine covenant

### The Two Vows
- In his state of gratitude, Yaqub (AS) made two solemn vows (mannat) to Allah:
  1. **First Vow:** If he returned to his parents safely, he would build a house of worship dedicated to Allah Almighty at the very spot where he had seen the dream
  2. **Second Vow:** Whatever Allah granted him in sustenance and wealth, he would spend one-tenth (a tithe — daswa hissa) of it in the path of Allah

### The Memorial Stone
- Yaqub (AS) anointed the stone — which he had used as a pillow — with oil
- He did this so that when he returned in the future, he could identify the exact spot by recognizing the stone
- This was a marker of the sacred location

---

## Part 7: Arrival in Harran — Life with Laban

### Reaching Harran
- After a long and arduous journey, Yaqub (AS) finally arrived in Harran
- He located his maternal uncle Laban

### The Reunion
- Laban was overjoyed to see his nephew
- He welcomed Yaqub (AS) with great warmth and hospitality
- He took him into his home and showed him great kindness

### Laban's Daughters
- Laban had two young, unmarried daughters:
  - **Leah (Lia)** — the elder daughter
  - **Rachel (Rahil)** — the younger daughter

### The Marriage Proposal
- Yaqub (AS) developed a desire to marry his cousin Rachel (Rahil), the younger daughter
- He approached his uncle Laban and formally requested Rachel's hand in marriage

### The Contract — Seven Years
- Laban agreed — but with a condition:
  - "You must work for me, grazing my sheep, for six years." (Note: some narrations say seven)
- Yaqub (AS) accepted the condition
- He worked diligently as a shepherd for his uncle for the agreed period

### The Deception
- When the term was completed and Yaqub (AS) had fulfilled his part of the agreement:
  - Laban gathered the community and arranged a wedding feast
  - The marriage ceremony was performed
- However, during the night — under the cover of darkness and with the bride veiled — a deception occurred
- In the morning, Yaqub (AS) discovered the truth:
  - He had been married not to Rachel, but to her elder sister, Leah

### The Confrontation
- Yaqub (AS) went to his uncle Laban and asked:
  - "Why have you done this to me?"
  - "I had proposed marriage to Rachel, not Leah."

### Laban's Justification
- Laban explained:
  - "In our tradition and customs, the younger daughter cannot be married before the elder daughter."
  - "Therefore, I had to marry Leah to you first."
- However, Laban offered a solution:
  - "If you still wish to marry Rachel, I will also marry her to you."
  - "But you must work for me for another seven years, grazing the sheep."

### The Second Contract
- Yaqub (AS) agreed
- He worked for an additional seven years
- After completing this second term, he was married to Rachel (Rahil) as well

### The Permissibility at That Time
- It is important to note that in the divine law of that era, it was permissible for a man to be married to two sisters simultaneously
- This law was specific to that time and was later abrogated in the Shariah of subsequent Prophets

---

## Part 8: The Family of Yaqub (AS)

### The Wives and Children
- Historical sources state that Yaqub (AS) had four wives:
  1. Leah (Lia)
  2. Rachel (Rahil)
  3. Zilpah (Zilfa) — the handmaid of Leah
  4. Bilhah (Bilha) — the handmaid of Rachel

### The Twelve Sons and One Daughter
- Allah Almighty blessed Yaqub (AS) with abundant offspring
- He had **12 sons** and **1 daughter**

### The Sons from Each Wife:

**From Leah:**
1. Reuben (Rubeel)
2. Simeon (Sham'un)
3. Levi (Lawi)
4. Judah (Yahuda)
5. Issachar (Yashakar)
6. Zebulun (Zaybulun)

**From Zilpah (Leah's handmaid):**
7. Gad (Jad)
8. Asher (Ashir)

**From Bilhah (Rachel's handmaid):**
9. Dan
10. Naphtali (Naftali)

**From Rachel (Rahil):**
11. Yusuf (Joseph) (AS)
12. Benjamin (Bin Yamin)

### The Twelve Tribes
- These twelve sons grew up, got married, and had children of their own
- Their descendants multiplied and spread
- Over generations, they formed **12 distinct tribes**
- These became known as **"The Twelve Tribes of Israel"** (Bani Israel)
- Each tribe traced its lineage back to one of the sons of Yaqub (AS)

---

## Part 9: The Return to Canaan

### The Divine Command
- Yaqub (AS) lived in Harran, working for his uncle, for a total of **20 years**
- After this period, he received a command from Allah to return to the land of Canaan (Kan'an)

### Preparations for Departure
- Yaqub (AS) began preparing for the journey back
- He gathered his wives, children, and all his belongings
- He also had substantial flocks of sheep and goats that he had earned during his years of service

### Laban's Sadness
- Laban was deeply saddened by the prospect of separation from his daughters and grandchildren
- He tried to persuade Yaqub (AS) to stay
- However, Yaqub (AS) was bound by the command of Allah and could not remain

### The Send-off
- Unable to change Yaqub's (AS) mind, Laban:
  - Gave him large flocks of sheep and goats as a gift
  - Provided provisions for the journey
  - Bid farewell to his daughters, grandchildren, and nephew with a heavy heart

### The Journey
- Yaqub (AS) set out with his entire household, wealth, and livestock
- They traveled from Harran toward Canaan

---

## Part 10: The Reunion with Esau (Ees)

### Arrival at Esau's Territory
- The caravan eventually reached the region where Yaqub's (AS) elder brother Ees (Esau) lived
- Ees noticed an unfamiliar, large caravan entering his territory
- Curious and cautious, he sent some men to investigate and find out who these travelers were

### The Report
- The scouts returned and reported:
  - "The leader of this caravan is a man called Yaqub."
  - "They are migrating from Harran and heading toward Canaan."

### Esau's Memory Awakens
- Upon hearing the name "Yaqub," Esau's 20-year-old memories came flooding back
- He immediately understood: this was his younger brother
- The passage of two decades had changed him

### The Advance With 400 Men
- Esau gathered 400 men from his tribe and rode out to meet Yaqub (AS)
- He was not necessarily coming with hostile intent — but with a large retinue befitting his status

### Yaqub's (AS) Fear
- When Yaqub (AS) learned that his elder brother was coming toward him:
  - He was struck with intense fear
  - He remembered that it was the fear of this very brother that had forced him to live away from his parents for 20 long years
  - Now, after all this time, he was about to face him

### Divine Reassurance
- At this critical moment, Allah Almighty sent angels to Yaqub (AS)
- The angels said:
  - "Do not be troubled. Do not be distressed."
  - "Allah is with you."
  - "Prepare to receive your brother warmly."

### The Gift for Esau
- This divine message gave Yaqub (AS) immense courage and hope
- To soften his brother's heart, he selected the most beautiful sheep and goats from his flocks as a gift

### The Face-to-Face Meeting
- Yaqub (AS) went ahead a certain distance to greet and receive his brother
- He approached with humility and respect

### The Power of Reconciliation
- **20 long years of separation** had done something remarkable
- The resentment, grudge, and hatred that Ees had once harbored had dissolved with time
- When Ees saw his younger brother approaching with respect and gifts:
  - The love for his younger brother surged in his heart
  - He rushed forward
  - He embraced Yaqub (AS) tightly

### The Emotional Reunion
- Both brothers kissed each other
- They held each other for a long time
- They wept, lost in the memories of their past
- The wounds of two decades were healed in that single embrace

---

## Part 11: Hospitality and Farewell

### Days Spent Together
- After the reunion, Yaqub (AS) spent several days as the guest of his elder brother Esau
- They spent time together, recounting old memories and renewing their brotherly bond

### The Farewell
- When it was time to continue his journey, Yaqub (AS) sought permission to leave
- Esau honored his younger brother
- He gave him many gifts
- He bid him farewell with great respect, honor, and love

---

## Part 12: Fulfilling the Vow — The Construction of Bayt al-Maqdis

### The Old Memory
- Yaqub (AS) clearly remembered the vow he had made 20 years earlier:
  - When he was fleeing to his uncle in Harran
  - He had slept with a stone as a pillow and seen the magnificent dream of the ladder
  - He had vowed: if he returned safely to his parents, he would build a house of worship for Allah at that very spot

### Retracing the Path
- Now, he was returning safely
- He remembered the old routes and began retracing his steps
- He traveled until he reached the vicinity of Jerusalem (Yerushalayim)

### The Search for the Stone
- Outside the settlement, Yaqub (AS) set up camp for his family
- He, along with his sons, began searching for the very stone he had anointed with oil 20 years ago
- Allah's help was with him — and soon, they successfully found the stone

### Purchasing the Land
- The land where the stone was located belonged to a man named Shechem bin Jamur
- Yaqub (AS) negotiated with him
- He purchased the land for **100 sheep**
- He now owned the sacred spot

### Construction of the House of Worship
- Yaqub (AS), together with his sons, began construction
- They built a dedicated place of worship — an Ibadaat Gah (House of Worship) — on that very land
- This was the fulfillment of his vow

### Bayt al-Maqdis (Jerusalem)
- This very place is today known as **Bayt al-Maqdis** (Jerusalem)
- It became one of the holiest sites in the world
- It would later be the location of Prophet Sulayman's (AS) magnificent temple
- It was the first Qibla of the Muslims before the Kaaba

---

## Part 13: The Death of Rachel (AS) — Birth of Benjamin

### The Second Son from Rachel
- During the time of their stay in the area of Bayt al-Maqdis:
  - Rachel (Rahil) (AS), the younger wife of Yaqub (AS), became pregnant
  - She gave birth to her second son

### The Birth of Benjamin
- The child was named **Benjamin (Bin Yamin)**
- He was the full brother of Prophet Yusuf (AS)

### The Tragedy During Childbirth
- Tragically, Rachel (AS) experienced severe complications during childbirth
- She did not survive the delivery
- She passed away (Inteqal)

### The Burial
- Yaqub (AS) buried his beloved wife Rachel (AS) in **Bayt al-Ham** (Bethlehem)
- He placed a stone at the head of her grave as a marker (Nishani)

### The Present-Day Grave
- According to historical accounts, the grave of Rachel (AS) and that marker stone still exist to this day
- It is a site of historical and religious significance
- (Wallahu A'lam — And Allah knows best)

### The Profound Grief
- Yaqub (AS) had a very deep and special love for Rachel (AS)
- Her death caused him severe shock and grief (Sakht Sadma)
- The pain was so great that he could no longer stay in Bayt al-Maqdis

---

## Part 14: Return to Hebron — The Death of Ishaq (AS)

### Moving to Hebron
- Yaqub (AS) left the residence at Bayt al-Maqdis
- He moved to **Hebron (Al-Khalil)**, where his father Ishaq (AS) lived
- This was the same place where his grandfather, Prophet Ibrahim (AS), had also resided

### The Family Reunited
- During this period, the elder brother Esau (Ees) also arrived in Hebron
- The family was together again

### The Death of Ishaq (AS)
- Prophet Ishaq (AS) passed away in the presence of both his sons — Esau and Yaqub (AS)
- He was **180 years old** at the time of his death

### The Burial
- Ishaq (AS) was buried in Hebron
- He was laid to rest beside his father, Prophet Ibrahim (AS)

---

## Part 15: The Trials of Yaqub (AS) — The Story of Yusuf (AS) Begins

### Special Love for the Two Younger Sons
- Yaqub (AS) had immense love for his two sons from Rachel (AS):
  1. Yusuf (AS)
  2. Benjamin (Bin Yamin)
- He always kept them close to him

### The Jealousy of the Ten Elder Brothers
- The ten elder sons (from other wives) deeply resented this special affection
- They were intensely jealous (Hasid) of Yusuf (AS) and Benjamin
- They never missed an opportunity to cause them pain, grief, and distress

### Yaqub's (AS) Awareness
- Yaqub (AS) was fully aware that the ten older brothers were jealous of the two younger ones
- This awareness caused him constant worry and anxiety

---

## Part 16: A Life of Sorrow, Patience, and Unshakeable Faith

### The First Trial — Exile in Youth
- As a young man, Yaqub (AS) had to flee from his home
- Fear of his elder brother kept him away from his parents for 20 years
- He lived in a foreign land (Diyar-e-Ghair)

### The Second Trial — Yusuf's (AS) Disappearance
- In his old age, he faced an even greater trial
- The ten elder sons inflicted severe pain upon Yusuf (AS)
- They took him away and threw him into a well
- They sold him into slavery
- They then lied to their father, claiming a wolf had devoured him
- This was a crushing blow to Yaqub's (AS) heart

### Forty Years of Weeping
- Yaqub (AS) was separated from his beloved son Yusuf (AS)
- For **40 continuous years**, he wept in remembrance of Yusuf (AS)
- He cried so much and so constantly that he eventually **lost his eyesight** (Binai Jaya Ho Gayi — he became blind)

### The Patience of a Prophet
- Despite this unimaginable grief, Yaqub (AS) never lost faith in Allah
- He never complained against Allah's decree
- He only expressed his sorrow to Allah
- His famous words, recorded in the Quran (Surah Yusuf, 12:86):
  - "I only complain of my suffering and grief to Allah, and I know from Allah that which you do not know."
- He practiced **"Sabr-e-Jameel"** (Beautiful Patience)

---

## Part 17: The Reunion — Yusuf (AS) as the Ruler of Egypt

### The Divine Plan Unfolds
- After 40 years of separation, Allah's plan came to fruition
- Yusuf (AS) had risen from a slave to a prisoner to the **Ruler (Wali)** of Egypt

### The Reunion
- Allah reunited Yaqub (AS) with his son Yusuf (AS) in the most honorable circumstances
- Yaqub (AS), the grieving father, was brought to Egypt
- He met his son, who was now a powerful king

### The Embrace
- When father and son met after four decades of separation:
  - They embraced each other
  - They wept for a long, long time
  - The tears were no longer only of grief — they were now tears of joy, gratitude, and relief

### Life in Egypt
- Yaqub (AS) lived in Egypt with his son Yusuf (AS) for **24 years**
- During this time, he was surrounded by his children and grandchildren
- The entire family — all 12 sons — was reunited and living in peace and prosperity
- The family numbered **93 persons** when they first entered Egypt, and they grew from there

---

## Part 18: The Final Moments — The Question and The Answer

### The Approaching Death
- When the time of Yaqub's (AS) death drew near:
  - He gathered all his sons around him

### The Crucial Question
- Yaqub (AS) asked his sons a question — not about wealth, not about land, not about worldly matters
- He asked, as recorded in Surah Al-Baqarah (2:133):
  - **"What will you worship after me?"**
  - "Who will you worship when I am gone?"

### The Unanimous Answer
- All the sons answered in one voice, with complete unity:
  - "O our father!"
  - "We will worship your God (Ilah) — the God of your forefathers:"
    - **Ibrahim (AS)**
    - **Ismail (AS)**
    - **Ishaq (AS)**
  - "He is the One and Only God (Ma'bud-e-Yakta)."
  - "And we are Muslims — submitting to Him in obedience."

### The Significance
- This was Yaqub's (AS) final act of Prophethood
- He ensured that his children were firmly established upon Tawhid (Monotheism)
- He passed on the legacy of Ibrahim (AS) to the next generation

---

## Part 19: The Final Command of Ibrahim (AS) and Yaqub (AS)

### The Verse in Surah Al-Baqarah (2:132)
- Allah Almighty says:
  - "And Ibrahim instructed his sons (to worship Allah alone) — and so did Yaqub."
  - "(Saying): O my sons! Allah has chosen this religion (Deen) for you. Therefore, do not die except in a state of Islam (submission to Allah)."

### The Meaning
- This was the eternal testament of the Prophets
- They did not teach their children to pursue wealth, power, or fame
- Their only concern was that their offspring remain firm on Tawhid
- That they live as Muslims and die as Muslims

---

## Part 20: The Death and Burial of Yaqub (AS)

### The Place and Time of Death
- Yaqub (AS) lived in Egypt for 24 years after reuniting with Yusuf (AS)
- He then passed away
- His blessed age at the time of death was **147 years**

### The Final Will (Wasiyyat)
- Before his death, Yaqub (AS) made a will to his son Yusuf (AS):
  - "Send my body back to my homeland."
  - "Bury me beside my father, Ishaq (AS)."

### The Funeral Journey
- Said bin Jubair (RA) narrates:
  - The body of Yaqub (AS) was placed in a wooden coffin (coffin/casket)
  - It was transported from Egypt to **Bayt al-Maqdis** (Palestine)

### The Origin of a Jewish Custom
- This act became the origin of a widespread custom among the Jews
- Because Yaqub (AS) — Israel — was carried from Egypt to Palestine for burial:
  - Jews began the practice of transporting their deceased from far-off places to be buried in Jerusalem (Bayt al-Maqdis)

### The Burial Site
- Yaqub (AS) was laid to rest in Hebron (Al-Khalil)
- He was buried beside his father Ishaq (AS) and his grandfather Ibrahim (AS)
- This site — the Cave of the Patriarchs (Ibrahimi Mosque) — remains one of the most sacred places on earth

---

## Part 21: The Growth of Bani Israel

### The Entry into Egypt
- Hazrat Abdullah bin Mas'ud (RA) narrates:
  - When Yaqub (AS) and his family (Aulad) entered Egypt, they numbered **93 persons** (73 according to some narrations)

### The Exodus
- Generations passed
- The descendants of these 93 people multiplied exponentially
- When Prophet Musa (AS) led Bani Israel out of Egypt:
  - Their number had reached an astonishing **600,700** (six hundred thousand seven hundred / 6 lakh 7 hazaar)

### The Fulfillment of the Promise
- This was the fulfillment of Allah's promise to Yaqub (AS) at Bethel:
  - "I will make your descendants as numerous as the dust of the earth."

> The life of Prophet Yaqub (AS) is one of the most poignant and emotionally profound narratives in the Quran. He was a man who experienced nearly every form of human trial — sibling rivalry, exile from home, deception in marriage, the death of a beloved wife, the false loss of a cherished son, decades of blindness from weeping, and the pain of an uncertain reunion. Yet through it all, he remained the servant of Allah — "Israel" — one who strives with and for Allah. His legacy is not just his twelve sons or the nation that bears his name, but his example of beautiful patience and unshakeable trust in the divine promise. His final concern was not wealth or power, but that his children remain true to the worship of the One God. In this, he perfected the legacy of his grandfather Ibrahim (AS), his father Ishaq (AS), and set the foundation for his son Yusuf (AS) and all the Prophets who would descend from his blessed lineage.
