# The Story of Prophet Idris ؑ — Detailed Notes

---

## 📍 Part 1: The Family Tree — From Adam ؑ to Idris ؑ

### The Chain of Prophets
- **Adam ؑ** — First human, first Prophet
- **Shees ؑ (Seth)** — Son of Adam ؑ, second Prophet
- **Idris ؑ** — Descended from Shees ؑ through his son **Noush**

### Idris's ؑ Family
- **Father**: Yared
- **Mother**: Barkana
- **Wife**: Adma
- **Son**: Methuselah (Matoshalik)
- **Famous descendant**: He is the **great-grandfather of Nuh ؑ (Noah)**
- **Birthplace**: Babylon (Babel) — in modern-day Iraq

### His Original Name
- His real name was **Ukhnuh** or **Hunu**
- The title **"Idris"** was given because he was the first to give **"Dars" (lessons/teaching)** to people
- The word Idris literally means **"The Teacher"** or "One who gives lessons"

## ⚡ Part 4: The Background — What Happened Between Adam ؑ and Idris ؑ

### The Story of Habil and Qabeel (Abel and Cain)

**The Marriage Dispute:**
- Children of Adam ؑ were born in pairs — a boy and a girl born together
- Allah's law at that time: a boy from one pair would marry the girl from another pair
- **Qabeel's twin sister was very beautiful** — Qabeel refused to give her up
- But she was rightfully promised to **Habil**

**The Test — The Sacrifice:**
- Adam ؑ resolved the dispute by commanding both sons to offer a sacrifice to Allah
- Whichever sacrifice was accepted — that son would win the right to marry her
- The sign of acceptance: fire would descend from heaven and consume the accepted sacrifice

**The Result:**
- **Habil's sacrifice was accepted** — fire consumed it
- **Qabeel's was rejected**
- Qabeel was furious and threatened: *"I will kill you"*

**Habil's Noble Response (Quran 5:28-29):**
> *"Even if you stretch your hand to kill me — I will not stretch my hand to kill you. I fear Allah, Lord of all worlds. I want you to carry the burden of my sin and your sin — and be among the companions of Fire"*

**The First Murder:**
- Qabeel killed Habil — while he slept with his head on a stone
- Qabeel didn't know how to bury the body — no one had died in Adam's ؑ family before
- **Allah sent a crow** that dug the ground, killed another crow, and buried it — teaching Qabeel how to bury his brother
- Qabeel said in shame: *"Woe to me — could I not be like this crow and hide my brother's body?"*

**The Eternal Consequence:**
The Prophet ﷺ said:
> *"Whenever any person is killed unjustly — the sin is also recorded in Qabeel's book of deeds, because he was the first to establish unjust killing"*

### Shees ؑ — The Gift After Grief

**Adam's ؑ Grief:**
- After Habil's martyrdom, Adam ؑ was deeply sorrowful
- This was the first death in his family — an enormous grief

**Allah's Gift:**
- Allah gave Adam ؑ another son — **Shees ؑ** — to console him
- Some scriptures were revealed to Shees ؑ (narrations mention 50 scrolls)
- Shees ؑ was described as the most handsome of all Adam's ؑ children
- The light of the future Prophet Muhammad ﷺ shone brightly from his lineage

**Shees's ؑ Character:**
- He constantly listened to Adam's ؑ descriptions of Paradise
- He withdrew from worldly pleasures
- He was always absorbed in worship

**The Two Camps of Humanity:**
During Shees's ؑ time, humanity split into two groups:
1. **Followers of Shees ؑ** — on the straight path, following divine guidance
2. **Followers of Qabeel's lineage** — deep in sin and corruption

**Shees's ؑ Advice on a True Believer:**
He taught:
- Know Allah
- Distinguish good from evil
- Obey the ruler of the time
- Honor and serve parents
- Maintain family bonds
- Show kindness and love to people
- Control anger
- Feed the poor and have mercy on them
- Avoid sins
- Be patient in hardship
- Be grateful for blessings

**His Age**: approximately **912 years**
*(Note: Shees ؑ is not mentioned in the Quran — mentioned in other Islamic historical sources)*

## 🌟 Part 5: The Birth and Mission of Idris ؑ

### Why Idris ؑ Was Chosen
- After Shees ؑ, corruption spread further
- The descendants of Qabeel had dominated society
- The followers of divine guidance had become a tiny minority
- Allah decided to send a new Prophet to this corrupt society
- He chose **Idris ؑ from the lineage of Shees ؑ**

### Idris's ؑ Personal Qualities
He possessed all qualities necessary for prophethood:
- Extremely devoted worshipper
- Deeply pious
- Absolutely truthful
- Intensely contemplative and thoughtful
- Deeply committed to the religion of Adam ؑ

### His Physical Description(some old scholars)
- Handsome, clean appearance
- Perfect, well-built stature
- Beautiful face
- Full, thick beard
- Broad shoulders
- Strong bones
- Serious and composed
- Dark, bright, kohl-like eyes
- Spoke with wisdom and weight
- Naturally quiet and reserved
- Kept gaze lowered while walking
- When speaking in anger — would point with his index finger

### His Devotional Practices
Three things were especially important for him:
1. **Burning of incense/fragrances**
2. **Animal sacrifice**
3. **Wine** *(note: the context suggests this refers to pre-Islamic religious offerings, not intoxicating wine)*

Additionally:
- The first fruit of each season was offered
- Among fruits: **apple** was most honored
- Among grains: **wheat** was most honored
- Among flowers: **rose** was most honored

## 🏆 Part 6: Idris's ؑ World-Changing Contributions

### The Firsts of Human Civilization
Idris ؑ is credited with introducing to humanity:

| Contribution | Significance |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Writing with a pen** | First person in history to write — giving literacy to humanity |
| **Tailoring clothes** | Before him, people wore animal skins for clothing |
| **Astronomy** | First to observe and study stars and planets scientifically |
| **Mathematics** | Introduced numerical calculations and arithmetic |
| **Weights and measures** | Established standardized systems of measurement |
| **Weapons** | Invented weapons — then used them for jihad against Qabeel's descendants |
| **Urban planning** | Designed cities and buildings according to blueprints |

### On Writing
**Ibn Ishaq** states:
> *"The first person in the world who wrote with a pen was Hazrat Idris ؑ"*

Muwawiya bin huqm question about **Ilm al-Raml**
The Prophet ﷺ was also asked about **Ilm al-Raml** (a form of knowledge involving drawing lines in sand). He replied:
> *"There was a Prophet who wrote this — whoever's writing matches his — that is correct"*

This is understood by scholars as referring to Idris ؑ and his mathematical/geometric knowledge.

## 🏙️ Part 7: Idris ؑ as a Statesman and Civilizational Builder

### The Hijra (Migration) from Babylon
- When Idris ؑ began preaching in **Babylon**, people of Qabeel's lineage severely persecuted him and his followers
- They:
  - Mocked him
  - Threw stones at him
  - Physically harassed his followers
- Idris ؑ decided to migrate

### His Followers Hesitated
- Babylon was a beautiful, fertile land between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers
- His followers were reluctant to leave such richness
- Idris ؑ counseled and persuaded them

### Migration to Egypt
- Idris ؑ and his followers migrated to **Egypt** (Misr) and settled on the banks of the **Nile River**
- The Nile region was even more fertile and beautiful than Babylon
- Here, his mission continued with greater success

### Languages
- Idrees know many languages and teach people in there own language.

### Idrees and Nuh
- Idrees is great grand son of Nuh and Live 1000 years before Nuh(Tafseer Ma'ariful Quran).

### Four regions
Prophet Idrees established rules, systems, and laws that were accepted by all thoughtful people of his time.
For proper administration of the earth:
He divided the population into four regions
Appointed a ruler (hakim) for each region
Created laws based on this geographical division

### The Urban Revolution
- Idris ؑ selected students from different tribes
- He taught them:
  - **Ilm al-Hikma** (Wisdom and philosophy)
  - **Ilm al-Nujum** (Astronomy)
  - **Urban governance and political science**
  - **Laws and regulations**
  - Building and civic principles

- When these students returned to their tribes, they established **approximately 200 cities** — all built on civilized principles
- The smallest of these cities was **"Ruha"**
- Road networks connecting cities were laid
- **Markets** established for trade
- **Sports fields** built for recreation(Grounds for sports and activities)
- City buildings designed according to **architectural plans**(Planned construction of houses and buildings)


### Division of Society
Idris ؑ divided society by **knowledge and responsibility** (not by race or family):

| Class | Role | Accountability |
|-------|------|---------------|
| **Scholars (Ulama)** | Highest — guides and teachers | Responsible for themselves, rulers, and people |
| **Rulers (Badshah)** | Second — governors and administrators | Responsible for themselves and state affairs |
| **Common People (Rayya)** | Third — general population | Responsible for themselves only |

*Important note: This was based on merit and responsibility — NOT on birth, lineage, or racial distinctions*

### His Personal Inscriptions
Words engraved on his **ring**:
> *"Patience alongside faith in Allah is a sign of success"*

Words on his **belt**:
> *"True happiness lies in protecting Allah's obligations — and the perfection of religion is connected to divine law"*

Words on his **funeral belt**:
> *"Blessed is one who guards his own soul. A person's intercessor before Allah is his own good deeds"*

### His Laws and Governance
He established:
- Laws for different nations/regions according to their situations
- A system of annual **Eid celebrations**
- Fixed times for charity and sacrifice
- Obligatory **sacrifices at appointed times**
- A complete **Sharia (divine law system)** with five core principles:
  1. The only worthy of worship is the Creator of heavens and Earth
  2. Good deeds lead to a peaceful life
  3. Material world is temporary and will perish
  4. Justice and rule of law eliminate corruption from society
  5. Thoughtful, sincere application of divine laws produces the best outcomes

### Knowledge and Education
* He taught not only religion but also:
  * Politics
  * Urban life
  * Civilization and social systems
* He gathered students and trained them in:
  * Civil administration
  * Laws and governance
* When they became skilled:
  * They returned to their tribes
  * Established cities based on these principles
* It is said:
  * Around **many cities were built**
  * One of the smallest cities was called **Ruha**

### علوم (Sciences Taught)
* Prophet Idrees taught:
  * Wisdom (Hikmah)
  * Astronomy (Ilm-un-Nujum)
  * Mathematics (Counting and calculation)
* He is considered among the **first to explore these sciences**
* Allah taught him:
  * Structure of the heavens
  * Movements of planets
  * Their combinations and separations
  * Hidden systems of the universe

## ⚔️ Part 8: Jihad Against Qabeel's Descendants

### The Preaching Phase
- Idris's ؑ primary effort was always **peaceful preaching (tabligh)**
- He hoped that through wisdom and counsel, the descendants of Qabeel would return to the right path
- He spoke to them in **their own languages** — he knew all the languages of his time

### The Rejection
- His message was deeply unwelcome to those steeped in sin
- They would become furious when he spoke of:
  - Worshipping one Allah
  - Abandoning evil
  - Day of Judgment
- They mocked him, harassed him, threw stones at him

### The Decision for Jihad
- After exhausting all peaceful options
- When preaching clearly wasn't reaching these people
- By Allah's command, **Idris ؑ declared jihad against the descendants of Qabeel**
- He assembled a group of young believers
- Fought against them
- **Allah granted him victory**
- Many people returned to the truth after this

## 🌌 Part 9: The Unique Ending — Idris ؑ and the Angel of Death

### The Context — His Extraordinary Worship
- Idris ؑ was among the most devoted worshippers Allah had created
- Allah revealed to him:
  > *"I give you every day the equivalent deeds of all the children of Adam combined"*
- Idris ؑ desired to do even more — and wished to know how much of his life remained

### The Angel Carries Him to Heaven
- An angel came and **carried Idris ؑ on his wings** toward the heavens
- When they reached the **fourth heaven**, they encountered **Azrael ؑ (the Angel of Death)** descending

### The Encounter
- The angel asked Azrael ؑ about Idris's ؑ remaining lifespan
- Azrael ؑ was **astonished**: *"How strange — I was commanded to take his soul on the fourth heaven. I was wondering how, since I thought he was on Earth. But here he is!"*
- Azrael ؑ looked at Idris ؑ and said: *"His remaining life is less than the blink of an eye"*
- When the angel looked — Idris ؑ had already **passed away**

### Angles
- Idress alwasy busy in worshiping allah and angles come to him and sit beside him.
- Once up on a time, an Azrael come to him in human face with permission of allah, When idress get to know he is Azrael(Angle of death) then:

### Idris's ؑ Wish — To Experience Death and See Paradise
Some narrations mention:
- Idris ؑ had requested Azrael ؑ to **let him taste death** once
- Azrael ؑ, with Allah's permission, temporarily took his soul — then returned it
- After this, Idris ؑ asked to **see Hell and Paradise**
- Azrael show him  Hell and Paradise with permission of allah.
- He was shown Hell — and was deeply affected
- He was taken into Paradise
- When Azrael ؑ said it was time to return — Idris ؑ refused:
  > Prophet Idrees said, ‘I will not leave until the Creator of Hell and Paradise commands me.
  > *"By Allah's word — 'We will not remove them from it' — I have tasted death, and now I am in Paradise. I will not leave"*
- A third angel was sent to arbitrate
- The ruling: **Idris ؑ remains on the sixth heaven worshipping with the angels**

### His Location
- Most reliable narrations (confirmed hadith from Miraj): Idris ؑ is on the **fourth heaven**
- The Prophet ﷺ met him there during the Night Journey (Miraj)
- Idris ؑ greeted him:
  > *"Welcome — righteous brother and righteous Prophet"*

### The Quranic Reference
> *"And We raised him to a high station"* — Surah Maryam (19:57)

This verse is understood by scholars as referring to his elevation to the heavens while still alive — or his elevated rank with Allah.

## 🗝️ Key Lessons from the Story of Idris ؑ

| Lesson | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Knowledge is divine** | The first writer, first mathematician, first astronomer — these gifts came through a Prophet |
| **Civilization begins with education** | Idris ؑ built 200 cities by first educating students — knowledge precedes infrastructure |
| **Preaching before fighting** | He tried peaceful tabligh first — jihad was only the last resort |
| **Merit over lineage** | His social ranking was based on responsibility and knowledge — not race or birth |
| **Worship elevates** | His extraordinary devotion literally elevated him to the heavens |
| **Death is for everyone — even the great** | Even a Prophet raised to the heavens experienced the Angel of Death |

| **Silence and contemplation** | He was naturally quiet and thoughtful — wisdom comes from reflection |

### 📖 Story related to *“And We raised him to a high station”*

(from Surah Maryam 19:57, about Prophet Idrees)

* Abdullah ibn Abbas (رضي الله عنه) asked Ka'b al-Ahbar (رضي الله عنه):
  👉 *What is the meaning of the verse: “We raised him to a high station”?*

* Ka'b (رضي الله عنه) replied:

  * Allah sent revelation to Prophet Idrees saying:
    👉 *“I grant you daily the reward equal to the deeds of all the children of Adam.”*
    (Meaning: all people of that time)

  * Hearing this, Idrees (عليه السلام) thought:
    👉 *“Then I should increase my deeds even more.”*

* When angels came to him:

  * He said:
    👉 *“I want to speak to the Angel of Death and ask how much life I have left.”*

  * An angel lifted him on his wings and took him towards the sky.

* When they reached the **fourth heaven**:

  * They met Angel of Death (Azrael), who was coming down.

  * The angel accompanying Idrees said:
    👉 *“This is Idrees.”*

  * The Angel of Death asked:
    👉 *“Where is Idrees?”*

  * The angel replied:
    👉 *“He is on my wings.”*

* The Angel of Death expressed surprise:

  * 👉 *“SubhanAllah! I was sent with the command to take the soul of Idrees in the fourth heaven.”*
  * 👉 *“I was wondering how I would do that while he is on earth…”*
  * 👉 *“…and now you have brought him here!”*

* Then:

  * The Angel of Death took the soul of Prophet Idrees **in the fourth heaven**

* It is also mentioned (by Ibn Abi Hatim in tafsir):

  * Idrees (عليه السلام) asked the angels:
    👉 *“Ask the Angel of Death how much life I have left.”*

  * The Angel of Death said:
    👉 *“I cannot answer until I see him.”*

  * When he saw him, he said:
    👉 *“You are asking about a man whose life has less time left than the blink of an eye.”*

  * When the angel looked beneath his wings:
    👉 Idrees (عليه السلام) had already passed away.
