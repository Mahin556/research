# The Story of Prophet Lut (Lot) and the People of Sodom — Detailed Notes

---

## Part 1: A City Showered With Wealth

### The Land of Blessings
- There was a city built with joy and laughter — inhabited by a nation blessed with every favor
- Yet it became a land where sins were committed openly and publicly — not hidden in secret
- The crime of this nation was so unique and unprecedented that nothing like it had ever been recorded in human memory
- Allah did not approve of their way — and then Allah's decree descended
- In a single moment, the entire nation was overturned along with its civilization
- It remains a lesson and warning until the Day of Judgment

---

## Part 2: Sodom and Gomorrah — The Twin Cities

### Location
- Two famous cities lay between modern-day Jordan and Palestine:
  1. Sodom (Sadum)
  2. Gomorrah (Gomora)

### The Land's Abundance
- Sodom was a city where the earth contained springs and gardens both
- The crops were so strong and abundant that people never imagined this land could ever reject them
- Date palm clusters were soft and full of fruit
- The water from the earth had natural sweetness
- Everything about the land spoke of Allah's generosity

---

## Part 3: Introduction to the People of Lut

### The Corruption Begins Slowly
- Despite the sweetness of the land, their hearts slowly began turning hard
- Initially, their sins were individual and private
  > *"Your smile is very beautiful."*
- Then leaders smiled and said:
  > *"This is a new path. There's enjoyment in this."*
- Gradually, this became a habit, then a custom, then the dominant culture
- Sin was no longer just an act — it had become embedded into the civilization itself

### Open and Public Sin
- People came out from the darkness of night into open gatherings
- They would announce their decisions publicly and mock Allah's commandments in debate
- The valley of blessings had transformed into an arena of sins

---

## Part 4: Hazrat Ibrahim and Hazrat Lut — In Babylon

### Their Connection
- Far away, in the city of Babylon (Babul):
  - Hazrat Ibrahim (Abraham) (AS) — the Prophet of Allah
  - Hazrat Lut (Lot) (AS) — his nephew
- Together they conveyed Allah's message to the people

### The Migration
- By Allah's command, they migrated (hijrah) from Babylon
- Hazrat Lut (AS) was the first person to believe in Ibrahim (AS)
- This means they were connected not only by blood but also by the journey of faith

### Quranic Reference
The Quran states:
> *"And We saved him and Lut to the land which We have blessed for all the worlds."*
- This refers to the region of Sham (Greater Syria)
- Ibrahim (AS) settled in one city
- Lut (AS) was sent by Allah to another settlement — Sodom (Sadum)
- There, he was appointed as a Prophet for the guidance of the people

---

## Part 5: Hazrat Lut's Entry Into Sodom

### First Impressions
- When he entered the city, amidst the lights and colors of the world, a strange shiver passed through his heart
- Before his eyes were:
  - Trade caravans
  - High buildings
  - The fragrance of ambergris and silk in the markets
  - Lush greenery waving in the fields
- Everything was testifying to Allah's blessings

### The Prophet's Grief
- But as he moved among the people, the smile slowly faded from his face
- What he was witnessing was not just a scene of sadness for a Prophet — it was a heart-shaking truth

---

## Part 6: The Era of Immorality and Oppression

### The Core Sin
- These were people who had abandoned the natural disposition (fitrah) given by Allah
- They had turned their desires into their god
- Men had turned away from women and inclined toward men instead
- A sin that had never occurred in human history before this

### Public Nature of Their Sin
- According to the Quran, they committed this evil not secretly but openly
- In their gatherings: shamelessness
- In their streets: obscenity
  > *"Close the door and bring the wine."*
- On the roads: mistreatment of travelers had become normal
  > *"It seems he is alone. Come, let us take the fruit of his labor."*
  > *"In Sodom, the law is ours. Give us these goods. With them I will host you all tonight."*

### Lawlessness and Pride
- Think about this — the city Allah had given:
  - Peace
  - Sustenance (Rizq)
  - Beauty
- On that very land, people sat on the streets and:
  - Harassed passersby
  - Looted their wealth
  - Held inappropriate intentions toward them
  - And felt proud of these actions
- > *"For God's sake, let me go free!"*
  - [Laughter follows]

---

## Part 7: Hazrat Lut's Call and Guidance

### His Message
One day, Hazrat Lut (AS) came to his nation and said:
> *"My people, fear Allah. I have brought you a message in which lies your well-being."*

### Their Response
The youth of the nation laughed and said:
> *"Well-being flows in the streets of our city. We didn't ask for any Prophet."*

Lut (AS) replied:
> *"The rise of nations comes through faith (Iman), not through the height of walls."*

The leaders angrily responded:
> *"We built the height of these walls with our own hands. What is this thing called faith?"*

Lut (AS) said with gentleness:
> *"Where human nature itself is trampled, the lifespan of cities also becomes short."*

The nation mocked:
> *"Bring that punishment you keep threatening us with!"*

### They Thought It Was Just Talk
- They believed Allah's word was merely conversation
- But they did not know that Allah's word is a decree (Faisla)
- Not one day, not one month — but an entire era passing through generations
- Lut (AS) stood in those same streets, same markets, among those same people
- Every day he raised the same call:
  > *"Fear Allah. This act is against nature. Leave this path. Return to Allah's mercy."*
- But the reply was always the same:
  - The same faces
  - The same mockery
  - The same arrogance
  > *"This body is ours. We have authority over it. No one can stop us."*

### The Prophet's Burden
- Witnessing all this had become mental torment and spiritual weight for Lut (AS)
- Finally, that same helplessness entered his heart which is the heaviest moment of every Prophet's test

---

## Part 8: Hazrat Lut's Supplication — "O Allah, Save Me!"

He turned to his Lord and spread his hands in supplication:
> *"O my Lord, help me against this corrupt nation!"*

And from here, the wind of Allah's decree began to blow.

---

## Part 9: The Angels Visit Hazrat Ibrahim — The Climax Begins

### The Heavenly Guests
One day, angels in human form came as guests to Hazrat Ibrahim (AS)

### Ibrahim's Hospitality
- He welcomed them with honor and a smile
- He immediately displayed his traditional hospitality:
  > *"Stay for a while. Let me bring something for you."*
- Then he presented a feast before them
- But he noticed the guests were not extending their hands or touching the food
- Lines of worry appeared on his blessed face

### The Angels Reveal Themselves
The angels understood that questions had arisen in the heart of Khalilullah (The Friend of Allah)
They said:
> *"Do not be afraid. We are Allah's angels."*

And alongside this, they gave him glad tidings of a son — the birth of Hazrat Ishaq (Isaac) (AS)

Hazrat Ibrahim's (AS) heart filled with joy and the smile of gratitude returned.

### The Second Decree
After the good news, the angels announced the next decree:
> *"We are now going toward a settlement that has drowned in excessive corruption and shamelessness."*
> *"A Prophet resides there — Hazrat Lut (AS)."*
> *"And we have been sent to destroy his nation."*

### Ibrahim's Compassion
- Hazrat Ibrahim (AS), who was an ocean of mercy and compassion
- He interceded with the angels, hoping perhaps some path of guidance might still remain
- But Allah's decree had already been written in the heavens
- The period of guidance had expired
- And the angels departed from there

---

## Part 10: The Angels Visit Hazrat Lut in Sodom

### Arrival at Lut's Home
- The angels went to the house of Hazrat Lut (AS) in human form, as guests
- As soon as news spread in the city that guests had come to Lut's house:
  > *"Guests have come to Lut's house! Come with me immediately!"*
- People came running
- The sound of voices like acid began raining at the door

### The Mob at the Door
The youth shouted:
> *"Open the door! We will welcome the guests ourselves!"*

### Lut's Response
Hazrat Lut (AS):
- Stopped them in very clear words
- Gave them counsel
- Reminded them of the fear of Allah
- He said:
  > *"Leave from here and do not harass my guests."*

### Their Rejection
The nation screamed:
> *"We have no interest in your advice. Send those guests out!"*

---

## Part 11: The Angels Reveal Their True Form

### The Revelation
The angels revealed their true form and said to Hazrat Lut (AS):
> *"Do not be afraid. We are Allah's angels. These people cannot harm you at all."*

### The Instructions
> *"Leave with your household during the darkness of night — except your wife."*
> *"And do not look back at all."*
> *"For by morning, we will destroy this nation."*

### The Meaning
- From the very land where the people of mercy departed, the first note of punishment rose
- The moment those blessed ones left, doom became inevitable

---

## Part 12: The Punishment Begins — The Earth Quakes!

### When Allah's Command Came
- The earth began to tremble
- Houses began to flip over
- From the sky, marked stones began to fall

### Nature of the Stones
- Every stone came with:
  - Its own address
  - Its own target
  - Its own criminal
- No stone was random — each was designated for a specific sinner

### The Ultimate Punishment
According to some narrations, Allah lifted the settlement from the chest of the earth upward

Stones were raining from all directions:
- Neither the high roofs of palaces could protect them
- Nor the strength of leaders was of any use
- Nor did the wealth of the people help

People were:
- Screaming
- Running
- And all their arrogance and sins were sinking beneath the earth with every stone

---

## Part 13: The City Turned Upside Down — The Greatest Punishment!

### The Final Moment
Then came the moment when Allah turned the entire city upside down

### Complete Destruction
- Every road — overturned
- Every street — overturned
- Every house — overturned
- The names of the cities were erased
- The roads were erased
- And the people were erased

### Why It Was Not Forgotten
But the incident was not erased — because Allah willed it to remain a lesson (Ibrah) for all time

---

> The story of the People of Lut (AS) is one of the most sobering in the Quran — a nation blessed with abundant gardens, sweet water, and thriving trade, yet they chose to invert the natural order Allah created. They became the first civilization in human history to normalize what no nation before them had ever practiced openly. Their destruction was equally unprecedented — the earth itself was commanded to lift them up and flip them over, while marked stones rained from the sky, each carrying the name of its intended target. The Dead Sea region still bears the scars of this event. The Quran preserves this account not as mere history, but as an everlasting warning: that Allah's blessings are a trust, and when a people collectively abandon fitrah and celebrate their defiance, no wall, no wealth, and no numbers can shield them from divine justice.
