## Part 2: The Birth of Hazrat Ismail (AS)

### Background
- Hazrat Ibrahim (AS)'s first marriage was to his cousin, Hazrat Sarah (AS)
- For a long period, they remained childless
- One day, Sarah (AS) suggested that Ibrahim (AS) marry Hajra (Hagar)
  - She hoped Allah might grant them offspring through her
- This was all part of Allah's divine plan

### The Marriage and Birth
- Ibrahim (AS) married Hazrat Hajra (AS)
- One year after the marriage, she gave birth to Hazrat Ismail (AS)
- At that time, Ibrahim (AS) was 86 years old
- When Hajra was expecting, an angel gave glad tidings of a son and instructed them to name him Ismail
- Ismail (AS) was born in 1800 BC

### The Test Begins
- Ibrahim (AS), after years of prayers, desires, and hopes, had just received the blessing of his first child in old age
- But he could not fully express his joy
- He was immediately tested by Allah
- By Allah's command, he left his newborn son and obedient wife thousands of miles away from Sham (Syria)

### The Valley of Faran
- The location was the valley of Faran (Paran) — among the black, rocky mountains
- In the scorching desert, under an old tree, a righteous and devoted servant of Allah sat with her newborn, innocent son
- Their provisions ran out within days
- Mother and child became distressed from hunger and thirst
- The mother had no worry for herself, but the restlessness of her hungry and thirsty child stirred the natural maternal instinct
- But in that blazing desert, where was water?

### The Search for Water — Safa and Marwa
- The child's thirst was intensifying
- The mother's restlessness was increasing
- In this state of distress, she climbed the nearby hill of Safa — hoping to spot any human or sign of water
- Finding nothing, with a restless heart, she ran to the other hill, Marwa
- Again, nothing
- In this manner, she completed seven rounds between the two hills in search of water

### The Miracle of Zamzam
- Meanwhile, Allah Almighty, through Hazrat Jibraeel (AS), caused the world's most blessed spring to gush forth from beneath the feet of the infant Ismail (AS)
- Regarding Zamzam water, Hazrat Abdullah bin Abbas (RA) narrates that the Prophet (SAW) said:
  - "May Allah have mercy on the mother of Ismail (AS). If she had not cupped the water and had not told it to stop, it would have been a flowing spring."
- Zamzam water is:
  - Nourishment
  - Healing
  - Medicine
  - A means of accepted supplication
- Allah not only provided the best nourishment for mother and child but made it a means of blessing for Muslims until the Day of Judgment

### The Settlement of Makkah
- Allah's intention was to make this place the most sacred city in the world
- One day, some people from the Arab tribe of Banu Jurhum arrived in search of water
- With Hajra's (AS) permission, they settled there
- Then other tribes came and settled as well
- Thus, the once desolate desert transformed into Makkah Mukarramah — the most sacred and blessed city in the world
- Hajra (AS) paid special attention to her son's upbringing, education, and training

### Ismail's Youth
- Thirteen long years passed like the blink of an eye
- Ismail (AS) grew up in the sacred atmosphere of Makkah, drinking Zamzam water, and under the training of his great mother — a paragon of patience and gratitude
- He became a beautiful young man with the finest qualities

## Part 3: The Vision of Prophet Ibrahim (AS)

### Ibrahim's Return
- One day, Hazrat Ibrahim (AS) arrived and said:
  - "O my son, I see in a dream that I am sacrificing you. So think — what is your opinion?"

### Ismail's Response
- The obedient son bowed his head in submission and said:
  - "O my father, do what you have been commanded. If Allah wills, you will find me among the patient ones."
- This martyrdom-loving response of Ismail (AS) is a magnificent example of the etiquette of a righteous son for all the children of the world

### Preparation for Sacrifice
- Ibrahim (AS) embraced his son upon hearing this response
- He brought him to Hajra (AS) and said:
  - "O Hajra, today prepare our beloved son with your own hands."
- The mother, immersed in sacred maternal love and priceless emotions:
  - Dressed her only son in new clothes
  - Applied kohl (surma) to his eyes
  - Oiled his hair
  - Perfumed him
  - Made him ready to go out with his father
- Meanwhile, Ibrahim (AS) had arranged a sharp knife
- He took his son and set out from Makkah toward Mina

### Shaytan's Interference — The Three Jamarat
- Shaytan overheard the conversation between father and son
- When he witnessed this scene of patience, steadfastness, and devotion to Allah, he became distressed
- He decided to dissuade father and son from this sacrifice

#### First Attempt — Jamrah Aqabah
- Shaytan blocked their path at the place of Jamrah Aqabah
- The angel accompanying Ibrahim (AS) said: "This is Shaytan. Pelt him with pebbles."
- Ibrahim (AS) said "Allahu Akbar" and threw seven pebbles at him
- Shaytan sank into the ground

#### Second Attempt — Jamrah Usta
- As they moved forward, Shaytan emerged again at the place of Jamrah Usta to deceive them
- Ibrahim (AS) pelted him with pebbles again
- Shaytan sank into the ground once more

#### Third Attempt — Jamrah Aula
- As they advanced further, Shaytan appeared at the place of Jamrah Aula
- Ibrahim (AS) said "Allahu Akbar" and pelted him a third time with pebbles
- Shaytan sank into the ground

### The Significance of Rami (Pelting)
- Allah liked this act of Ibrahim Khalilullah (AS) — pelting Shaytan with pebbles — so much
- He included it as a mandatory ritual (Wajib) of Hajj for all time until the end of the world

### Shaytan's Attempt With Hajra (AS)
- Shaytan, distressed by his failure, was wounded by the pebbles in all three attempts
- He devised a new trick
- Disguised as a woman, he rushed to Hajra (AS) and said:
  - "O mother of Ismail, do you know where Ibrahim has taken your beloved son?"
- Hajra (AS) replied: "Yes, they have gone out. Perhaps to meet some friend."
- Shaytan, striking his head, said:
  - "He has taken your son to the valley of Mina to sacrifice him!"
- Hajra (AS) expressed amazement and said:
  - "How is it possible that a kind and merciful father would sacrifice his beloved and only son?"
- At this, the helpless Shaytan blurted out:
  - "He is doing all this by Allah's command!"
- Upon hearing this, the embodiment of patience, gratitude, obedience, and submission — Allah's devoted servant — said:
  - "If this is Allah's command, then not just one Ismail — a hundred Ismails are sacrificed for His command!"

### Shaytan's Despair
- Shaytan returned to Mina disappointed
- But seeing the faith-inspiring scene there, he was compelled to throw dust on his head and beat his face

### The Sacrifice Scene
- The black mountains of Mina, the clear blue sky, the bright sun raining fire — the angels engaged in glorification and praise — all were witnessing a great story of worship, obedience, submission, contentment, patience, and gratitude

### Ismail's Further Instructions
- When Ibrahim (AS) instructed Ismail (AS) to lie down, Ismail (AS) said:
  - "O father, I will lie on my forehead so that your face is not visible to me."
  - "And you also tie a cloth over your eyes, so that when you are sacrificing me, there is no possibility of fatherly affection overcoming the divine command."
- Ibrahim (AS) acted upon this advice
- When Ismail (AS) lay down, Ibrahim (AS) — in the duty of submission to Allah — ran the sharp knife with full force across the neck of his beloved son

### The Divine Intervention
- At this great sacrifice of father and son, Allah's power manifested
- By Allah's command, Hazrat Jibraeel (AS) appeared with a beautiful white ram, raised in the gardens of Paradise
- When Ibrahim Khalilullah (AS), after completing the sacrifice, removed the cloth from his eyes, he was astonished
- In place of his son, a beautiful ram lay sacrificed
- And Ismail (AS) stood nearby, smiling

### The Heavenly Voice
- At that moment, a voice from the unseen called out:
  - "O Ibrahim! You have made your dream come true. Indeed, this is how We reward the doers of good."

### The Legacy of the Sacrifice
- This unparalleled spirit of obedience, submission, courage, and contentment was accepted
- A ransom (fidya) was provided from the unseen
- Allah Almighty liked this act of His two chosen servants so much that He made it a means of worship, obedience, and nearness to Him — to continue until the Day of Judgment

### The Nature of Prophetic Tests
- The matter of those close to the Divine Court is not like that of ordinary humans
- They have to pass through the hardest stages of trial and testing
- At every step, they must demonstrate surrender of life, submission, and contentment (Raza)

### The Prophet's Statement
- The Holy Prophet (SAW) said:
  - "We, the group of Prophets, are placed in the furnace of trials according to our respective ranks."
- Ibrahim (AS), being a Prophet of high stature, had to face various trials
- According to his elevated rank, he proved complete and perfect in every test

### The Three Major Tests of Ibrahim (AS)
- **First Test**: When thrown into the fire — he demonstrated patience and contentment with Allah's decree. That greatness was his alone.
- **Second Test**: When commanded to leave Ismail (AS) and Hajra (AS) in the wilderness of Faran — this was no ordinary test. It was a severe trial. In old age, the fruit of years of prayers, the light of his eyes — Ismail — he left in a waterless, desolate jungle solely to obey Allah's command. And he did not even look back, lest fatherly affection arise and cause any hesitation in fulfilling the divine command.
- **Third Test**: This was even more soul-wrenching than the first two. For three consecutive nights, Ibrahim (AS) saw the dream: "O Ibrahim, sacrifice your only son in Our path."

### The Obedience of Father and Son
- The dreams of Prophets are true visions and divine revelation
- Ibrahim (AS) became an embodiment of contentment and submission, ready to quickly carry out Allah's command
- But this matter did not concern only himself — the other part of this test was the son whose sacrifice was commanded
- So the father told his son about his dream and Allah's command
- The son — being the son of such an elevated Prophet and Messenger — immediately bowed his head in submission
- He said: "If this is Allah's will, then Insha'Allah you will find me among the patient ones."
- And Ibrahim (AS) fully carried out this sacrifice

### The Legacy of Sacrifice — Eid al-Adha
- This is the sacrifice that was so accepted in Allah's court
- It was established as a symbol of the Millat-e-Ibrahimi (The Abrahamic Tradition) as a perpetual memorial
- Even today, on the 10th of Dhul Hijjah, this symbol is celebrated throughout the entire Islamic world in the same manner

## Part 4: The Question of Who Was Sacrificed — Ismail (AS) or Ishaq (AS)?

### The Controversy
- A question often raised regarding this sacrifice is: Who was the Zabih (the sacrificed one)?
- Was it Ismail (AS) or Ishaq (Isaac) (AS)?

### The Quranic Evidence
- The Holy Quran, although it does not name the Zabih, the way the event is described clearly indicates — without any doubt — that the Quranic text points to Ismail (AS) as the Zabih
- This is the true event and reality

### Surah As-Saffat — The Quranic Account
In Surah As-Saffat, the event is described as follows:
- "O my Lord, grant me a righteous son."
- So We gave him glad tidings of a forbearing boy.
- Then when he reached the age to work with his father, Ibrahim said: "O my son, I have seen in a dream that I am sacrificing you. So see what you think."
- He said: "O my father, do what you are commanded. If Allah wills, you will find me among the patient ones."
- Then when they both had submitted and he laid him down on his forehead,
- We called out to him: "O Ibrahim!"
- "You have made the dream come true. Indeed, this is how We reward the doers of good."
- "Indeed, this was the clear trial."
- And We ransomed him with a great sacrifice (a ram).
- And We left for him among the later generations (this mention):
- "Peace be upon Ibrahim."
- Indeed, this is how We reward the doers of good.
- Indeed, he was among Our believing servants.
- And We gave him glad tidings of Ishaq, a Prophet from among the righteous.
- And We blessed him and Ishaq.

### Analysis of the Verses
- These verses mention the glad tidings of two sons of Ibrahim (AS)
- **First son**: Not named — described as "Ghulamin Halim" (a forbearing boy) — and the event of his great sacrifice is narrated
- **Second son**: Named — the glad tidings of Ishaq (AS) are given afterward by name

### Conclusion from the Quranic Order
- It is an established fact that Ibrahim (AS) had two sons: Ismail (AS) and Ishaq (AS)
- Ismail (AS) is the elder, and Ishaq (AS) is the younger
- When the younger son is mentioned by name in the later verse, who else could be mentioned in the earlier verse besides Ismail (AS)?
- Without a doubt, it is Ismail (AS) who, by saying "Insha'Allah min as-Sabirin" (If Allah wills, you will find me among the patient ones), demonstrated the quality of sacrifice and attained the honor of "Zabih" (the sacrificed one)

### Evidence From the Torah
- Not only the Holy Quran declares Ismail (AS) as the Zabih
- If the text of the Torah is carefully studied, it also indicates that Ismail (AS) — and only Ismail (AS) — is the Zabih

### The Jewish Alteration
- The Jews have tried to attach the name of Ishaq (AS) to the title of Zabih
- This is why the question arose: "Who is the Zabih — Ismail or Ishaq?"

### The Torah Text
The Torah states:
> "After these things, God tested Abraham and said to him: 'Take your son, yes, your only son, whom you love — Ishaq — and go to the land of Moriah, and offer him there as a burnt offering on one of the mountains which I will tell you.'"

Then:
> "The angel of the Lord called to Abraham from heaven a second time and said: 'By myself I have sworn, declares the Lord, because you have done this and have not withheld your son, your only son...'"

### Analysis of the Torah's Contradiction
- Notice the contradiction in these two phrases: "your only son" and "your only son"
- Now read those earlier verses of the Torah where Ismail (AS) is described as the "only son" of Ibrahim (AS)
- Because Ismail was 14 years old when Ishaq (AS) was born

### The Clear Conclusion
- Does this not clearly reveal that it was the wrongful greed of associating such a great honor with Bani Israel (the Children of Israel) that led the Jews to tamper with the text?
- They inserted Ishaq's name out of place alongside the phrase "only son"
- This insertion contradicts:
  - The Torah's own text
  - The Quranic text
  - The actual reality of the event

### The Islamic Position
- There is no doubt that the great title of "Zabihullah" (The Sacrificed One of Allah) is reserved exclusively for Ismail (AS)
- This is Allah's favor — He grants it to whom He wills. And Allah is the Possessor of great favor.

### Scholars Who Erred
- It is surprising that some Islamic scholars also appear to fall into this error — claiming the Zabih was not Ismail (AS) but Ishaq (AS)
- The arguments they present in this regard are, unfortunately, not agreeable
- Their foundation is based merely on conjecture and assumption, not on the light of certainty

### Refuting the Weak Argument
- One of their major arguments: In the verses of Surah As-Saffat:
  - The first verse "Basharnahu bi ghulamin halim" (We gave him glad tidings of a forbearing boy) does not mention any name
  - The later verses, while mentioning the sacrifice, say "Wa basharnahu bi Ishaq" (And We gave him glad tidings of Ishaq)
  - They ask: Is not "Ghulamin Halim" the same as Ishaq?

### Detailed Refutation
- The context (Siyaq wa Sibaq) of these verses clearly distinguishes the two
- The first glad tiding is mentioned without a name
- Then, after the entire sacrifice narrative is complete, the glad tiding of Ishaq is introduced separately with the conjunction "wa" (and)
- According to Arabic grammar and eloquence, what scope is there to consider both as the same person?
- Especially when separate qualities are described for each

### Scholarly Opinion
- The author of "Qasas al-Anbiya", Abdul Wahab Najjar, has referred the pronoun in "Wa barakna alaihi wa ala Ishaq" (And We blessed him and Ishaq) back to the Zabih (the sacrificed one)
- He translates it as: "We sent blessings upon that Zabih and upon Ishaq"
- This confirms that the subject of the story is someone other than Ishaq (AS) — and that can only be Ismail (AS)

### Additional Evidence — Location
- This event took place near Makkah in Mina
- The phrase "only son" in the early part of the narrative is living testimony that Ishaq (AS) had not even been born yet at that time
- Therefore, the Torah's claim of this event occurring near Moriah is the same kind of textual corruption — of which the Torah is not free — and denying it is denying the obvious (Bidahat)

### Final Summary
- The real truth is that Hazrat Ismail (AS) alone is the Zabih
- His title is "Zabihullah" (The Sacrificed One of Allah)
- The doubts created around this are manufactured by the Jews due to the distortions in the Torah
- Muslims should not pay attention to such claims
- The event — of Ibrahim's (AS) dream and the sacrifice of Ismail (AS) — as narrated, is the original and true event

## Part 5: The Marriages and Children of Hazrat Ismail (AS)

### The First Marriage
- When several houses were built and Makkah became settled, and Ismail (AS) had grown into a young man during this period
- He established very close and friendly relations with the chiefs of Banu Jurhum
- His mother tongue was not Arabic, while Banu Jurhum were Arabs
- He learned the Arabic language from the people of Banu Jurhum
- From his youth, he was considered honorable and respected in their eyes
- The people of Banu Jurhum began to love him and gave him a girl from their family in marriage
- According to some narrations, the girl's name was Ammarah bint Sa'd bin Usama bin Uqail — from the Banu Amaliyah

### The Death of Hajra (AS)
- During this period, Ismail's (AS) mother, Hazrat Hajra (AS), passed away

### Ibrahim's (AS) First Visit After Marriage
- After Ismail (AS) was married, Ibrahim (AS), as was his custom, came to visit his family
- Ismail (AS) was not at home at that time
- Ibrahim (AS) asked his daughter-in-law: "Where is Ismail?"
- She replied: "He has gone in search of sustenance."
- Ibrahim (AS) asked: "How is your livelihood?"
- She complained and said: "We are passing through very difficult times." She also mentioned many other problems.
- Ibrahim (AS) said: "When your husband returns, convey my salam to him and tell him to change the threshold of his door."
- After giving this message, Ibrahim (AS) departed.

### Ismail's Return and Understanding
- When Ismail (AS) came home, he sensed something and asked his wife: "Did any guest come to you?"
- She said: "Yes, an elderly gentleman came. He asked about you, and I told him. Then he asked about our life, and I told him that we are in great difficulty and hardship."
- Hearing this, Ismail (AS) asked: "Did he leave any advice for you?"
- The wife said: "Yes, he instructed me to convey his salam to you and said that you should change the threshold of your door."
- Ismail (AS) said: "That was my father. And through this message, he has commanded me to separate from you. So go to your family."
- Thus, Ismail (AS) divorced that wife
- According to narrations, no children were born from her yet

### The Second Marriage
- Ismail (AS) then married another girl from the tribe of Banu Jurhum
- Her name was Sayyidah bint Mudad bin Amr al-Jurhumi

### The Children of Ismail (AS)
- According to narrations, from this wife, Ismail (AS) had 12 sons
- Ibn Kathir lists their names as follows:
  1. Nabit
  2. Qaydar
  3. Adhbil
  4. Minshi
  5. Masma
  6. Duma
  7. Arar
  8. Yatur
  9. Nabsh
  10. Tayma
  11. Qaydhuma
  12. Maqar

### The Daughter of Ismail (AS)
- According to the narration of Nabit, these 12 brothers had only one sister
- Her name was Nasma bint Ismail
- Ismail (AS) married his daughter to Al-As bin Ishaq, the son of his brother Ishaq bin Ibrahim
- This shows that despite the distance, the mutual relations between the two brothers were very strong and close
- According to Ibn Kathir, two grandsons of Ismail (AS) became very famous:
  - One named Al-Rum
  - The other named Al-Yunan

### Ibrahim's (AS) Second Visit — The Second Wife
- Ibrahim (AS) would only travel with Allah's permission
- He did not make the next journey to Makkah until Allah granted permission
- After a period, he arrived, and again Ismail (AS) was not at home
- He went to his wife and asked about Ismail (AS)
- She said: "He has gone out in search of sustenance."
- Ibrahim (AS) asked: "How are your days and nights passing?"
- The wife replied: "Alhamdulillah (All praise be to Allah), we are in well-being and peace." And she praised and glorified Allah.
- This answer pleased Ibrahim (AS)

### Further Questions and the Blessed Du'a
- Ibrahim (AS) asked: "What is your food (Ta'am)?"
- She replied: "Meat."
- He asked: "What is your drink (Mashrub)?"
- She replied: "Zamzam water."
- Ibrahim (AS) made du'a: "O Allah, bless their meat and water."
- The Prophet (SAW) later said: "That day, there was no other food item in that house. If there had been anything else, he would have also made du'a for blessing in it."
- The Prophet (SAW) also said: "She was a great example of the greatness and patience-gratitude of a magnificent woman — even though at that moment there was not a single grain of food in the house."
- Because of the patience, trust in Allah (Tawakkul), and the du'a of Khalilullah (Ibrahim AS), everything is found in abundance in Makkah

### Ibrahim's Message for the Second Wife
- While departing, Ibrahim (AS) said to Ismail's (AS) wife:
  - "When your husband returns, convey my salam to him and give him the command from me to keep the threshold of his door firm (Barqarar)."

### Ismail's Return — Second Time
- When Ismail (AS) came home, he asked his wife: "Did any guest come to you?"
- She replied: "Yes, a very handsome and pleasant elderly gentleman came." And she praised him greatly.
- "He asked about you, and I answered him. Then he asked how our life was going, and I told him we are in well-being and peace."
- Ismail (AS) was pleased to hear this
- He asked: "Did he leave any advice?"
- She replied: "Yes, he conveyed salam to you and commanded that you keep the threshold of your door firm."
- Ismail (AS) said: "That was my father, Ibrahim (AS). And the threshold is you. I have been commanded to keep you with me."

### Additional Information
- Narrations state that all the Arabs of Hijaz Muqaddas (Makkah and Madinah) are from the progeny of two sons of Ismail (AS): Qaydar and Nabit
- It is written that the first person in the world to ride a horse was Ismail (AS)
- Before this, horses were wild animals
- Ismail (AS) tamed and domesticated them, and then rode them
- Abdullah bin Umar (RA) narrates that the Prophet (SAW) said:
  - "Adopt horses and ride them, for indeed they are the heritage (Miraath) of your father Ismail (AS)."

### Ismail's (AS) Languages
- Umru Rahmatullah narrates a hadith that the Prophet (SAW) said:
  - "The first person whose tongue spoke clear Arabic was Ismail (AS), and he was 14 years old at that time."
- Besides Arabic, he also had command over other languages, including Hebrew (Ibraani)

## Part 6: The Construction of the Kaaba

### Ismail's Role
- During the construction of the Kaaba, Ismail (AS) worked shoulder to shoulder with his father
- He would hand stones and mortar to his father, who would set them in the wall

### The Stones of the Kaaba
- Narrations state that the stones used in the walls of the Kaaba were collected by Ismail (AS) from five mountains:
  1. Tur-e-Hira
  2. Tur-e-Zaita
  3. Tur-e-Lubnan (Lebanon)
  4. Tur-e-Sina (Sinai)
  5. Tur-e-Judi

### Hajar-e-Aswad (The Black Stone)
- Under the guidance of Jibraeel (AS), Ismail (AS) brought Hajar-e-Aswad from Jabal-e-Abu Qais
- It is a ruby (Yaqoot) from Paradise

### The Quranic Verse
As stated in Surah Al-Baqarah, Verse 127:
> "And when Ibrahim and Ismail were raising the foundations of the House (Kaaba), (they prayed): 'Our Lord, accept this from us. Indeed, You are the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.'"

### Ibrahim's Visits to Makkah
- Although Ibrahim (AS) resided in Palestine, he would regularly come to Makkah to visit Hajra and Ismail (AS)
- During one such visit, Allah commanded Ibrahim (AS) to build the Kaaba
- Ibrahim (AS) discussed this with Ismail (AS), and both father and son began the construction

### The Original Foundation
- Hafiz Ibn Hajar Asqalani (RA) narrated in Fath al-Bari a tradition which indicates:
  - The very first foundation of Baitullah was laid by Hazrat Adam (AS)
  - The angels had shown him the exact location where the Kaaba was to be built
  - However, events over thousands of years had erased its traces
  - It remained only in the form of a mound or raised ground

### Rediscovery
- This was the place that divine revelation showed to Ibrahim (AS)
- When he and Ismail (AS) began digging, the foundations of the previous construction became visible
- Baitullah was rebuilt on those very foundations
- However, the Holy Quran begins the account of Baitullah's construction from Ibrahim (AS) and does not mention the earlier state

### The World Before the Kaaba
- Before this event, in every corner of the world, temples and shrines existed for the worship of idols and stars
- Great structures were built in their names

### Examples of Pre-Kaaba Worship
- **Egypt**: Temples for the Sun God, Idris, Udrasar, Sis, and Baal Devta
- **Assyria**: Built a temple for Baal Devta and erected the Sphinx to display physical grandeur
- **Canaanites**: Built the famous temple of Baal in Baalbek, which remains as a memorial to this day
- **Gaza**: Inhabitants made offerings to Dajan, the Fish Goddess — human-shaped with a fish body
- **Ammonites**: Worshipped the Sun God alongside Astarte (a moon goddess) as a deity, building magnificent temples
- **Persia**: Established the sanctity of fire and built fire temples (Atish Kade)
- **Romans**: Made idols of the Virgin Mary (AS) and adorned churches
- **Hindus**: Believed Mahatma Buddha, Shri Ramchandra, Shri Mahavir, and Mahadev as deities and avatars — and prepared thousands of idols like Kali Devi, Sheetla Devi, Sita Devi, and Parvati Devi, building massive temples at places like Haridwar, Yag Kashpur, Taxila, Sanchi, and Bodh Gaya

### The Uniqueness of the Kaaba
- In contrast to all this, exclusively for the worship of One God (Khuda-e-Wahid) and to bow in submission to His Oneness:
  - The first house among all the idol-temples of the world to be called "The House of God" (Khuda Ka Ghar) is this very Baitullah

### The Quranic Declaration
The Divine Revelation states:
> "Indeed, the first House (of worship) appointed for mankind was that at Bakkah (Makkah) — full of blessing and a source of guidance for all the worlds."

### The Honor of This Construction
- This construction holds the honor that:
  - A Prophet of the stature of Ibrahim (AS) is its architect
  - A Prophet and Zabih (Sacrificed One) like Ismail (AS) is its laborer
  - Father and son are equally engaged in its construction

### Maqam-e-Ibrahim
- When the walls rose high and the elderly father's hand could no longer reach
- By the guidance of divine power, a stone was made as a stepping stone
- Ismail (AS) would support it with his hands, and Ibrahim (AS) would climb upon it to continue building
- This is the memorial known today as "Maqam-e-Ibrahim" (The Station of Ibrahim)

### The Placement of Hajar-e-Aswad
- When the wall reached the point where Hajar-e-Aswad is fixed
- Jibraeel Ameen (AS) guided them and brought out Hajar-e-Aswad, safely preserved, from a mountain
- It is called a stone brought from Paradise

### The House is Completed
- Thus, Baitullah was built
- Allah informed Ibrahim (AS) that this was to be:
  - The Qibla for the Millat-e-Ibrahimi (Abrahamic Nation)
  - A symbol of bowing before Allah
- Therefore, it was designated as the Center of Tawhid (Monotheism)

### The Du'a of Ibrahim and Ismail
- Ibrahim and Ismail (AS) then prayed:
  - That Allah guide them and their progeny to establish prayer (Salat) and give charity (Zakat)
  - That steadfastness be granted
  - That from all the dwellers of the world, guided groups be drawn toward this place
  - That they come from far distances and perform the rituals of Hajj
  - That they gather at this center of guidance and steadfastness, and fill their lives with true direction

### The Quranic Description of the Kaaba's Status
The Holy Quran, in describing Baitullah's construction and the prayers of Ibrahim and Ismail (AS), declares its magnificence:

> "Indeed, the first House (of worship) appointed for mankind is the one at Bakkah (Makkah) — blessed, and a source of guidance for all people."
> "In it are clear signs — the Station of Ibrahim (Maqam-e-Ibrahim)."
> "And whoever enters it shall be safe."
> "And pilgrimage (Hajj) to the House is a duty owed to Allah by all people who are able to undertake it."
> "And whoever disbelieves — then indeed, Allah is free from need of all the worlds."

## Part 7: The Beautiful Du'a of Ibrahim and Ismail (AS)

Allah Almighty has repeatedly mentioned the construction of the Kaaba and the du'a recited by Ibrahim (AS) and Ismail (AS) during its building.

### The Du'a in the Quran
> "And (remember) when We made the House (Kaaba) a place of return and security for mankind. And (We commanded): 'Take the Station of Ibrahim as a place of prayer.'"
> "And We made a covenant with Ibrahim and Ismail: 'Purify My House for those who perform Tawaf, and those who stay in worship (I'tikaf), and those who bow and prostrate (in prayer).'"

> "And (remember) when Ibrahim said: 'My Lord, make this a peaceful city and provide its people with fruits — those among them who believe in Allah and the Last Day.'"
> "He (Allah) said: 'And whoever disbelieves — I will grant him enjoyment for a little while, then I will compel him to the punishment of the Fire. And what an evil destination!'"

> "And (remember) when Ibrahim was raising the foundations of the House, and Ismail with him, (they prayed): 'Our Lord, accept this from us. Indeed, You are the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.'"
> "'Our Lord, make us both submitting (Muslims) to You, and from our descendants a nation submitting to You. And show us our rituals, and accept our repentance. Indeed, You are the Acceptor of Repentance, the Most Merciful.'"
> "'Our Lord, send among them a Messenger from themselves, who will recite to them Your verses, teach them the Book and wisdom, and purify them. Indeed, You are the All-Mighty, the All-Wise.'"

### The Acceptance of the Du'a
- This beautiful and immense du'a of Ibrahim (AS) and Ismail (AS) was accepted
- From the progeny of Ibrahim (AS) and Ismail (AS), Allah raised the beloved Prophet Muhammad (SAW) — the Leader of both worlds — as the fulfillment of this prayer

## Part 8: The Institution of Hajj

Allah Almighty further describes the purpose of the Kaaba:

> "And (remember) when We designated for Ibrahim the site of the House: 'Do not associate anything with Me, and purify My House for those who perform Tawaf, those who stand in prayer, and those who bow and prostrate.'"
> "And proclaim the Hajj to mankind. They will come to you on foot and on every lean camel — coming from every distant mountain pass."
> "So that they may witness benefits for themselves, and mention the name of Allah on known days over the cattle He has provided for them. Then eat of them and feed the poor and distressed."
> "Then let them complete their prescribed duties, fulfill their vows, and circle around the Ancient House (Kaaba)."

### The Spirit of Sacrifice
> "It is neither their meat nor their blood that reaches Allah, but what reaches Him is your piety (Taqwa). Thus He has made them subservient to you, so that you may glorify Allah for guiding you. And give glad tidings to the doers of good."
