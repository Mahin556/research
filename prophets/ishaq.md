# The Life of Prophet Ishaq (Isaac) (AS) and Prophet Yaqub (Jacob) (AS) — Detailed Notes

---

## Part 1: Introduction to Prophet Ishaq (AS)

### The Divine System of Guidance
- Allah Almighty sent approximately 124,000 Prophets (AS) for the guidance of humanity
- Among these noble figures, Prophet Ishaq (AS) holds a distinguished and honored position
- He was the son of Prophet Ibrahim (AS) (Abraham), the Friend of Allah (Khalilullah)
- He was the younger brother of Prophet Ismail (AS) (Ishmael), the Sacrificed One (Zabihullah)
- He was the father of Prophet Yaqub (AS) (Jacob), also known as Israel

### The Miraculous Birth of Ishaq (AS)
- Prophet Ibrahim (AS) was 100 years old at the time of Ishaq's (AS) birth
- His wife, Sarah (AS), was 90 years old
- By all natural laws, they were far beyond the age of having children
- At that time, Ibrahim (AS) was residing in the region of Sham (Greater Syria/Palestine)
- His elder son, Ismail (AS), was already 14 years old and living in Makkah Mukarramah with his mother Hajra (AS)

### The Visit of the Angels
- One day, Allah sent a group of angels to earth
- Their primary mission was to descend upon the people of Lut (AS) with divine punishment — that nation had transgressed all limits
- However, before going to Sodom, the angels first stopped at the house of Ibrahim (AS)
- They appeared in human form — as three handsome male travelers

### Ibrahim's (AS) Hospitality
- Prophet Ibrahim (AS) saw three strangers approaching and immediately extended hospitality
- He invited them into his home with honor and respect
- He prepared a roasted calf and placed it before them
- However, to his surprise and concern, the guests did not extend their hands toward the food
- In the culture of that time, if a guest refused to eat, it was a sign of ill intent or hostility
- Anxiety appeared on Ibrahim's (AS) face

### The Angels Reveal Their Identity
- Sensing his distress, the angels immediately reassured him
- They said: "Do not be afraid. We are angels sent by Allah."
- They then revealed the purpose of their visit:
  1. They were going to destroy the nation of Lut (AS)
  2. They brought glad tidings — Sarah (AS) would bear a son

### The Glad Tidings of Ishaq (AS)
- The angels said: "Allah is granting you a son. You shall name him Ishaq."
- They further stated that this son would himself be a Prophet
- They also gave the additional glad tiding that from Ishaq (AS) would be born Yaqub (AS), who would also be a Prophet
- This meant Sarah (AS) was being told not only of her son, but of her grandson as well

### Sarah's (AS) Astonishment
- When Sarah (AS) heard this news, she was utterly astonished
- She exclaimed in amazement, as recorded in the Quran (Surah Hud, 11:72):
  - "Woe to me! Shall I bear a child when I am an old woman and my husband is an old man? This is indeed a strange thing!"
- She was expressing natural human disbelief — not in Allah's power, but in the apparent physical impossibility
- Her reaction was one of joyful shock mixed with wonder

### The Angels' Response
- The angels replied (Surah Hud, 11:73):
  - "Are you amazed at the decree of Allah? May the mercy of Allah and His blessings be upon you, O people of the house. Indeed, He is Praiseworthy and Glorious."

### Ibrahim's (AS) Reaction
- Prophet Ibrahim (AS) was equally amazed at the news
- He too was extremely old and had long passed the age of fatherhood
- He said to the angels (Surah Al-Hijr, 15:54-55):
  - "Do you give me glad tidings when old age has overtaken me? Of what then do you give glad tidings?"
- The angels replied:
  - "We give you glad tidings in truth. Do not be of those who despair."

### The Birth of Ishaq (AS)
- In due course, the divine promise was fulfilled
- Sarah (AS) conceived and, after nine months, gave birth to a beautiful son
- As commanded, Ibrahim (AS) named him Ishaq
- The birth occurred 14 years after the birth of Ismail (AS)
- This was the same year that Ibrahim (AS) and Ismail (AS) constructed the Kaaba in Makkah

### Ibrahim's (AS) Gratitude
- Prophet Ibrahim (AS) was overwhelmed with gratitude to Allah
- After waiting an entire lifetime, he had been blessed with not one but two righteous sons
- The Quran records his prayer of gratitude (Surah Ibrahim, 14:39):
  - "All praise is due to Allah, who has granted me in old age Ismail and Ishaq. Indeed, my Lord is the Hearer of supplication."

### The Quranic Testimony to Ishaq's (AS) Prophethood
- The Quran itself testifies that Ishaq (AS) was a Prophet
- Allah says (Surah As-Saffat, 37:112):
  - "And We gave him glad tidings of Ishaq — a Prophet from among the righteous."
- In another verse (Surah Al-Ankabut, 29:27):
  - "And We granted him Ishaq and Yaqub, and We placed Prophethood and the Scripture in his descendants."
- This establishes that the prophetic lineage continued through Ishaq (AS)

### The Blessings Upon Ishaq (AS) and His Progeny
- Allah blessed Ishaq (AS) and his descendants abundantly
- The Quran states (Surah As-Saffat, 37:113):
  - "And We blessed him and Ishaq. And among their descendants are those who do good, and those who clearly wrong themselves."
- From his lineage came countless Prophets — including Yaqub, Yusuf, Musa, Harun, Dawud, Sulayman, Zakariya, Yahya, Isa, and many others (AS)
- Almost all the Prophets mentioned in the Quran after Ibrahim (AS) descended from Ishaq (AS), with the exception of Prophet Muhammad (SAW) who descended from Ismail (AS)

## Part 2: The Upbringing and Character of Ishaq (AS)

### His Childhood and Youth
- Ishaq (AS) grew up as an obedient and devoted son
- He was raised in the household of Ibrahim (AS) — the Friend of Allah — and received the finest spiritual and moral education
- He witnessed his father's unwavering faith and dedication to Tawhid (monotheism)
- He learned the principles of worship, sacrifice, and complete submission to Allah
- He was known for his diligence, hard work, and righteous character

### The Promise Fulfilled
- As the angels had foretold, Ishaq (AS) was chosen by Allah to be a Prophet
- He continued the mission of his father — calling people to the worship of One God
- He preached to the people of Kan'an (Canaan) — the land where he lived

### Ibrahim's (AS) Concern for His Son's Marriage
- As Ishaq (AS) reached marriageable age, Ibrahim (AS) became deeply concerned
- He was now extremely old and felt his time in this world was limited
- He wanted to see his son married and settled before he passed away
- However, there was a critical condition in his mind

### The Condition — No Marriage with the Canaanites
- Ibrahim (AS) was adamant that Ishaq (AS) should not marry a woman from the Canaanite community
- The reason was profound and spiritual:
  - The Canaanites were idol worshippers
  - They did not believe in Allah
  - Marrying into such a community could potentially compromise the faith of future generations
  - The prophetic lineage needed to be preserved in its purity and devotion to Tawhid

### The Search for a Righteous Wife
- Ibrahim (AS) decided to find a wife for Ishaq (AS) from among his own extended family
- His family, though living elsewhere, still maintained belief in One God
- He summoned his most trusted servant — a man known for his loyalty and wisdom
- He gave the servant a solemn mission: travel to Harran (in modern-day Turkey/Syria region) and find a suitable wife from among his relatives

### The Oath and Instructions
- Ibrahim (AS) made his servant swear an oath
- The conditions were clear:
  1. The bride must be from Ibrahim's (AS) own family, not from the Canaanites
  2. Under no circumstances should Ishaq (AS) marry a Canaanite woman
  3. The servant should not take Ishaq (AS) back to the old homeland — his future was in the blessed land

### The Servant's Journey to Harran
- The servant prepared a caravan with camels loaded with gifts
- He traveled for many days across the desert
- The journey was long and arduous
- He finally reached the region of Harran, specifically a place called Nahor

### The Prayer at the Well
- Upon arriving, the servant stopped at a well outside the city
- It was evening — the time when women came to draw water
- He made a heartfelt supplication to Allah:
  - "O Allah, grant me success in this mission. Let the right woman show herself through a sign."
  - He asked that the chosen woman would not only give him water but also offer to water his camels — a sign of exceptional kindness and generosity

### The Meeting with Rebekah (Rifqa)
- As he finished his prayer, a young woman approached the well
- She was beautiful, modest, and carried herself with dignity
- The servant asked her for water
- She immediately gave him water and then, without being asked, said: "I will also draw water for your camels until they have had enough to drink."
- This was the exact sign the servant had prayed for

### Rebekah's (AS) Identity
- The servant inquired about her family
- He learned that she was Rebekah (Rifqa), the daughter of Bethuel, who was the son of Nahor
- Nahor was the brother of Ibrahim (AS)
- This meant Rebekah was the granddaughter of Ibrahim's (AS) brother — exactly the family connection he was seeking

### The Proposal
- The servant was invited to their home
- He explained his mission — that he had come on behalf of Ibrahim (AS) to seek a wife for his son Ishaq (AS)
- He recounted the miraculous birth of Ishaq (AS) and the status of Ibrahim (AS) as a Prophet
- He presented the gifts Ibrahim (AS) had sent
- The family recognized this as a divine arrangement
- They agreed to the marriage

### The Marriage of Ishaq (AS) and Rebekah (AS)
- Rebekah (AS) agreed to travel back with the servant
- The journey back to Canaan was undertaken
- When they arrived, Ishaq (AS) was walking in the fields at evening time
- He saw the caravan approaching
- Rebekah (AS) saw him and asked who he was
- When she learned it was Ishaq (AS), she covered herself with her veil — a sign of her modesty
- The marriage was solemnized
- Ishaq (AS) was 40 years old at the time of his marriage
- The union brought him great comfort and companionship

## Part 3: The Children of Ishaq (AS) — The Twin Sons

### The Long Wait for Children
- Despite the blessed marriage, years passed and Rebekah (AS) remained childless
- This was a source of deep sorrow for both of them
- Ishaq (AS) continuously prayed to Allah for offspring
- He made heartfelt supplications, knowing that Prophethood was destined for his lineage
- Like his father before him, he was being tested with patience

### The Divine Gift — 20 Years Later
- After 20 years of marriage, Allah answered Ishaq's (AS) prayers
- Rebekah (AS) conceived — and the pregnancy was extraordinary
- She was carrying twins
- Even in the womb, the children seemed to be struggling with each other
- Rebekah (AS) was concerned and asked Allah about this unusual experience

### The Divine Revelation About the Twins
- Allah revealed to her that she was carrying two sons
- These two sons would be the fathers of two great nations
- However, the younger would surpass the elder in status
- The elder would serve the younger
- This was a reversal of the normal order — a divine indication of what was to come

### The Birth of Esau (Ees) and Yaqub (Jacob)
- When the time came, Rebekah (AS) gave birth to twin sons
- The firstborn was covered with red hair — they named him Esau (called 'Ees' or 'Eis' in Arabic)
- The second came out grasping his brother's heel — they named him Yaqub (Jacob)
- The name "Yaqub" is derived from the Arabic root meaning "heel" or "one who follows"

### The Characteristics of Esau
- Esau grew up to be a strong, physically imposing man
- He was a skilled and passionate hunter
- He loved the outdoors and spent his days in the fields and forests
- He was his father Ishaq's (AS) favorite son — perhaps because he brought game meat that Ishaq (AS) particularly enjoyed
- However, he was impulsive, emotional, and less inclined toward spiritual matters

### The Characteristics of Yaqub (AS)
- Yaqub (AS) was different from his brother
- He was more refined, intelligent, and contemplative
- He preferred to stay near the tents and dwellings — indicating a domestic and thoughtful nature
- He was deeply spiritual and concerned with matters of faith
- He was his mother Rebekah's (AS) favorite
- Allah had chosen him to be a Prophet — a fact that may have influenced his mother's special affection

### The Rivalry Between the Brothers
- As they grew older, a rivalry developed between the two brothers
- Esau was jealous of Yaqub (AS) because:
  - He sensed that Yaqub was favored by their mother
  - He knew instinctively that Yaqub had a special spiritual status
  - He felt that Yaqub was a threat to his position as the firstborn
- The jealousy grew day by day
- It created a tension in the household that would eventually lead to a major crisis

### The Rights of the Firstborn — Background
- In the tradition of Ibrahim (AS), the firstborn son held special rights and privileges:
  - He received the primary blessing from the father
  - He inherited a double portion of the estate
  - He became the spiritual and temporal leader of the family after the father's death
  - In this family, the firstborn would also carry the prophetic legacy
- Esau, being the firstborn, was technically entitled to these rights
- However, Allah's will was different — the blessing and Prophethood were destined for Yaqub (AS)

## Part 4: The Blessing Incident — How Yaqub (AS) Received the Primary Blessing

### Ishaq's (AS) Old Age and Blindness
- Prophet Ishaq (AS) lived a long life
- In his advanced years, his eyesight deteriorated significantly
- He became completely blind (Nabeena)
- He was elderly, frail, and could no longer distinguish between people by sight
- He relied on other senses — particularly sound, touch, and smell — to identify people

### Ishaq's (AS) Craving for Game Meat
- One day, Ishaq (AS) developed a strong desire for fresh game meat
- He called his elder son Esau and expressed his wish
- He said: "O my son, go out and hunt some game for me. Prepare the meat the way I like it. When you return and I have eaten, I will give you my special blessing before I die."
- This was the blessing of Prophethood, leadership, and divine favor — the blessing that would determine the spiritual destiny of his descendants

### Esau Departs for the Hunt
- Esau, eager to receive his father's blessing, immediately set out
- He took his bow and arrows and went into the wilderness
- Hunting was his passion, and he was confident he would return quickly with the best game
- However, finding, killing, and preparing a wild animal takes considerable time — even for a skilled hunter

### Rebekah (AS) Overhears the Conversation
- Rebekah (AS) had been nearby and had overheard the entire conversation between Ishaq (AS) and Esau
- She knew that the blessing in question was of immense spiritual significance
- She also knew — through the divine revelation she had received during pregnancy — that Yaqub (AS) was destined for greatness
- She believed that the blessing rightfully belonged to Yaqub (AS) according to Allah's decree
- She decided to take action

### Rebekah's (AS) Plan
- She hurried to Yaqub (AS) and told him everything she had heard
- She said: "Your father has sent your brother to hunt and bring game. After eating, he will give Esau the special blessing. You must act now before your brother returns."
- She instructed him:
  1. "Go quickly to our flock and bring me two of the best young goats."
  2. "I will prepare the meat exactly the way your father likes it."
  3. "You will take it to your father before Esau returns."
  4. "This way, you will receive the blessing instead of your brother."

### Yaqub's (AS) Hesitation
- Yaqub (AS) was initially reluctant
- He raised a practical concern: "Esau is a hairy man. My skin is smooth. If my father touches me, he will know that I am not Esau. He will think I am trying to deceive him, and instead of a blessing, I will bring a curse upon myself."
- This shows that Yaqub (AS) was not naturally deceitful — he was genuinely worried about being caught

### Rebekah's (AS) Solution
- Rebekah (AS) had already thought of this
- She said: "Let any curse fall on me, not on you. Just do as I say."
- She took goat skins and placed them on Yaqub's (AS) hands and the back of his neck
- She dressed him in Esau's best clothes — which had Esau's scent on them
- She prepared the goat meat precisely to Ishaq's (AS) taste
- She gave him bread she had baked
- Thus prepared, Yaqub (AS) approached his father

### Yaqub (AS) Enters His Father's Room
- Yaqub (AS) entered the room where his blind father sat
- He spoke in a deep voice, approximating his brother's tone
- He said: "My father, I am your son. Please sit up and eat the game meat I have brought. Then give me your blessing."

### Ishaq's (AS) Suspicion
- Ishaq (AS) was surprised at how quickly "Esau" had returned
- He said: "How did you find game and prepare it so quickly, my son?"
- Yaqub (AS) replied: "Allah brought the game to me swiftly."
- Ishaq (AS) was still unsure. He said: "Come close, my son, so that I may touch you and know if you are truly my son Esau or not."

### The Test of Touch
- Yaqub (AS) came close
- Ishaq (AS) touched the hands and felt the goat skin — it felt like Esau's hairy hands
- He was confused because the voice sounded somewhat like Yaqub, but the hands felt like Esau
- He said: "The voice is the voice of Yaqub, but the hands are the hands of Esau."

### The Test of Smell
- Still uncertain, Ishaq (AS) asked his son to come even closer
- He smelled Esau's clothing which Yaqub (AS) was wearing — it carried the scent of the fields and the outdoors
- He smelled the goat skins — which had the musky smell of animals
- His senses of touch and smell combined convinced him this was Esau

### The Blessing Is Given
- Ishaq (AS) ate the food that was brought
- He was pleased with the taste
- Then, with full conviction that he was blessing his elder son, he began to pray and pronounce the blessing:
  - "May Allah give you of the dew of heaven and the richness of the earth — abundance of grain and new wine."
  - "May nations serve you, and peoples bow down to you."
  - "Be lord over your brothers, and may the sons of your mother bow down to you."
  - "May those who curse you be cursed, and those who bless you be blessed."
- This was the comprehensive blessing — encompassing material prosperity, political dominion, and spiritual leadership

### Yaqub (AS) Leaves
- Having received the blessing, Yaqub (AS) left his father's room
- He had barely departed when Esau returned from his hunt

### Esau Returns
- Esau had successfully hunted game, prepared it, and now approached his father with the food
- He entered the room and said: "My father, sit up and eat the game your son has brought. Then give me your blessing."

### The Shocking Realization
- Ishaq (AS) was startled and confused
- He asked: "Who are you?"
- Esau replied: "I am your son, your firstborn — Esau."
- Ishaq (AS) began to tremble violently
- He said: "Then who was it that came just before you and brought me food? I ate it and I blessed him. And indeed, he shall be blessed!"

### Esau's Reaction
- When Esau heard these words, he let out a loud and bitter cry
- He was devastated, angry, and heartbroken
- He cried out: "Bless me too, my father! Do you have only one blessing?"
- He reminded his father: "Isn't he rightly named Yaqub (the heel-grabber)? He has deceived me twice. He took my birthright, and now he has taken my blessing!"

### Ishaq's (AS) Response
- Ishaq (AS) was deeply troubled, but he recognized that the blessing, once given in Allah's name, could not be revoked
- He said: "I have already made him lord over you, and I have given him all his brothers as servants. I have sustained him with grain and new wine. What then can I do for you, my son?"
- The blessing was irrevocable — it was a prophetic pronouncement, not merely a father's wish

### The Supplementary Blessing for Esau
- Esau continued to weep and plead
- Moved by his son's tears, Ishaq (AS) gave him a supplementary blessing:
  - "Your dwelling will be away from the richness of the earth — in the desert and wild places."
  - "You shall live by your sword."
  - "You shall serve your brother."
  - "But when you grow restless, you will break his yoke from your neck."
- This blessing foretold a life of struggle and eventual independence for Esau's descendants

### Esau's Wrath and Threat
- Esau was consumed with rage and hatred toward his brother
- He said in his heart: "The days of mourning for my father are near. After my father dies, I will kill my brother Yaqub."
- He was waiting for Ishaq (AS) to pass away before executing his revenge

## Part 5: Yaqub's (AS) Flight to Harran

### Rebekah (AS) Learns of the Threat
- Word of Esau's murderous intentions reached Rebekah (AS)
- She was terrified for Yaqub's (AS) life
- She knew Esau was a man of physical strength and impulsive anger — he would likely carry out his threat

### Rebekah (AS) Advises Yaqub (AS) to Flee
- She called Yaqub (AS) urgently and said:
  - "Your brother Esau is planning to kill you to avenge himself."
  - "You must leave immediately. Go to my brother Laban in Harran."
  - "Stay with him for a while until your brother's anger subsides."
  - "When he has forgotten what you did to him, I will send for you to return."
  - "Why should I lose both my sons in one day?" — meaning if Esau killed Yaqub, Esau would be executed for murder

### The Father-Son Discussion
- Rebekah (AS) also approached Ishaq (AS) about the situation
- She framed it differently, not wanting to distress her blind, elderly husband with news of fraternal murder plots
- She expressed concern that Yaqub (AS) might also marry a Canaanite woman like Esau had
- Ishaq (AS) agreed that Yaqub (AS) should go to Harran to find a wife from among his maternal family
- This gave the journey a legitimate purpose beyond escaping danger

### Ishaq's (AS) Blessing for Yaqub's (AS) Journey
- Before Yaqub (AS) departed, Ishaq (AS) called him and gave him a formal blessing for his journey:
  - He repeated and confirmed the Abrahamic blessing upon him
  - He prayed that Allah would make him fruitful and multiply his descendants
  - He specifically mentioned that the blessing of Ibrahim (AS) — including the promised land — would pass through Yaqub (AS)

### The Departure
- Yaqub (AS) bid farewell to his mother and his blind father
- He took provisions for the long journey
- He set out from Beersheba (in southern Canaan) toward Harran (in northern Mesopotamia)
- He was leaving behind everything — his home, his parents, his comfortable life
- He was now a fugitive, a traveler heading into the unknown

---

## Part 6: Yaqub's (AS) Journey and the Vision at Bethel

### The Wilderness Journey
- Yaqub (AS) traveled alone through the wilderness
- He followed the route that would later become the main highway between Canaan and Mesopotamia
- The journey was hundreds of miles through harsh, semi-desert terrain
- He walked for many days

### The Evening at Luz (Later Bethel)
- One evening, the sun set, and Yaqub (AS) was exhausted
- He was at a place called Luz
- He decided to stop and rest for the night
- He had no tent, no bed, no comfort
- He found a stone and placed it under his head as a pillow
- He lay down on the bare ground and fell into a deep sleep

### The Magnificent Dream
- During that night, Yaqub (AS) experienced one of the most profound visions in prophetic history
- In his dream, he saw:
  - A ladder (or staircase) whose base was on the earth
  - Its top reached all the way to the heavens — to the very throne of Allah
  - Angels were ascending and descending upon it continuously
  - This symbolized the connection between earth and heaven
  - It showed that despite being a lonely fugitive, Yaqub (AS) was connected to the divine realm

### The Divine Address
- In the dream, Allah Himself spoke to Yaqub (AS)
- Standing above the ladder, Allah said:
  - "I am the Lord, the God of your father Ibrahim and the God of Ishaq."
  - "The land on which you are lying — I will give it to you and your descendants."
  - "Your descendants shall be as numerous as the dust of the earth."
  - "You shall spread out to the west and to the east, to the north and to the south."
  - "All the families of the earth shall be blessed through you and your descendants."
  - "Behold, I am with you and will protect you wherever you go."
  - "I will bring you back to this land."
  - "I will not leave you until I have done what I have promised you."
- This was a direct, personal covenant from Allah to Yaqub (AS)
- It confirmed that the Abrahamic promise was now his

### Yaqub (AS) Awakens
- Yaqub (AS) woke from his sleep with a start
- He was filled with awe, wonder, and a sense of the divine presence
- He exclaimed:
  - "Surely the Lord is in this place, and I was not aware of it!"
  - "How awesome is this place!"
  - "This is none other than the House of God!"
  - "This is the Gate of Heaven!"

### The Memorial Stone
- Early in the morning, Yaqub (AS) took the stone he had used as a pillow
- He set it up as a pillar — a memorial marker
- He poured oil over the top of it — an act of consecration
- This was an ancient way of marking a sacred site

### Naming the Place — Bethel (Bayt-il)
- Yaqub (AS) renamed the place
- Originally called Luz, he called it "Bethel" — meaning "The House of God" in Hebrew
- In Arabic, this is "Bayt-il" or "Baytullah" — the same root as the name of the Kaaba
- This place — Bethel — would later become one of the most significant holy sites in the history of the Children of Israel

### Yaqub's (AS) Vow
- Standing before the stone pillar, Yaqub (AS) made a solemn vow to Allah:
  1. If Allah would be with him and protect him on his journey
  2. If Allah would give him food to eat and clothes to wear
  3. If he returned safely to his father's house
  4. Then:
     - Allah would be his God
     - This stone would be God's house (a place of worship)
     - He would give a tenth (tithe) of everything Allah gave him back to Allah
- This is one of the earliest recorded instances of the practice of tithing (giving one-tenth)

### The Significance of the Dream — The Ladder
- The dream of the ladder (called "Jacob's Ladder" in Judeo-Christian tradition) is rich in symbolism:
  - It represents the connection between heaven and earth
  - The angels ascending and descending represent divine communication
  - It confirmed that Prophethood and revelation would continue through Yaqub's (AS) line
  - Some Islamic scholars interpret it as a vision of the future — that from Yaqub's (AS) descendants would arise Prophets who would ascend to receive revelation and descend to guide humanity
  - It established Bethel as a sacred sanctuary

## Part 7: Yaqub (AS) Arrives in Harran

### Completion of the Journey
- After many days of travel, Yaqub (AS) finally reached the region of Harran
- He was exhausted but hopeful
- He stopped near a well outside the city

### The Meeting at the Well
- Shepherds were gathered at the well with their flocks
- They were waiting for all the shepherds to arrive so they could collectively roll away the large stone covering the well
- Yaqub (AS) approached them and asked: "Do you know Laban, the son of Nahor?"
- They replied: "Yes, we know him well. He is well. And look — here comes his daughter Rachel with the sheep."

### Rachel (AS)
- Rachel (Rahil) was approaching with her father's flock
- She was a shepherdess — indicating she was hardworking and not afraid of labor
- Yaqub (AS) saw her and was immediately drawn to her
- In a gesture of strength and helpfulness, he rolled away the heavy stone from the well by himself and watered her flock
- He then introduced himself — he was her cousin, the son of her aunt Rebekah

### Rachel's Reaction
- Rachel ran home and told her father the news
- Laban hurried out to meet his nephew
- He embraced Yaqub (AS) and welcomed him with joy
- He said: "You are my own flesh and blood!"
- He brought him into his home

### Yaqub's (AS) New Life in Harran
- Yaqub (AS) settled into life in his uncle's household
- He was not one to be idle — he immediately began helping with the work
- He proved himself to be diligent, honest, and hardworking
- Laban soon recognized that Yaqub (AS) was an exceptional worker — everything prospered under his care

### The Love for Rachel
- During this time, Yaqub (AS) grew to love Rachel deeply
- She possessed both beauty and good character
- He desired to marry her

### The Marriage Contract
- After some time, Yaqub (AS) approached Laban with a proposal
- He said: "I wish to marry your daughter Rachel."
- Laban, seeing an opportunity, set a condition:
  - "Work for me for seven years, and I will give you Rachel as your wife."
- In the custom of the time, this was a form of bride price (Mahr) — the groom would work to prove his worth and provide for the marriage

### The Seven Years of Labor
- Yaqub (AS) agreed to the terms
- He worked for seven full years
- He tended Laban's flocks faithfully
- Under his care, the flocks multiplied and prospered
- The years passed, but his love for Rachel never wavered
- The Torah beautifully states: "So Yaqub served seven years for Rachel, and they seemed to him but a few days because of the love he had for her."

### The Wedding Night — The Deception
- After seven years, Yaqub (AS) approached Laban and said: "Give me my wife, for my term is complete."
- Laban arranged a great wedding feast
- People gathered from all around to celebrate
- That night, the bride was brought to Yaqub (AS), veiled according to custom
- The wedding was completed

### The Morning Discovery
- When morning came and light entered the tent, Yaqub (AS) made a shocking discovery
- The woman beside him was not Rachel — it was Leah (Lia), the elder daughter
- Leah had weak eyesight and was not as beautiful as Rachel
- Yaqub (AS) had been deceived — just as he had once deceived his own father in the darkness

### The Confrontation
- Yaqub (AS) went to Laban, angry and hurt
- He said: "What is this you have done to me? Did I not serve you for Rachel? Why have you deceived me?"
- Laban calmly replied:
  - "It is not the custom in our country to give the younger daughter in marriage before the elder."
  - "Complete the bridal week with Leah, and then I will give you Rachel as well."
  - "But you must work for me another seven years for Rachel."

### The Lesson of Divine Justice
- Many scholars note the poetic justice in this event
- Yaqub (AS), who had once deceived his father and taken the blessing meant for his elder brother, was now himself deceived
- The elder daughter was placed in the position meant for the younger
- It was a reminder that actions have consequences, and Allah's justice is precise

### The Second Marriage
- Yaqub (AS) agreed to Laban's terms
- After completing the week with Leah, he married Rachel as well
- He then worked another seven years
- Thus, he had two wives — Leah and Rachel
- Polygamy was permitted in the divine law of that time

### The Children of Yaqub (AS)
- Over the years, Allah blessed Yaqub (AS) with twelve sons and at least one daughter
- These twelve sons would become the progenitors of the Twelve Tribes of Israel

### The Special Status of Yusuf (AS)
- Yusuf (Joseph) was born to Rachel after many years of childlessness
- He was exceptionally beautiful in appearance and character
- Yaqub (AS) loved him with a special love
- Yusuf (AS) would later become a Prophet and play a pivotal role in the history of the Children of Israel

## Part 8: Yaqub's (AS) Return to Canaan

### The Decision to Return
- After 20 years in Harran (14 years of service plus additional years), Yaqub (AS) had become a wealthy man
- Through divine blessing, his flocks had multiplied exponentially
- He now had a large family, many servants, and substantial wealth
- Allah commanded him to return to the land of his fathers — Canaan

### The Secret Departure
- Yaqub (AS) decided to leave without informing Laban
- He feared that Laban might try to prevent him or take back some of what he had earned
- While Laban was away shearing sheep, Yaqub (AS) gathered his family, flocks, and possessions
- They crossed the Euphrates River and headed toward Canaan

### Laban's Pursuit
- Laban learned of Yaqub's (AS) departure three days later
- He gathered his men and pursued
- He caught up with Yaqub (AS) after seven days in the hill country of Gilead

### The Covenant at Mizpah
- Allah appeared to Laban in a dream, warning him not to harm Yaqub (AS)
- When Laban finally confronted Yaqub (AS), there was an initial argument
- However, they eventually made a peace covenant
- They set up a heap of stones as a witness
- They agreed: "May Allah watch between you and me when we are absent from each other."
- They shared a meal and Laban blessed his daughters and grandchildren
- The next morning, Laban departed and returned home

### Preparing to Meet Esau
- As Yaqub (AS) continued his journey, he knew he would soon face his brother Esau
- Twenty years had passed, but he did not know if Esau's anger had subsided
- He sent messengers ahead to Esau with a humble and diplomatic message
- The messengers returned with alarming news: Esau was coming to meet him — with 400 men

### Yaqub's (AS) Fear and Prayer
- Yaqub (AS) was greatly distressed
- He divided his people into two camps, hoping that if one was attacked, the other might escape
- He then turned to Allah in profound prayer:
  - He acknowledged his unworthiness of Allah's kindness
  - He reminded Allah of His promise to protect him
  - He pleaded for deliverance from his brother's wrath

### The Gifts for Esau
- Yaqub (AS) prepared a massive gift for Esau:
  - Hundreds of goats, sheep, camels, cattle, and donkeys
  - He divided them into separate droves and sent them ahead with servants
  - He instructed each servant to present the animals as a gift from "your servant Yaqub" to "my lord Esau"
  - He hoped this gesture would soften his brother's heart before the actual meeting

### The Night of Wrestling
- The night before meeting Esau, Yaqub (AS) sent his family across the Jabbok River
- He remained alone on the other side
- During that night, a mysterious being appeared and wrestled with him until daybreak
- It was an angel in human form (or, according to some traditions, a divine manifestation)
- The being could not overcome Yaqub (AS) but touched his hip socket, causing a permanent limp
- As dawn broke, the being said: "Let me go, for the day breaks."
- Yaqub (AS) replied: "I will not let you go unless you bless me."

### The New Name — Israel
- The being asked: "What is your name?"
- He answered: "Yaqub."
- The being said: "Your name shall no longer be called Yaqub (the heel-grabber, the supplanter), but Israel (Isra'il) — for you have struggled with God and with men, and have prevailed."
- This is one of the most profound moments in prophetic history:
  - "Isra" means "to struggle/strive" and "Il" means "God"
  - "Israel" means "One who struggles with/for God" or "God's contender"
  - This became his prophetic name and the name of the nation that descended from him — Bani Israel (The Children of Israel)

### The Limp — A Reminder
- As the sun rose, Yaqub (AS) crossed the river — now limping
- The injured hip was a permanent physical reminder of that sacred night
- It symbolized that even Prophets carry marks of their human struggles
- The Children of Israel traditionally do not eat the sinew of the hip socket — commemorating this event

### The Reunion with Esau
- In the morning, Yaqub (AS) saw Esau approaching with his 400 men
- He arranged his family strategically — placing the most beloved Rachel and Yusuf (AS) at the rear, most protected
- He went ahead of everyone and bowed to the ground seven times as he approached his brother
- This was an act of humility — showing respect to the elder brother he had wronged

### Esau's Response
- Contrary to all of Yaqub's (AS) fears, Esau ran to meet him
- He embraced him, fell on his neck, and kissed him
- Both brothers wept
- Twenty years of separation and anger dissolved in tears of reconciliation

### The Meeting of the Families
- Esau looked up and saw the women and children approaching
- He asked: "Who are these with you?"
- Yaqub (AS) introduced his family — his wives, his handmaids, and his children
- They all bowed before Esau

### The Gift Refused and Accepted
- Esau initially refused the generous gift: "I have enough, my brother. Keep what you have."
- Yaqub (AS) insisted: "Please accept this gift, for to see your face is like seeing the face of God, since you have received me favorably."
- This was not flattery — Yaqub (AS) genuinely saw the mercy in his brother's eyes
- Esau finally accepted

### The Parting
- Esau offered to accompany Yaqub (AS) or leave men to protect him
- Yaqub (AS) politely declined, saying his young children and nursing animals needed to travel slowly
- They parted peacefully
- Esau returned to Seir (the region that would become Edom)
- Yaqub (AS) continued his journey into Canaan

## Part 9: Yaqub (AS) Settles in Canaan

### The Journey to Shechem
- Yaqub (AS) traveled to the city of Shechem in Canaan
- He bought a piece of land there — the first land in the promised territory that belonged to him personally
- He pitched his tents and settled his family

### The Altar at Bethel
- Allah commanded Yaqub (AS) to go to Bethel and build an altar
- This was the same Bethel where he had seen the ladder dream years before
- Before the journey, Yaqub (AS) told his household:
  - "Get rid of the foreign gods among you"
  - "Purify yourselves"
  - "Change your garments"
  - "Let us go to Bethel and build an altar to the God who answered me in the day of my distress"
- This shows that some household members may have brought idols from Harran
- Yaqub (AS), as the Prophet of Allah, was purifying his household from any trace of shirk (idolatry)
- They buried all the idols and the earrings associated with them under an oak tree near Shechem

### The Journey to Bethel
- As they traveled to Bethel, a divine terror fell upon the surrounding cities
- No one pursued or attacked them — Allah was protecting them
- At Bethel, Yaqub (AS) built the altar as commanded
- Allah reaffirmed the covenant with him there

### Allah's Reaffirmation of the Name "Israel"
- At Bethel, Allah appeared to Yaqub (AS) again
- He confirmed: "Your name is Yaqub. But you shall no longer be called Yaqub — your name shall be Israel."
- He repeated the promises:
  - "I am Allah Almighty. Be fruitful and multiply."
  - "A nation and a company of nations shall descend from you."
  - "Kings shall descend from you."
  - "The land I gave to Ibrahim and Ishaq — I give it to you and your descendants after you."

### The Death of Rachel (AS)
- After leaving Bethel, while traveling, Rachel (AS) went into labor
- It was a difficult childbirth
- She gave birth to a son — Benjamin (Binyamin)
- But she herself died during the delivery
- As she was dying, she named the child "Ben-oni" (son of my sorrow)
- Yaqub (AS) renamed him "Benjamin" (son of the right hand — a name of honor)
- Rachel (AS) was buried on the way to Ephrath (Bethlehem)
- Yaqub (AS) set up a pillar over her grave
- The Tomb of Rachel remains a significant site near Bethlehem to this day

### The Return to Ishaq (AS)
- Yaqub (AS) finally returned to his elderly father Ishaq (AS) at Mamre (Hebron)
- Ibrahim (AS) had long since passed away, but both Ishaq (AS) and Ismail (AS) had buried him
- Now, in their old age, Ishaq (AS) and Ismail (AS) were together again with Yaqub (AS)

### The Death of Ishaq (AS)
- After many years, Prophet Ishaq (AS) passed away
- He was 180 years old (or 160 according to some sources)
- He had lived to see his sons grow, his grandchildren multiply, and his family prosper
- At the time of his death, both his sons — Esau and Yaqub (AS) — were present
- They buried him in the Cave of Machpelah in Hebron
- This was the same cave that Ibrahim (AS) had purchased as a family burial ground
- There, Ishaq (AS) was laid to rest beside his father Ibrahim (AS) and his mother Sarah (AS)

  - Surah Al-Ankabut
  - Surah As-Saffat
  - Surah Sad

### The Lineage of Prophethood
- The line of Prophethood continued through Ishaq (AS) and Yaqub (AS)
- All the Prophets of Bani Israel (Children of Israel) descended from this line:
  - Yusuf (AS) — the son of Yaqub (AS)
  - Musa (AS) and Harun (AS) — descendants of Levi, son of Yaqub (AS)

- This is the fulfillment of Allah's promise to Ibrahim (AS) that Prophethood would be in his descendants

### The Twelve Tribes of Israel
- The twelve sons of Yaqub (AS) became the twelve tribes of Israel
- Each tribe developed into a large clan with its own identity
- They would later migrate to Egypt during the time of Yusuf (AS)
- After centuries in Egypt, they would return to Canaan under the leadership of Musa (AS) and Yusha (AS)

### The Significance of the Name "Israel"
- The name "Israel" (Isra'il) carries deep meaning:
  - It means "Striver with God" — one who struggles in the path of Allah
  - It signifies the spiritual wrestling that Yaqub (AS) underwent
  - It represents the journey of the soul toward Allah — a journey of effort, struggle, and ultimate submission
- The term "Bani Israel" (Children of Israel) refers to the descendants of Yaqub (AS)
- The Quran frequently addresses them, reminding them of the blessings bestowed upon their ancestors

### The Location of Ishaq's (AS) and Yaqub's (AS) Burials
- Ishaq (AS) was buried in the Cave of Machpelah in Hebron (Al-Khalil), Palestine
- He lies beside his father Ibrahim (AS) and his mother Sarah (AS)
- Yaqub (AS) was also buried in the same cave
- This site — the Cave of the Patriarchs — is one of the most sacred locations for Jews, Christians, and Muslims
- It is in the city of Hebron, which is named "Al-Khalil" in Arabic, after Ibrahim Khalilullah (AS)
