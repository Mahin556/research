```

    Billi so rahi thi.

    Paani thanda hai.

    Yeh teesri baar hai jab tumne pucha hai.

    Yahi ek raasta hai bahar jaane ka.

31. **That is the only way to do it.**
   
    Yahi ek tarika hai ise karne ka.

32. **It is the only way to do it.**
   
    Yahi ek tarika hai ise karne ka.

    Ghar ki chhat tapat rahi hai.

    Jitna zyada sote ho utna accha feel karte ho.
```

### Be(base form)

* Used with: *to, will, can, should, must, always, may, need*
* Example:
  * I want to **be** a developer
  * You should **be** careful

```
# Will + be (future)

1. I will be there.
   Main wahan hounga.

2. I will be ready soon.
   Main jald taiyaar hounga.

3. I will be there soon.
   Main jald wahan hounga.

4. She will be happy.
   Woh khush hogi.

5. She will be late.
   Woh late hogi.

6. They will be ready.
   Woh taiyaar honge.

7. They will be ok.
   Woh theek honge.

8. They will be successful.
   Woh safal honge.

9. There will be a meeting.
   Ek meeting hogi.

10. There will be problems.
    Problems aayengi.

---

# Should / Must + be

1. You should be careful.
   Tumhe savdhaan rehna chahiye.

2. You must be careful.
   Tumhe zaroor savdhaan rehna chahiye.

3. He must be tired.
   Woh zaroor thaka hua hoga.

---

# Can / May + be

1. We can be friends.
   Hum dost ban sakte hain.

2. Life can be beautiful.
   Zindagi khubsurat ho sakti hai.

3. Life can be so unpredictable.
   Zindagi itni anjaani ho sakti hai.

4. May be.
   Shayad. (possibility)

5. Can be.
   Ho sakta hai. (possibility)

6. Can't be.
   Ho hi nahi sakta. (impossibility)

---

# To + be (infinitive)

1. Try to be honest.
   Imaandaar rehne ki koshish karo.

2. To be honest.
   Sachchi baat yeh hai. (phrase)

3. I want to be a doctor.
   Main doctor banna chahta hoon.

4. I want to be a SRE.
   Main SRE banna chahta hoon.

5. You need to be careful.
   Tumhe savdhaan rehna hoga.

6. You need to be correct.
   Tumhe sahi rehna hoga.

7. You need to be sure.
   Tumhe pakka hona hoga.

8. You need to be super honest with me.
   Tumhe mere saath bilkul seedha rehna hoga.

9. She hopes to be successful.
   Woh safal hone ki umeed rakhti hai.

10. They plan to be leaders.
    Woh leader banne ka plan rakhte hain.

11. I want to be a developer.
    Main developer banna chahta hoon.

---

# Always + be (advice / habit)

1. Always be kind.
   Hamesha dयालु raho.

2. Always be honest.
   Hamesha imaandaar raho.

3. Always be yourself.
   Hamesha apne aap raho.

```

### BEING (present participle)

Used in: *continuous / passive*
```
He is **being** rude
The work is **being** done


# Continuous (describing temporary behavior)
# Someone is acting in a certain way right now*

He is being polite.
She is being rude.
They are being noisy.
You are being silly.
He is being very kind today.
She is being difficult.
They are being helpful.
He is being honest with me.


# Passive Continuous (action happening right now)
# Something is in the process of being done*

The food is being prepared.
The car is being washed.
The road is being fixed.
The house is being built.
The letter is being written.
The project is being completed.
The child is being treated at the hospital.
The problem is being solved.


# After Verbs (being as object)
# Like, enjoy, hate, mind, avoid + being*

I enjoy being alone.
He likes being helpful.
She hates being ignored.
I don't mind being corrected.
He avoids being late.
She loves being the center of attention.
They enjoy being together.
I hate being misunderstood.


# After Prepositions (being as noun)
# Instead of, despite, for, about + being*

Despite being tired, she worked hard.
Instead of being angry, he stayed calm.
He was punished for being late.
She was praised for being honest.
He apologized for being rude.
Despite being poor, he was happy.
She was nervous about being alone.
He is known for being kind.


# Being = a Living Creature (noun use)

He is a human being.
She is a beautiful being.
All living beings need water.
He treated every being with respect.
```
