### Ishraq Salah (Salat al-Ishraq)
**Time:** About 15–20 minutes after sunrise  
**Rak‘ahs:** 2 or 4
**Benefit:** Reward similar to one Hajj and one Umrah (as mentioned in hadith when prayed after Fajr and remembrance).

---
### Salat al-Tawbah (Prayer of Repentance)
**Time:** When one sincerely repents from a sin  
**Rak‘ahs:** 2
**Benefit:** Means of seeking forgiveness from Allah.

---
### Salat al-Istikharah
**Time:** Any permissible time (not during forbidden times)  
**Rak‘ahs:** 2
**Purpose:** To seek Allah’s guidance when making an important decision.  
After prayer → Recite the **Istikharah dua**.

---
### Salat al-Hajah (Prayer of Need)
**Time:** When facing difficulty or need  
**Rak‘ahs:** 2
**Purpose:** To ask Allah for help in hardship.

---
### Tahajjud Salah
**Time:** After Isha, preferably in the last third of the night  
**Rak‘ahs:** Minimum 2, usually up to 8 or 12
**Benefit:** One of the most powerful voluntary prayers.  
Best time for dua acceptance.

---
### Salat al-Duha (Chasht)
**Time:** Mid-morning (after sunrise until before Dhuhr)  
Best time: Around late morning
**Rak‘ahs:** 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, or 12
**Benefit:** Charity on behalf of all body joints.
_(Note: Ishraq is considered part of Duha time by many scholars.)_

---
### Tahiyyat al-Wudu
**Time:** Immediately after performing Wudu  
**Rak‘ahs:** 2
**Benefit:** Great reward (Hadith mentions promise of Paradise for consistent practice).

---
### Tahiyyat al-Masjid
**Time:** Upon entering the mosque, before sitting  
**Rak‘ahs:** 2
**Purpose:** To honor the mosque.

---
### Times When Nafl Is Not Allowed
- During sunrise
- At exact noon (Zawal)
- During sunset
- After Fajr until sunrise
- After Asr until Maghrib
