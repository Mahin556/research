### English Translation
**In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.**
1.  Say, "He is Allah, [who is] One.
2.  Allah, the Eternal, the Self-Sufficient Master (upon whom all of creation depends).
3.  He neither begets nor is He born.
4.  Nor is there to Him any equivalent."

### Hinglish (Roman Urdu) Translation

3.  Na usne kisi ko janam diya aur na woh kisi se paida hua.
4.  Aur na koi uske baraabar ya hamsar hai."

### Tafseer (Commentary / Haashiyah)

**Background of Revelation (Shaan-e-Nuzul):**
This Surah was revealed in response to the polytheists (Kuffaar-someone who believes in many gods) of Makkah. They asked the Prophet Muhammad (صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ وَسَلَّمَ) questions about the nature of his Lord. Their questions were rooted in materialistic concepts, such as:
- "What is your God made of? (Gold, silver, etc.)?"
- "What does He eat and drink?"
- "From whom did He inherit His divinity?"
- "Who will be His heir?"

This Surah was revealed to provide a definitive and pure answer about the essence of Allah, completely refuting any anthropomorphic or materialistic concepts of God.

**Explanation of the Verses:**

1.  **Verse 1: "Qul Hu-wallaahu 'Ahad"**
    - The command "Say" (Qul) is directed to the Prophet, emphasizing that the description of God is not based on human speculation but on divine revelation.
    - "Huwa" (He) refers to the Being whose reality is beyond human comprehension, but whose existence is undeniable.
    - **"Ahad"** signifies absolute Oneness. Unlike "Waahid" (which also means one but can imply a second or third), "Ahad" implies a uniqueness that excludes the very concept of any partner, equal, or similarity. He is One in His essence, attributes, and actions.

2.  **Verse 2: "'Allaahu-s-Samad"**
    - **"Samad"** is a comprehensive attribute with several deep meanings:
        - The Master who is perfect in His sovereignty, nobility, and glory.
        - The Eternal and Independent; the One to whom all creatures turn for their needs, while He is free of all needs.
        - The One who neither eats nor drinks, as He is free from any physical requirements.
        - The One who does not beget nor is born, because He is the First and the Last. The "Haashiyah" explicitly states this was in response to the pagans' questions about Allah's physical needs and lineage.

3.  **Verse 3: "Lam yalid, wa lam yuu lad"**
    - **"Lam yalid" (He neither begets):** This refutes the beliefs of those who attribute offspring to God, such as the Christians (who claim Jesus as the son of God) and the pagan Arabs (who believed angels were daughters of God). Begetting implies a physical process, need for a partner, and division of the divine essence, which is impossible for the One, the Samad.
    - **"Wa lam yuu lad" (nor is He born):** This refutes those who believe God came into existence at a point in time. Everything that is born has a beginning and an end, and is dependent on a parent. Allah is Eternal and Pre-existent, without origin.

4.  **Verse 4: "Walam yakul-la-Huu kufuwan 'ahad"**
    - This is a comprehensive negation of any form of partnership or similarity. It means: There is none equal, comparable, or equivalent to Him in any way—neither in His essence, nor in His attributes, nor in His actions, nor in His rights to be worshipped.
    - The word **"Kufuwan"** denotes absolute equality and likeness. This final verse closes every possible loophole through which polytheism (Shirk) might enter, affirming the absolute uniqueness of Allah.
