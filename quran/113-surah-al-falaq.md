### Metadata
- **Surah Name:** Al-Falaq (الفلق)
- **Surah Number:** 113
- **Meaning of Name:** The Daybreak, The Dawn.
- **Classification:** Makki (Revealed in Mecca)
- **Number of Verses (Ayaat):** 5
- **Number of Sections (Rukoos):** 1
- **Juz (Para):** 30
- **Title/Theme:** Seeking refuge in Allah from various forms of evil.

### English Translation
**In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.**
1.  Say, "I seek refuge in the Lord of daybreak
2.  From the evil of that which He created
3.  And from the evil of darkness when it settles
4.  And from the evil of the blowers in knots
5.  And from the evil of an envier when he envies."

### Hinglish (Roman Urdu) Translation
**Allah ke naam se shuru jo bada meharban aur nehayat rahem waala hai.**
1.  (Aye Nabi!) Kaho, "Main subah (Falaq) ke Rubb ki panaah maangta hoon.
2.  Har us cheez ke shar (buraayi) se jo usne paida ki.
3.  Aur andheri raat ke shar se jab wo chaa jaaye.
4.  Aur girhon (mein phoonkne waaliyon) ke shar se.
5.  Aur hasad karne waale ke shar se, jab wo hasad kare."

### Tafseer (Commentary / Haashiyah)

**Background of Revelation (Shaan-e-Nuzul):**
The "Haashiyah" (footnote) provides the specific historical context for the revelation of Surah Al-Falaq (along with Surah An-Naas):
- A Jewish man named **Labeed bin A'sam** and his daughters performed black magic (sihr) on the Prophet Muhammad (صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ وَسَلَّمَ).
- The physical body of the Prophet (صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ وَسَلَّمَ) was affected, but importantly, **his heart, intellect, and faith remained completely untouched**.
- The angel Jibreel (عليه السلام) informed the Prophet of the location where the magical items were hidden.
- The Prophet sent Hazrat Ali (صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ) to retrieve the items.
- Allah revealed **Surah Al-Falaq** and **Surah An-Naas**. Through the blessing (barakah) of these Surahs, the effect of the magic was nullified, and the Prophet (صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ وَسَلَّمَ) was fully cured.

**Explanation of the Verses:**

1.  **Verse 1: "Qul a'uzu bi-Rabbil-Falaq"**
    - The command "Say" (Qul) instructs the believer to actively seek protection.
    - **"Rabbil-Falaq"** (Lord of the Daybreak): "Falaq" means to split or cleave. It refers to the One who splits the dawn from the darkness of night. By seeking refuge in the Lord of this phenomenon, we acknowledge His absolute power to bring light out of darkness and to dispel all forms of harm.

2.  **Verse 2: "Min sharri ma khalaq"**
    - This is a general, comprehensive refuge from the evil embedded within all of creation. It implies that while creation itself is not inherently evil, Allah has informed us that there is harm and evil that can emanate from it, and we seek His protection from that.

3.  **Verse 3: "Wa min sharri ghasiqin iza waqab"**
    - **"Ghasiq"** refers to the darkness of the night. **"Iza waqab"** means when it enters or settles, becoming intensely dark.
    - The night is mentioned specifically because it is a time when harmful creatures emerge, evil plans are often concocted in secret, and a person's heart can be filled with fear and loneliness. Seeking refuge from the "evil of the night" is seeking protection from the spiritual and physical harms that thrive in darkness.

4.  **Verse 4: "Wa min sharrin-naffaasati fil-'uqad"**
    - **"An-Naffaasat"** refers to those (specifically women, as per the classical commentary and the Urdu/Hinglish notes) who blow into knots. This is a reference to a specific form of witchcraft.
    - In the practice of magic, women would tie knots in a string or thread and blow into them while reciting incantations (spells) to harm someone. This verse specifically seeks Allah's protection from the evil of such magical practices, which was the direct cause of this Surah's revelation regarding the Prophet's own experience.

5.  **Verse 5: "Wa min sharri haasidin iza hasad"**
    - **"Haasid"** is an envier. Envy (hasad) is a destructive spiritual disease where a person wishes for the blessings (ne'mat) of another to disappear.
    - The "Haashiyah" explains that this can refer generally to any envier, but in the specific context of the revelation, it refers to the envious Jews (Yahood), and more specifically to **Labeed bin A'sam** himself, whose envy led him to practice magic against the Prophet.
    - The phrase **"iza hasad"** (when he envies) indicates that the true harm of an envier manifests when he acts upon his envy. We seek Allah's protection not only from the external harm of the envier but also from the spiritual harm that envy can cause in society.

**Summary (Unwaan):**
The title given in the text perfectly summarizes the Surah's purpose:
- **"Mukhtalif cheezon kay shar say Panaah-e-Ilahi talab karnay ki ta'leem"** : Teaching us to seek Divine refuge from the evil of various things.
- **"Jaadoo kay shar say panaah"** : Refuge from the evil of magic.
- **"Haasid kay shar say panaah"** : Refuge from the evil of the envier.
