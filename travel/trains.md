## Online Booking (Most Common Method)
👉 Done through IRCTC Rail Connect or website of Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation

### 📱 Steps:
* Create account on IRCTC (username, password, mobile, email)
* Login to app/website
* Enter:
  * From station
  * To station
  * Date of journey
* Click **Search Trains**
* Choose train (based on time, price, class)
* Select class:
  * SL (Sleeper)
  * 3AC / 2AC / 1AC
  * CC / EC (Chair Car)
* Enter passenger details (name, age, ID)
* Choose seat preference (optional)
* Make payment:
  * UPI / Debit card / Net banking / Wallet
* Ticket gets booked → you receive **PNR number**

## Waiting List (WL)
* You **did not get a confirmed seat** at the time of booking
* Your ticket is in a **queue (line)**
* If someone cancels their ticket, your status can **move up and become confirmed**

### Example
* You booked a ticket → Status shows **WL 25**
* This means:
  * 24 people are ahead of you
  * You are **25th in line**
* If 25 people cancel → your ticket becomes **Confirmed (CNF)**

### WL Status Flow

| Stage             | Meaning                       |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------- |
| WL (Waiting List) | No seat yet                   |
| RAC               | Partial seat (you can travel) |
| CNF               | Full seat confirmed           |

### Types of Waiting List

| Type | Meaning                            |
| ---- | ---------------------------------- |
| GNWL | General Waiting List (most common) |
| RLWL | Remote Location Waiting List       |
| PQWL | Pooled Quota Waiting List          |
| TQWL | Tatkal Waiting List                |

## Important Rules

* ❌ **WL ticket is not allowed in reserved coach** (if still WL after chart preparation)
* 💰 **Automatic refund** if booked online via IRCTC
* ✅ **RAC allowed to travel**, WL not allowed
* 📅 Final status is decided when **chart is prepared (4 hours before departure)**

## RAC (Reservation Against Cancellation)
* You are **allowed to travel**, but
* You **don’t get a full seat/berth initially**
* You get a **shared seat (usually side lower berth)**

### Simple Example
* You book a ticket → Status = **RAC 10**
* Meaning:
  * You are 10th in RAC queue
  * You **can board the train**
  * You will share a seat with another RAC passenger

### RAC vs WL vs CNF

| Status            | Travel Allowed | Seat Type       |
| ----------------- | -------------- | --------------- |
| WL (Waiting List) | ❌ No           | No seat         |
| RAC               | ✅ Yes          | Shared seat     |
| CNF (Confirmed)   | ✅ Yes          | Full seat/berth |


### How RAC Seat Works
* 1 berth is divided into **2 seats**
* Two RAC passengers share:
  * Sitting during day
  * Sleeping may be adjusted

### RAC to Confirm
* If people cancel tickets:
  * RAC → becomes **Confirmed (CNF)**
* Priority:
  * First WL → RAC
  * Then RAC → CNF

### Important Points
* RAC passengers **can travel legally**
* You will get at least a **seat (not full bed)**
* After chart preparation, status may still upgrade

## Tatkal
* “Tatkal” = **Immediate / urgent**
* Used when you need a ticket **1 day before travel**
* Costs **extra charges** but gives a **higher chance of getting a seat**

### Tatkal Booking Time

| Class Type                         | Booking Time                    |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| AC Classes (1AC, 2AC, 3AC, CC, EC) | 10:00 AM (1 day before journey) |
| Non-AC (Sleeper - SL)              | 11:00 AM (1 day before journey) |

👉 Booking is done via IRCTC Rail Connect

### Tatkal Charges (Approx)

| Class        | Extra Charge |
| ------------ | ------------ |
| Sleeper (SL) | ₹100 – ₹200  |
| 3AC          | ₹300 – ₹400  |
| 2AC          | ₹400 – ₹500  |
| CC/EC        | ₹125 – ₹400  |

### Tatkal vs Normal Ticket
| Feature      | Normal                | Tatkal                 |
| ------------ | --------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Booking Time | Up to 120 days before | 1 day before           |
| Price        | Normal fare           | Higher (extra charges) |
| Availability | More                  | Limited seats          |
| Refund       | Allowed (rules apply) | Very limited refund    |

### Important Rules
* Limited seats → **gets full very fast (within minutes)**
* No full refund on confirmed Tatkal tickets
* ID proof is **mandatory** during travel
* You can still get **WL or RAC in Tatkal**

## Before Booking (Most Important)

* Login **5–10 minutes before** (don’t wait till 10:00 AM)
* Keep:
  * Passenger names saved (Master List in IRCTC)
  * ID proof ready
  * Payment method ready (UPI best)
* Use:
  * **Fast internet (WiFi/4G/5G)**
  * **Laptop preferred** (faster than mobile)

### Step-by-Step (30 Seconds Strategy)
* 09:58 AM → Open train search page
* Enter:
  * From / To
  * Date
* Keep ready but **don’t search yet**
👉 At exact **10:00 AM (AC)** / **11:00 AM (SL)**:
* Click **Search → Book Now immediately**
* Select class quickly
* Choose **“Book Only If Confirmed”** (optional but smart)
* Auto-fill passengers (use saved list)
* Skip extras (no food, no insurance to save time)
* Select **UPI / Wallet payment**

### Payment Tricks (Game Changer)
* Use:
  * UPI (fastest)
  * IRCTC Wallet (instant)
* Avoid:
  * Net banking (slow sometimes)
* Keep UPI app already open

### Advanced Tricks (Very Powerful)
* Open **2–3 tabs/devices**
* Try **different classes** (3AC + Sleeper)
* Choose **less popular trains**
* Avoid typing → use **saved passenger list**
* Refresh page at **09:59:55** (perfect timing)

### Common Mistakes

* Logging in at 10:00 → ❌ too late
* Typing passenger details → ❌ waste time
* Slow payment → ❌ ticket gone
* Choosing only one train → ❌ risky
